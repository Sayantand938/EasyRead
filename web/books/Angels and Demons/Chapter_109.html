Angels and Demons

Chapter 109

Meanwhile, in St. Peter’s Square, the Swiss Guards shouted orders and spread out, trying to push the crowds back to a safer distance. It was no use. The crowd was too large and seemed more interested in the Vatican's coming destruction than in their own safety. The large media screens in the square were now showing a live countdown of the antimatter canister-a direct feed from the Swiss Guard security monitor-courtesy of the camerlegno. Unfortunately, the image of the canister counting down was not scaring the crowds away. The people in the square apparently looked at the tiny drop of liquid in the canister and decided it was not as dangerous as they had thought. They could also see the countdown clock now-a little under forty-five minutes until detonation. Plenty of time to stay and watch.

Nonetheless, the Swiss Guards all agreed that the camerlegno's bold decision to tell the world the truth and then give the media actual visuals of Illuminati betrayal had been a smart move. The Illuminati had probably expected the Vatican to be their usual secretive selves when facing trouble. Not tonight. Camerlegno Carlo Ventresca had proven himself a strong enemy.

Inside the Sistine Chapel, Cardinal Mortati was getting restless. It was past 11:15 P.M. Many of the cardinals were still praying, but others had gathered near the exit, clearly worried about the time. Some of the cardinals began hitting the door with their fists.

Outside the door Lieutenant Chartrand heard the hitting and didn't know what to do. He checked his watch. It was time. Captain Rocher had given strict orders that the cardinals were not to be let out until he said so. The hitting on the door became more intense, and Chartrand felt uneasy. He wondered if the captain had simply forgotten. The captain had been acting very strangely since his mysterious phone call.

Chartrand pulled out his walkie-talkie. "Captain? Chartrand here. It is past time. Should I open the Sistine?"

"That door stays shut. I think I already gave you that order."

"Yes, sir, I just-"

"Our guest is arriving soon. Take a few men upstairs, and guard the door of the Pope's office. The camerlegno is not to go anywhere."

"I'm sorry, sir?"

“What is it you don’t understand, Lieutenant?”

“Nothing, sir. I’m on my way.”

Upstairs in the Pope’s Office, the camerlegno quietly stared at the fire, lost in thought. God, give me strength. Send us a miracle. He poked at the coals, wondering if he would live through the night.
