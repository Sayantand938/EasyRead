Angels and Demons

Chapter 5

On a busy European street, the killer weaved through a crowd. He was a powerful man. Dark and strong. Surprisingly quick. His muscles still felt tight from the excitement of his meeting.

It went well, he told himself. Although his employer had never shown his face, the killer felt honored to be near him. Had it really only been fifteen days since his employer had first called? The killer still remembered every word of that call…

“My name is Janus,” the caller had said. “We are alike in some ways. We share an enemy. I hear you can be hired.”

“It depends on who you work for,” the killer replied.

The caller told him.

“Is this a joke?”

“You have heard our name, I see,” the caller replied.

“Of course. The brotherhood is famous.”

“And yet you doubt I am who I say I am.”

“Everyone knows the brothers are gone.”

“A clever trick. The most dangerous enemy is the one no one worries about.”

The killer doubted it. “The brotherhood still exists?”

“Hidden deeper than ever. Our influence is everywhere… even in the holy fortress of our greatest foe.”

“Impossible. They can’t be hurt.”

“We can reach far.”

“No one can reach that far.”

“Soon, you will believe. We’ve already proven the brotherhood’s power beyond doubt. A single act of betrayal is all the proof you need.”

“What did you do?”

The caller told him.

The killer’s eyes went wide. “An impossible task.”

The next day, newspapers around the globe carried the same headline. The killer became a believer.

Now, fifteen days later, the killer’s faith was stronger than ever. The brotherhood endures, he thought. Tonight they will appear and show their power.

As he walked through the streets, his black eyes gleamed with anticipation. One of the most secret and feared groups ever had asked him for help. They have chosen well, he thought. He was known for his secrecy and his deadly skills.

So far, he had served them well. He had killed his target and given the item to Janus as asked. Now, it was up to Janus to use his power to put the item where it needed to be.

The placement…

The killer wondered how Janus could handle such a huge job. The man clearly had connections. The brotherhood’s power seemed endless.

Janus, the killer thought. A code name, of course. Was it a reference to the Roman god with two faces… or to the moon of Saturn? It didn't matter. Janus had incredible power. He had proven that.

As the killer walked, he imagined his ancestors smiling down on him. Today he was fighting their battle, the same enemy they had fought for centuries, since the eleventh century… when the enemy’s armies had first invaded his land, raping and killing his people, calling them unclean, destroying their temples and gods.

His ancestors had formed a small but deadly army to defend themselves. The army became famous as protectors-skilled killers who traveled the land killing any of the enemy they could find. They were known for their brutal killings and for celebrating their victories by getting high on drugs. Their drug of choice was a strong substance called hashish.

As their fame grew, these deadly men became known by one word-Hassassin -meaning “hashish users.” The name Hassassin became a symbol of death in almost every language. The word is still used today, even in modern English… but like the art of killing, the word had changed.

It is now pronounced assassin.
