Angels and Demons

Chapter 76

Chinita Macri was running. She had the story of her life.

Her video camera felt like an anchor as she trudged across St. Peter’s Square, pushing through the growing crowd. Everyone seemed to be moving the other way… toward the commotion. Macri was trying to get away as fast as she could. The man in the tweed jacket had seen her, and now she felt others were after her, men she couldn't see, closing in from all sides.

Macri was still shocked by the images she had just recorded. She wondered if the dead man was really who she thought he was. Glick’s mysterious phone contact suddenly seemed a little less crazy.

As she hurried toward the BBC van, a young man with a military look stepped out of the crowd in front of her. Their eyes met, and they both stopped. Quickly, he raised a walkie-talkie and spoke into it. Then he moved toward her. Macri turned and ran back into the crowd, her heart pounding.

As she stumbled through the mass of people, she took the used video cassette out of her camera. Cellulose gold , she thought, tucking the tape under her belt against her back and letting her coat cover it. For once she was glad she was a little overweight. Glick, where are you!

Another soldier appeared to her left, getting closer. Macri knew she didn't have much time. She turned into the crowd again. Pulling a blank tape from her bag, she put it into the camera. Then she prayed.

She was thirty yards from the BBC van when the two men appeared right in front of her, arms folded. She was trapped.

“Film,” one snapped. “Now.”

Macri pulled back, wrapping her arms around her camera. “No way.”

One of the men pulled aside his jacket, showing a gun.

“So shoot me,” Macri said, surprised by how brave she sounded.

“Film,” the first one repeated.

Where the devil is Glick? Macri stomped her foot and yelled as loud as she could, “I am a professional videographer with the BBC! By Article 12 of the Free Press Act, this film belongs to the British Broadcast Corporation!”

The men didn't even move. The one with the gun took a step toward her. “I am a lieutenant with the Swiss Guard, and by the Holy Doctrine governing this property, you are subject to search and seizure.”

A crowd had started to gather around them.

Macri yelled, “I will not give you the film in this camera without speaking to my editor in London. I suggest you-”

The guards stopped her. One yanked the camera out of her hands. The other grabbed her arm and twisted her toward the Vatican. “Grazie,” he said, leading her through the pushing crowd.

Macri prayed they wouldn't search her and find the tape. If she could somehow protect the film long enough to-

Suddenly, the unthinkable happened. Someone in the crowd was reaching under her coat. Macri felt the video yanked away. She turned, but stopped herself from speaking. Behind her, a breathless Gunther Glick winked at her and disappeared back into the crowd.
