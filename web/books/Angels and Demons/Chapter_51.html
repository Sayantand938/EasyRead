Angels and Demons

Chapter 51

BBC reporter Gunther Glick stared at the cell phone in his hand for ten seconds before he finally hung up.

Chinita Macri watched him from the back of the van. “What happened? Who was that?”

Glick turned, feeling like a child who had just gotten a Christmas gift he worried wasn’t really for him. “I just got a tip. Something’s going on inside the Vatican.”

“It’s called conclave,” Chinita said. “Great tip.”

“No, something else.” Something big. He wondered if the story the caller had just told him could possibly be true. Glick felt ashamed when he realized he was hoping it was. “What if I told you four cardinals have been kidnapped and are going to be murdered at different churches tonight?”

“I’d say someone at the office with a twisted sense of humor is messing with you.”

“What if I told you we were going to be given the exact location of the first murder?”

“I’d want to know who you just talked to.”

“He didn’t say.”

“Maybe because he’s full of it?”

Glick was used to Macri’s cynicism, but she was forgetting that Glick had dealt with liars and crazy people for almost ten years at the British Tattler. This caller was neither. This man was coldly sane. Logical. I will call you just before eight, the man had said, and tell you where the first killing will occur. The images you record will make you famous. When Glick asked why the caller was giving him this information, the answer was as icy as the man’s Mideastern accent. The media is the right arm of anarchy.

“He told me something else, too,” Glick said.

“What? That Elvis Presley was just elected Pope?”

“Access the BBC database, okay?” Glick’s adrenaline was pumping now. “I want to see what other stories we’ve done on these guys.”

“What guys?”

“Just do it.”

Macri sighed and pulled up the connection to the BBC database. “This will take a minute.”

Glick’s mind was racing. “The caller really wanted to know if I had a cameraman.”

“Videographer.”

“And if we could broadcast live.”

“One point five three seven megahertz. What's this about?” The database beeped. “Okay, we're in. Who are you looking for?”

Glick gave her the keyword.

Macri turned and stared. “I really hope you’re kidding.”
