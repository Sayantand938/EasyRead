Angels and Demons

Chapter 80

When Langdon had left the Vatican Secret Archives only two hours ago, he never thought he would see them again. Now, breathless from jogging all the way back with his Swiss Guard escort, Langdon was back at the archives.

His escort, the guard with the scar, led Langdon through the rows of clear cubicles. The silence of the archives felt even more ominous now, and Langdon was glad when the guard spoke.

“Over here, I think,” he said, leading Langdon to the back of the room where smaller vaults lined the wall. The guard looked at the titles on the vaults and pointed to one. “Yes, here it is. Right where the commander said it would be.”

Langdon read the title. ATTIVI VATICANI. Vatican assets? He looked at the list of contents. Real estate… currency… Vatican Bank… antiquities… The list went on.

“Records of all Vatican assets,” the guard said.

Langdon looked at the cubicle. Jesus. Even in the dark, he could see it was full.

“My commander said anything Bernini made while working for the Vatican would be listed here as an asset.”

Langdon nodded, thinking the commander’s hunch might work. In Bernini’s time, anything an artist made while working for the Pope legally became the Vatican's property. It was more like feudalism than support, but top artists lived well and rarely complained. “Including works in churches outside Vatican City?”

The soldier looked at him strangely. “Of course. All Catholic churches in Rome are Vatican property.”

Langdon looked at the list in his hand. It had the names of about twenty churches on a direct line with West Ponente’s breath. The third altar of science was one of them, and Langdon hoped he had time to find out which one. If things were different, he would have loved to visit each church himself. But today, he had about twenty minutes to find what he needed—the church with a Bernini tribute to fire.

Langdon walked to the vault’s electronic revolving door. The guard didn’t follow. Langdon felt him hesitate. He smiled. “The air’s fine. Thin, but you can breathe.”

“My orders are to take you here and then go straight back to the security center.”

“You’re leaving?”

“Yes. The Swiss Guard can’t go inside the archives. I’m already breaking the rules by bringing you this far. The commander reminded me of that.”

“Breaking the rules?” Do you even know what’s happening tonight? “Whose side is your commander on!”

The guard’s friendly expression vanished. The scar under his eye jumped. He stared, suddenly looking a lot like Olivetti.

“I’m sorry,” Langdon said, wishing he hadn't said that. “It’s just… I could use some help.”

The guard didn’t blink. “I’m trained to follow orders. Not question them. When you find what you need, call the commander right away.”

Langdon was annoyed. “But where will he be?”

The guard took off his walkie-talkie and put it on a table close by. “Channel one.” Then he vanished into the dark.
