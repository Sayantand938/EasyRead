Money. Calls about money.

Some people already wanted antimatter technology.

Inside the Vatican, Gunther Glick was thrilled as he followed the camerlegno from the Sistine Chapel. Glick and Macri had just made the live broadcast of the decade. And what a broadcast it had been. The camerlegno had been captivating.

Now out in the hallway, the camerlegno turned to Glick and Macri. “I have asked the Swiss Guard to gather photos for you—photos of the branded cardinals and one of His late Holiness. I must warn you, these are not pleasant pictures. Horrible burns. Blackened tongues. But I would like you to broadcast them to the world.”

Glick thought it must always be Christmas inside Vatican City. He wants me to show everyone a photo only we have of the dead Pope? “Are you sure?” Glick asked, trying not to sound too thrilled.

The camerlegno nodded. “The Swiss Guard will also let you show live video of the antimatter as it counts down.”

Glick stared. Christmas. Christmas. Christmas!

“The Illuminati are about to learn,” the camerlegno said, “that they went too far.”
