Angels and Demons

Chapter 30

Maximilian Kohler. Please call your office right away.“

Bright sunlight filled Langdon’s eyes as the elevator doors opened into the main hall. Before the echo of the announcement faded, every electronic device on Kohler’s wheelchair began beeping and buzzing. His pager. His phone. His E-mail. Kohler looked down at the blinking lights, seeming confused. The director was back, and he was in range.

“Director Kohler. Please call your office.”

The sound of his name seemed to surprise Kohler.

He looked up, appearing angry and then quickly worried. Langdon’s eyes met his, and Vittoria’s too. The three of them stood still for a moment, as if all the tension between them had disappeared, replaced by a shared sense of dread.

Kohler took his cell phone from the armrest. He dialed a number and struggled to stop another coughing fit. Vittoria and Langdon waited.

“This is… Director Kohler,” he said, breathing heavily. “Yes? I was underground, out of range.” He listened, his gray eyes growing wider. “Who? Yes, connect me.” There was a pause. “Hello? This is Maximilian Kohler. I am the director of CERN. Who is this?”

Vittoria and Langdon watched silently as Kohler listened.

“It would be unwise,” Kohler finally said, “to talk about this on the phone. I will be there right away.” He was coughing again. “Meet me… at Leonardo da Vinci Airport. Forty minutes.” Kohler seemed to be running out of breath. He coughed deeply and barely managed to say, “Find the canister immediately… I am coming.” Then he hung up.

Vittoria ran to Kohler’s side, but Kohler could no longer speak. Langdon watched as Vittoria pulled out her cell phone and called CERN’s infirmary. Langdon felt like a ship on the edge of a storm… tossed but untouched.

Meet me at Leonardo da Vinci Airport. Kohler’s words echoed.

The vague shadows that had clouded Langdon’s mind all morning, in a single instant, turned into a clear picture. As he stood there in the chaos, he felt a door inside him open… as if some secret boundary had just been crossed. The ambigram. The murdered priest/scientist. The antimatter. And now… the target. Leonardo da Vinci Airport could only mean one thing. In a moment of sudden understanding, Langdon knew he had just changed. He had become a believer.

Five kilotons. Let there be light.

Two paramedics appeared, rushing across the atrium in white coats. They knelt by Kohler, putting an oxygen mask on his face. Scientists in the hall stopped and stepped back.

Kohler took two deep breaths, pushed the mask aside, and still gasping for air, looked up at Vittoria and Langdon. “Rome.”

“Rome?” Vittoria asked urgently. “The antimatter is in Rome? Who called?”

Kohler’s face was strained, his gray eyes tearing up. “The Swiss…” He struggled with the words, and the paramedics put the mask back over his face. As they prepared to take him away, Kohler reached up and grabbed Langdon’s arm.

Langdon nodded. He understood.

“Go…” Kohler breathed with difficulty beneath his mask. “Go… call me…” Then the paramedics were wheeling him away.

Vittoria stood frozen, watching him go. Then she turned to Langdon. “Rome? But… what was that about the Swiss? ”

Langdon put a hand on her shoulder, barely whispering the words. “The Swiss Guard,” he said. “The protectors of Vatican City.”
