Angels and Demons

Chapter 16

Hundreds of miles from CERN, a voice crackled through a walkie-talkie. “Okay, I’m in the hallway.”

The technician watching the video screens pressed the button on his transmitter. “You’re looking for camera #86. It should be at the far end.”

There was a long silence on the radio. The waiting technician started to sweat. Finally his radio clicked.

“The camera isn’t here,” the voice said. “I can see where it was attached, though. Someone must have taken it.”

The technician breathed out. “Thanks. Hold on a second, okay?”

Sighing, he looked back at the wall of video screens in front of him. Large parts of the complex were open to the public, and wireless cameras had gone missing before, usually taken by visitors as souvenirs. But as soon as a camera left the building and was out of range, the signal was lost, and the screen went blank. Confused, the technician looked up at the monitor. A clear picture was still coming from camera #86.

If the camera was stolen , he wondered, why are we still getting a signal? He knew there was only one answer. The camera was still inside, and someone had just moved it. But who? And why?

He stared at the monitor for a while. Finally he picked up his walkie-talkie. “Are there any closets in that stairwell? Any cupboards or dark corners?”

The voice sounded puzzled. “No. Why?”

The technician frowned. “Never mind. Thanks for your help.” He turned off his walkie-talkie and pursed his lips.

Given the small size of the video camera and that it was wireless, the technician knew that camera #86 could be sending signals from almost anywhere within the heavily guarded compound - a tightly packed group of thirty-two separate buildings spread over a half-mile area. The only hint was that the camera seemed to be in a dark place. But that wasn’t very helpful. The complex had countless dark spots - maintenance closets, heating vents, gardening sheds, bedroom closets, even a maze of underground tunnels. It could take weeks to find camera #86.

But that’s the least of my problems , he thought.

Even with the problem of the missing camera, there was something else that bothered him even more. The technician looked up at the image the lost camera was sending. It was a still object. A modern device unlike anything he had ever seen. He studied the blinking electronic screen at its base.

Even though the guard was well-trained to handle stressful situations, he felt his heart rate increasing. He told himself not to panic. There had to be a reason. The object seemed too small to be a big threat. But, the fact that it was inside the complex was worrying. Very worrying, indeed.

Today of all days , he thought.

Security was always a main concern for his employer, but today , more than any other day in the last twelve years, security was extremely important. The technician stared at the object for a long time and felt like something bad was about to happen.

Then, sweating, he called his boss.
