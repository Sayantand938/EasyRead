Angels and Demons

Chapter 124

Never had so many been so quiet.

In St. Peter's Square, faces turned away from the darkening sky, each person lost in their own thoughts. The media lights followed, pointing down to earth as if respecting the darkness. For a moment, it seemed the whole world was bowing its head together.

Cardinal Mortati knelt to pray, and the other cardinals joined him. The Swiss Guard lowered their swords, standing still. No one spoke or moved. Hearts everywhere were filled with sudden emotion: sadness, fear, wonder, belief, and a deep respect for the incredible power they had just seen.

Vittoria Vetra stood shaking at the bottom of the basilica's grand stairs. She closed her eyes. In the storm of emotions inside her, one word rang like a distant bell: pure, but also cruel. She pushed it away, but it echoed. Again, she tried to ignore it. The pain was too much. She tried to focus on what others were thinking: the amazing power of antimatter, the Vatican's rescue, the camerlegno, acts of bravery, miracles, selflessness. But the word still echoed, a lonely sting in the chaos.

Robert.

He had come for her at Castle St. Angelo.

He had saved her.

And now he was gone because of what she had created.

As Cardinal Mortati prayed, he wondered if he would also hear God's voice like the camerlegno. Do you need to believe in miracles to see them? Mortati was a modern man in an old faith. Miracles had never been part of his belief. His faith talked about miracles—bleeding hands, rising from the dead, images on cloths—but Mortati's logical mind had always seen them as myths. They were just stories people told because they needed proof. Miracles were just stories we held onto because we wanted them to be true.

And yet…

Am I so modern that I can't accept what I just saw? It was a miracle, wasn't it? Yes! God, with a few whispered words to the camerlegno, had stepped in and saved this church. Why was it so hard to believe? What would it say about God if He did nothing? That He didn't care? That He couldn't stop it? A miracle was the only answer!

As Mortati knelt in wonder, he prayed for the camerlegno’s soul. He thanked the young chamberlain who, even at his young age, had shown this old man the miracles of complete faith.

But Mortati had no idea how much his faith was about to be tested…

The silence of St. Peter’s Square broke with a soft wave at first. The wave grew to a murmur. And then, suddenly, to a roar. Without warning, the crowd cried out as one.

“Look! Look!”

Mortati opened his eyes and turned to the crowd. Everyone was pointing behind him, toward the front of St. Peter’s Basilica. They looked pale. Some fell to their knees. Some passed out. Some sobbed uncontrollably.

“Look! Look!”

Mortati turned, confused, following their pointing fingers. They were pointing to the top of the basilica, the rooftop terrace, where huge statues of Christ and his apostles watched over the crowd.

There, to the right of Jesus, with arms outstretched to the world… stood Camerlegno Carlo Ventresca.
