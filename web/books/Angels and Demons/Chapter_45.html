Angels and Demons

Chapter 45

It won’t work," Vittoria said, pacing the Pope’s office. She looked up at the camerlegno. "Even if a Swiss Guard team can block electronic interference, they will have to be practically on top of the canister before they detect any signal. And that’s if the canister is even accessible… not blocked by other things. What if it’s buried in a metal box somewhere on your grounds? Or up in a metal air duct. There’s no way they’ll trace it. And what if the Swiss Guards have been infiltrated? Who’s to say the search will be honest?"

The camerlegno looked exhausted. “What are you suggesting, Ms. Vetra?”

Vittoria felt annoyed. Isn’t it obvious! “I am suggesting, sir, that you take other actions immediately. We can hope that the commander’s search is successful. At the same time, look out the window. Do you see those people? Those buildings across the piazza? Those media vans? The tourists? They are quite possibly within range of the blast. You need to act now.”

The camerlegno nodded blankly.

Vittoria felt frustrated. Olivetti had convinced everyone there was plenty of time. But Vittoria knew if news of the Vatican crisis leaked out, the entire area could fill with onlookers in minutes. She had seen it once outside the Swiss Parliament building. During a hostage situation involving a bomb, thousands had gathered outside to watch. Despite police warnings that they were in danger, the crowd packed in closer. Nothing drew people in like a tragedy.

“Signore,” Vittoria urged, “the man who killed my father is out there somewhere. Every part of me wants to run from here and hunt him down. But I am standing in your office… because I have a responsibility to you. To you and others. Lives are in danger, signore. Do you hear me?”

The camerlegno did not answer.

Vittoria could hear her own heart racing. Why couldn’t the Swiss Guard trace that damn caller? The Illuminati assassin is the key! He knows where the antimatter is … hell, he knows where the cardinals are! Catch the killer, and everything is solved.

Vittoria sensed she was starting to lose control, a strange distress she barely remembered from childhood, the orphanage years, frustration with no way to handle it. You have tools, she told herself, you always have tools. But it was no use. Her thoughts crowded in, overwhelming her. She was a researcher and problem solver. But this was a problem with no solution. What data do you need? What do you want? She told herself to breathe deeply, but for the first time in her life, she could not. She was suffocating.

Langdon's head ached, and he felt like he was losing his grip on reason. He watched Vittoria and the camerlegno, but his vision was clouded by terrible images: explosions, a mob of reporters, cameras flashing, four branded people.

Shaitan … Lucifer … Bringer of light … Satan …

He shook the evil images from his mind. Planned terrorism, he reminded himself, trying to stay grounded. Planned chaos. He remembered a Radcliffe seminar he had once attended while studying praetorian symbolism. He had never viewed terrorists the same way since.

"Terrorism," the professor had said, "has one main goal. What is it?"

"Killing innocent people?" a student guessed.

"Wrong. Death is only a result of terrorism."

"A display of power?"

"No. There's no weaker way to persuade."

"To cause terror?"

"Exactly. Simply put, the goal of terrorism is to create terror and fear. Fear weakens trust in the system. It weakens the enemy from the inside… causing trouble among the people. Write this down. Terrorism is not an outburst of anger. Terrorism is a political tool. Take away a government's image of perfection, and you take away its people's trust."

Loss of faith …

Was that the heart of the matter? Langdon wondered how Christians worldwide would react to cardinals displayed like butchered animals. If the faith of a saint couldn't shield him from Satan's evils, what hope was there for anyone else? Langdon’s head throbbed harder now… conflicting thoughts battling within him.

Faith doesn't protect you. Medicine and airbags … those are your protectors. God offers no protection. Intelligence is your shield. Understanding. Trust in things that produce real results. When was the last time someone walked on water? Modern miracles come from science … computers, vaccines, space stations … even the sacred miracle of creation. Matter from nothing … in a lab. Who needs God? No! Science is God.

The killer's words echoed in Langdon's mind. Midnight … death in a mathematical sequence … sacrifici vergini nell‘ altare di scienza.“

Then, as quickly as a crowd scattering from a gunshot, the voices vanished.

Robert Langdon shot up. His chair toppled over, hitting the marble floor.

Vittoria and the camerlegno startled.

“I overlooked it,” Langdon whispered, amazed. “It was right there…”

“Overlooked what?” Vittoria asked.

Langdon faced the priest. “Father, for three years, I've requested access to the Vatican Archives. I've been turned down seven times.”

“Mr. Langdon, I am sorry, but this doesn't seem like the right time to bring this up.”

“I need access now. The four missing cardinals. I might be able to figure out where they’re going to be killed.”

Vittoria stared, looking like she hadn't heard right.

The camerlegno looked worried, like he was the target of a bad joke. “You expect me to believe this information is in our archives?”

“I can’t promise I can find it in time, but if you let me in…”

“Mr. Langdon, I need to be in the Sistine Chapel in four minutes. The archives are across Vatican City.”

“You’re serious, right?” Vittoria interrupted, staring into Langdon’s eyes, sensing his sincerity.

“This is no joke,” Langdon said.

“Father,” Vittoria said, turning to the camerlegno, “if there’s a chance… any chance at all of finding where these killings will happen, we could watch those locations and-”

“But the archives?” the camerlegno asked. “How could they have any clue?”

“Explaining it,” Langdon said, “will take more time than you have. But if I’m right, we can use the information to catch the Hassassin.”

The camerlegno looked like he wanted to believe but couldn't. “Christianity’s most sacred books are in that archive. Treasures I myself am not allowed to see.”

“I know that.”

“Only a written order from the curator and the Board of Vatican Librarians allows access.”

“Or,” Langdon stated, “by papal order. It says so in every rejection letter your curator ever sent me.”

The camerlegno nodded.

“I don’t mean to be rude,” Langdon said, “but if I’m not wrong, a papal order comes from this office. As far as I can tell, tonight you have the power of his position. Considering the situation…”

The camerlegno took a pocket watch from his robe and looked at it. “Mr. Langdon, I am ready to give my life tonight, truly, to save this church.”

Langdon saw only truth in the man’s eyes.

“This document,” the camerlegno said, “do you really think it’s here? And that it can help us find these four churches?”

“I wouldn’t have asked so many times if I wasn't sure. Italy is too far to come for no reason on a teacher’s salary. The document you have is an ancient-”

“Please,” the camerlegno interrupted. “Forgive me. I can’t take in any more information right now. Do you know where the secret archives are?”

Langdon felt a thrill of excitement. “Just behind the Santa Ana Gate.”

“Impressive. Most scholars think it’s through the secret door behind St. Peter’s Throne.”

“No. That’s the Archivio della Reverenda di Fabbrica di S. Pietro. A common mistake.”

“A librarian always goes with everyone who enters. Tonight, the librarians are gone. You’re asking for complete access. Not even our cardinals go in alone.”

“I will treat your treasures with great respect and care. Your librarians won’t know I was even there.”

Above, the bells of St. Peter’s began to ring. The camerlegno checked his watch. “I have to go.” He paused, looking tense, and looked at Langdon. “I’ll have a Swiss Guard meet you at the archives. I’m trusting you, Mr. Langdon. Go now.”

Langdon couldn’t speak.

The young priest now seemed strangely calm and confident. He reached over and squeezed Langdon’s shoulder with surprising strength. “I want you to find what you’re looking for. And find it quickly.”
