Angels and Demons

Chapter 114

The confrontation was over quickly.

Camerlegno Ventresca was still yelling when Chartrand went around Rocher and kicked open the Pope’s office door. The guards rushed in. Langdon and Vittoria followed right behind them.

What they saw was shocking.

The room was lit only by candles and a fading fire. Kohler was near the fireplace, standing unsteadily in front of his wheelchair. He held a pistol, pointing it at the camerlegno, who was on the floor at his feet, twisting in pain. The camerlegno’s robe was ripped open, and his bare chest was burned black. Langdon couldn't see the symbol from across the room, but a large, square branding iron was on the floor near Kohler. The metal was still glowing red.

Two of the Swiss Guards reacted immediately. They started shooting. The bullets hit Kohler in the chest, pushing him backward. Kohler fell into his wheelchair, his chest filled with blood. His gun slid across the floor.

Langdon stood frozen in the doorway.

Vittoria seemed unable to move. "Max..." she whispered.

The camerlegno, still writhing on the floor, rolled toward Rocher, and with the terrified look of the old witch hunts, pointed at Rocher and shouted one word. "ILLUMINATUS!"

"You bastard," Rocher said, running at him. "You self-righteous-"

This time it was Chartrand who acted without thinking, shooting Rocher three times in the back. The captain fell forward onto the tile floor and slid, dead, through his own blood. Chartrand and the guards hurried to the camerlegno, who was clutching himself, shaking in pain.

Both guards cried out in horror when they saw the mark burned on the camerlegno’s chest. The second guard saw the mark upside down and immediately stepped back, his eyes wide with fear. Chartrand, also shocked by the mark, pulled the camerlegno’s torn robe up over the burn, hiding it from sight.

Langdon felt dizzy as he moved across the room. Through a haze of madness and violence, he tried to understand what he was seeing. A disabled scientist, in a final show of power, had flown into Vatican City and marked the church’s highest official. Some things are worth dying for , the Hassassin had said. Langdon wondered how a handicapped man could have overpowered the camerlegno. But then, Kohler had a gun. It doesn’t matter how he did it! Kohler finished his mission!

Langdon moved toward the awful scene. The camerlegno was being helped, and Langdon felt drawn to the smoking mark on the floor near Kohler’s wheelchair. The sixth mark? The closer Langdon got, the more confused he became. The mark seemed to be a perfect square, quite large, and had clearly come from the sacred center part of the chest in the Illuminati Lair. A sixth and final mark , the Hassassin had said. The most brilliant of all.

Langdon knelt beside Kohler and reached for the object. The metal was still hot. Grabbing the wooden handle, Langdon picked it up. He wasn't sure what he expected to see, but it definitely wasn't this.

Langdon stared for a long, confused moment. Nothing made sense. Why had the guards cried out in horror when they saw this? It was a square of meaningless scribbles. The most brilliant of all? It was symmetrical, Langdon could tell as he turned it in his hand, but it was nonsense.

When he felt a hand on his shoulder, Langdon looked up, expecting Vittoria. But the hand was covered with blood. It belonged to Maximilian Kohler, who was reaching out from his wheelchair.

Langdon dropped the mark and stood up, shocked. Kohler’s still alive!

Slumped in his wheelchair, the dying director was still breathing, though barely, taking in shaky breaths. Kohler’s eyes met Langdon’s, and it was the same hard look that had greeted Langdon at CERN earlier that day. The eyes looked even harder in death, the hatred and anger rising to the surface.

The scientist's body trembled, and Langdon sensed he was trying to move. Everyone else in the room was watching the camerlegno, and Langdon wanted to shout, but he couldn't move. He was frozen by the strong feeling coming from Kohler in these last moments of his life. The director, with shaky effort, raised his arm and took a small device off his wheelchair's arm. It was about the size of a matchbox. He held it out, trembling. For a moment, Langdon worried Kohler had a weapon. But it was something else.

"G-give..." Kohler's last words were a gurgling whisper. "G-give this... to the m-media." Kohler fell still, and the device dropped into his lap.

Surprised, Langdon stared at the device. It was electronic. The words SONY RUVI were printed on the front. Langdon knew it as one of those new very small, hand-held camcorders. What nerve! he thought. Kohler had likely recorded some kind of final suicide message he wanted the media to show... probably some lecture about how important science is and how bad religion is. Langdon decided he had done enough for this man's cause tonight. Before Chartrand saw Kohler's camcorder, Langdon put it in his deepest jacket pocket. Kohler's final message can stay lost forever!

It was the camerlegno's voice that broke the silence. He was trying to sit up. "The cardinals," he gasped to Chartrand.

"Still in the Sistine Chapel!" Chartrand shouted. "Captain Rocher ordered-"

"Leave... now. Everyone."

Chartrand sent one of the other guards running to let the cardinals out.

The camerlegno winced in pain. "Helicopter... out front... get me to a hospital."
