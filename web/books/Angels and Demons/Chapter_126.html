Angels and Demons

Chapter 126

Cardinal Mortati knew no words could add to the mystery of this moment. The silence of the vision over St. Peter’s Square was louder than any angel choir.

As he stared up at Camerlegno Ventresca, Mortati felt his heart and mind clash. The vision seemed real, touchable. And yet… how could it be? Everyone had seen the camerlegno get in the helicopter. They had all watched the ball of light in the sky. And now, somehow, the camerlegno stood high above them on the rooftop terrace. Brought by angels? Reborn by God?

This is impossible…

Mortati’s heart wanted to believe, but his mind needed reason. Yet all around him, the cardinals stared up, seeing what he was seeing, frozen in wonder.

It was the camerlegno. No doubt. But he looked different somehow. Divine. As if he had been made pure. A spirit? A man? His white skin shone in the spotlights, weightless and unreal.

In the square, people cried, cheered, and applauded. A group of nuns fell to their knees and wailed. A pulsing grew from the crowd. Soon, the entire square chanted the camerlegno’s name. The cardinals, some crying, joined in. Mortati looked around, trying to understand. Is this really happening?

Camerlegno Carlo Ventresca stood on the rooftop terrace of St. Peter’s Basilica and looked down at the crowds staring up at him. Was he awake or dreaming? He felt changed, not of this world. He wondered if his body or just his spirit had floated down from heaven toward the soft, dark Vatican City Gardens… landing like a silent angel on the empty lawns, his black parachute hidden from the chaos by the tall shadow of St. Peter’s Basilica. He wondered if his body or his spirit had had the strength to climb the old Stairway of Medallions to the rooftop terrace where he now stood.

He felt as light as a ghost.

Though the people below chanted his name, he knew they were not cheering for him. They were cheering from sudden joy, the same joy he felt every day as he thought about God. They were feeling what they had always wanted… proof of the afterlife… evidence of the Creator's power.

Camerlegno Ventresca had prayed his whole life for this moment, and still, even he could not believe God had made it happen. He wanted to shout to them, Your God is alive! See the miracles everywhere!

He stood there a moment, unable to feel and yet feeling more than ever. When he finally felt ready, he bowed his head and stepped away from the edge.

Alone now, he knelt on the roof and prayed.
