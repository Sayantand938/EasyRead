Angels and Demons

FACT

The world’s largest scientific research center—Switzerland’s Conseil Europйen pour la Recherche Nuclйaire (CERN)—recently succeeded in making the first antimatter particles. Antimatter is just like regular matter, except its particles have the opposite electric charges.

Antimatter is the most powerful energy source we know. It releases energy with 100 percent efficiency (nuclear fission is 1.5 percent efficient). Antimatter creates no pollution or radiation, and a single drop could power New York City for a whole day.

But there's one problem…

Antimatter is very unstable. It explodes when it touches anything… even air. A single gram of antimatter has the energy of a 20-kiloton nuclear bomb—the same size as the one dropped on Hiroshima.

Until recently, antimatter has only been made in very small amounts (a few atoms at a time). But CERN has now started building its new Antiproton Decelerator—an advanced antimatter production center that should create antimatter in much larger amounts.

One question remains: Will this highly explosive substance save the world, or will it be used to create the deadliest weapon ever?

PROLOGUE

Physicist Leonardo Vetra smelled burning flesh, and he knew it was his own. He stared up in terror at the dark figure looming over him. “What do you want!”

“La chiave,” the raspy voice replied. “The password.”

“But… I don’t-”

The intruder pressed down again, grinding the white hot object deeper into Vetra’s chest. There was the hiss of burning flesh.

Vetra cried out in agony. “There is no password!” He felt himself drifting toward unconsciousness.

The figure glared. “ Ne avevo paura. I was afraid of that.”

Vetra fought to stay awake, but the darkness was closing in. His only comfort was in knowing his attacker would never get what he wanted. A moment later, however, the figure produced a blade and brought it to Vetra’s face. The blade hovered. Carefully. Surgically.

“For the love of God!” Vetra screamed. But it was too late.
