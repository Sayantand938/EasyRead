Angels and Demons

Chapter 71

The scaffolding felt less steady the higher Langdon went. But his view of Rome improved with each step. He kept climbing.

He was breathing harder than he expected when he reached the top. He pulled himself onto the last platform, brushed off the plaster, and stood up. The height didn't bother him at all. In fact, it energized him.

The view was amazing. Like a burning ocean, the red rooftops of Rome stretched out before him, glowing in the red sunset. From there, for the first time, Langdon saw past the pollution and traffic to Rome's ancient heart—Cittа di Dio—The City of God.

Squinting into the sunset, Langdon looked for a church steeple or bell tower. But as he looked toward the horizon, he saw nothing. There are hundreds of churches in Rome, he thought. There must be one southwest of here! If the church is even visible, he reminded himself. Hell, if the church is even still there!

Forcing himself to slowly scan the line of the horizon, he tried again. He knew that not all churches would have visible spires, especially smaller, hidden churches. Also, Rome had changed a lot since the 1600s when churches were legally the tallest buildings. Now, Langdon saw apartment buildings, high-rises, and TV towers.

For the second time, Langdon's gaze reached the horizon without seeing anything. Not a single spire. In the distance, on the edge of Rome, Michelangelo's huge dome blocked the setting sun. St. Peter's Basilica. Vatican City. Langdon wondered how the cardinals were doing, and if the Swiss Guards had found the antimatter. Something told him they hadn't… and wouldn't.

The poem was running through his head again. He thought about it carefully, line by line. From Santi’s earthly tomb with demon’s hole. They had found Santi’s tomb. ‘ Cross Rome the mystic elements unfold. The mystic elements were Earth, Air, Fire, Water. The path of light is laid, the sacred test. The path of Illumination made by Bernini's sculptures. Let angels guide you on your lofty quest.

The angel was pointing southwest…

“Front stairs!” Glick yelled, pointing through the windshield of the BBC van. “Something’s happening!”

Macri lowered her camera back down to the main entrance. Something was definitely happening. At the bottom of the stairs, the military-looking man had pulled one of the Alpha Romeos close and opened the trunk. Now he was scanning the square as if looking for anyone watching. For a moment, Macri thought he’d seen them, but his eyes kept moving. Seeming satisfied, he pulled out a walkie-talkie and spoke into it.

Almost instantly, an army seemed to come out of the church. Like a football team breaking from a huddle, the soldiers formed a straight line across the top of the stairs. Moving like a human wall, they began to walk down. Behind them, almost hidden by the wall, four soldiers seemed to be carrying something. Something heavy. Difficult to manage.

Glick leaned forward. “Are they stealing something from the church?”

Chinita zoomed in even more, using the telephoto to look through the wall of men, searching for an opening. One split second, she thought. A single frame. That’s all I need. But the men moved as one. Come on! Macri stayed focused on them, and it worked. When the soldiers tried to lift the object into the trunk, Macri found her opening. Ironically, it was the older man who stumbled. Only for an instant, but long enough. Macri had her frame. Actually, it was more like ten frames.

“Call editorial,” Chinita said. “We’ve got a dead body.”

Not finding what he wanted, Kohler moved to Vetra’s bedroom. The top drawer of his bedside table was locked. Kohler forced it open with a kitchen knife.
