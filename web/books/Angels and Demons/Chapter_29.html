Angels and Demons

Chapter 29

Vittoria didn't recall arriving at the main elevator, but they were there. Going up. Kohler was behind her, breathing heavily now. Langdon's worried look went right through her. He had taken the fax from her and put it in his jacket pocket where she couldn't see it, but she still remembered the image clearly.

As the elevator rose, Vittoria felt like she was losing her mind. Papa! She reached for him in her thoughts. For a moment, in her memory, Vittoria was with him. She was nine, rolling down hills of edelweiss flowers, the Swiss sky spinning above her.

Papa! Papa!

Leonardo Vetra was laughing next to her, smiling brightly. "What is it, angel?"

"Papa!" she giggled, snuggling close to him. "Ask me what's the matter!"

"But you look happy, sweetie. Why would I ask you what's the matter?"

"Just ask me."

He shrugged. "What's the matter?"

She immediately started laughing. "What's the matter? Everything is the matter! Rocks! Trees! Atoms! Even anteaters! Everything is the matter!"

He laughed. “Did you make that up?”

“Pretty smart, huh?”

“My little Einstein.”

She frowned. “He has dumb hair. I saw his picture.”

“He’s got a smart brain, though. I told you what he proved, right?”

Her eyes widened with dread. “Dad! No! You promised!”

“E=MC2!” He tickled her playfully. “E=MC2!”

“No math! I told you! I hate it!”

“I’m glad you hate it. Because girls aren’t even supposed to do math.”

Vittoria stopped short. “They aren’t?”

“Of course not. Everyone knows that. Girls play with dolls. Boys do math. No math for girls. I’m not even allowed to talk to little girls about math.”

“What! But that’s not fair!”

“Rules are rules. Absolutely no math for little girls.”

Vittoria looked horrified. “But dolls are boring!”

“I’m sorry,” her father said. “I could tell you about math, but if I got caught…” He looked nervously around the empty hills.

Vittoria followed his gaze. “Okay,” she whispered, “just tell me quietly.”

The motion of the elevator startled her. Vittoria opened her eyes. He was gone.

Reality rushed in, wrapping a cold grip around her. She looked to Langdon. The sincere concern in his gaze felt like the warmth of a guardian angel, especially in the presence of Kohler’s chill.

A single thought began pounding at Vittoria with unrelenting force.

Where is the antimatter?

The awful answer was coming in a moment.
