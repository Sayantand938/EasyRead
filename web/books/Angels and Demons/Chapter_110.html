Angels and Demons

Chapter 110

Eleven-twenty-three P.M.

Vittoria stood shaking on the balcony of Castle St. Angelo, looking out over Rome, her eyes wet with tears. She longed to hug Robert Langdon, but she couldn't. Her body felt numb. Readjusting. Assessing the situation. The man who had killed her father lay far below, dead, and she had almost been a victim too.

When Langdon’s hand touched her shoulder, the warmth seemed to magically break the ice. Her body shuddered back to life. The fog cleared, and she turned. Robert looked awful-wet and messy-he had clearly been through hell to come rescue her.

“Thank you…” she whispered.

Langdon gave a tired smile and reminded her that she deserved thanks-her ability to almost pop her shoulders out of their sockets had just saved them both. Vittoria wiped her eyes. She could have stood there with him forever, but the break was short-lived.

“We need to get out of here,” Langdon said.

Vittoria’s mind was elsewhere. She was staring out toward the Vatican. The world’s smallest country looked disturbingly close, glowing white under a flood of media lights. To her shock, much of St. Peter’s Square was still full of people! The Swiss Guard had apparently only been able to clear about a hundred and fifty feet back-the area right in front of the basilica-less than one-third of the square. The crowd around the square was tighter now, those at safer distances pushing to get a better look, trapping the others inside. They are too close! Vittoria thought. Much too close!

“I’m going back in,” Langdon said simply.

Vittoria turned, not believing what she heard. “Into the Vatican?”

Langdon told her about the Samaritan, and how it was a trick. The Illuminati leader, a man named Janus, was actually coming himself to brand the camerlegno. A final Illuminati act of control.

“Nobody in Vatican City knows,” Langdon said. “I can’t contact them, and this guy is arriving any minute. I have to warn the guards before they let him in.”

“But you’ll never get through the crowd!”

Langdon’s voice was confident. “There’s a way. Trust me.”

Vittoria felt again that the historian knew something she didn’t. “I’m coming.”

“No. Why risk both of-”

“I have to find a way to get those people out of there! They’re in terrible dange-”

Just then, the balcony they were standing on began to shake. A deafening rumble shook the whole castle. Then a white light from the direction of St. Peter’s blinded them. Vittoria had only one thought. Oh my God! The antimatter exploded early!

But instead of an explosion, a huge cheer went up from the crowd. Vittoria squinted into the light. It was a mass of media lights from the square, now aimed, it seemed, at them! Everyone was turned toward them, shouting and pointing. The rumble grew louder. The air in the square seemed suddenly joyful.

Langdon looked puzzled. “What the devil-”

The sky above roared.

Coming from behind the tower, without warning, was the Pope's helicopter. It boomed fifty feet above them, heading straight for Vatican City. As it passed over, shining in the media lights, the castle shook. The lights followed the helicopter as it went by, and Langdon and Vittoria were suddenly in the dark again.

Vittoria felt worried that they were too late as they watched the huge machine slow down over St. Peter’s Square. Stirring up a cloud of dust, the chopper landed on the open part of the square between the crowd and the basilica, touching down at the bottom of the basilica’s stairs.

“Now that’s an entrance,” Vittoria said. Against the white marble, she could see a tiny dot of a person come out of the Vatican and go toward the chopper. She wouldn't have known who it was except for the bright red beret on his head. “Red carpet welcome. That’s Rocher.”

Langdon hit his fist on the railing. “Someone has to warn them!” He turned to leave.

Vittoria grabbed his arm. “Wait!” She had just seen something else, something she couldn't believe. Fingers shaking, she pointed toward the chopper. Even from far away, there was no mistake. Coming down the ramp was another person… a person who moved so strangely that it could only be one man. Even though the person was seated, he sped across the open square with easy control and surprising speed.

A king on an electric throne.

It was Maximilian Kohler.
