Angels and Demons

Chapter 103

As a water polo player, Robert Langdon had been through many underwater fights. The fierce competition that happened under the water in a water polo pool, where the referees couldn't see, was as rough as any wrestling match. Langdon had been kicked, scratched, held, and even bitten once by a frustrated defender he kept getting away from.

But now, struggling in the cold water of Bernini’s fountain, Langdon knew he was far from the Harvard pool. He wasn't fighting for a game, but for his life. This was their second fight. No referees here. No do-overs. The arms pushing his face down into the basin were strong and clearly meant to kill him.

Langdon quickly spun around like a torpedo. Break the hold! But the grip twisted him back, his attacker having an advantage no water polo defender ever had—both feet on solid ground. Langdon struggled, trying to get his feet under him. The Hassassin seemed to favor one arm… but his grip remained tight.

It was then that Langdon knew he wasn't going to surface. He did the only thing he could think of. He stopped trying to go up. If you can’t go north, go east. Using his last bit of strength, Langdon kicked his legs like a dolphin and pulled his arms under him in an awkward butterfly stroke. His body shot forward.

The sudden change in direction seemed to surprise the Hassassin. Langdon’s sideways movement pulled his captor’s arms to the side, throwing him off balance. The man's grip weakened, and Langdon kicked again. It felt like a rope had snapped. Suddenly, Langdon was free. Blowing the stale air out of his lungs, Langdon reached for the surface. He only got one breath. The Hassassin came down on him again with full force, palms on his shoulders, pushing all his weight down. Langdon struggled to get his feet under him, but the Hassassin's leg swung out, knocking Langdon down.

He went under again.

Langdon’s muscles burned as he twisted in the water. This time, his efforts were useless. Through the bubbly water, Langdon looked at the bottom, searching for the gun. Everything was blurry. The bubbles were thicker here. A bright light flashed in his face as the killer dragged him deeper, towards an underwater spotlight attached to the fountain floor. Langdon reached out, grabbing the light. It was hot. Langdon tried to pull himself free, but the device was on hinges and turned in his hand. He lost his leverage instantly.

The Hassassin pushed him even deeper.

That's when Langdon saw it. Sticking out from under the coins right in front of his face. A thin, black tube. The silencer from Olivetti’s gun! Langdon reached out, but as his fingers closed around the tube, he felt plastic, not metal. When he pulled, the flexible rubber hose came loose, floating towards him like a weak snake. It was about two feet long, with bubbles coming out of the end. Langdon hadn't found the gun at all. It was just one of the fountain’s harmless spumanti… bubble makers.

Only a few feet away, Cardinal Baggia felt his soul struggling to leave his body. Although he had prepared for this moment his entire life, he had never imagined the end would be like this. His body was in agony… burned, bruised, and held underwater by an immovable weight. He reminded himself that this suffering was nothing compared to what Jesus had endured.

He died for my sins…

Baggia could hear the sounds of a battle nearby. He could not bear the thought of it. His captor was about to end yet another life… the man with kind eyes, the man who had tried to help.

As the pain grew, Baggia lay on his back and stared up through the water at the black sky above him. For a moment he thought he saw stars.

It was time.

Releasing all fear and doubt, Baggia opened his mouth and let out what he knew would be his last breath. He watched his spirit float upward in a burst of transparent bubbles. Then, without thinking, he gasped. The water poured in like icy daggers to his sides. The pain lasted only a few seconds.

Then… peace.

The Hassassin ignored the burning in his foot and focused on the drowning American, whom he now held pinned beneath him in the churning water. Finish it completely. He tightened his grip, knowing this time Robert Langdon would not survive. As he predicted, his victim’s struggling became weaker and weaker.

Suddenly Langdon’s body went rigid. He began to shake wildly.

Yes, the Hassassin thought. The tremors. When the water first fills the lungs. The tremors, he knew, would last about five seconds.

They lasted six.

Then, exactly as the Hassassin expected, his victim suddenly went limp. Like a large balloon losing air, Robert Langdon fell still. It was over. The Hassassin held him down for another thirty seconds to let the water fill his lungs. Slowly, he felt Langdon’s body sink, by itself, to the bottom. Finally, the Hassassin let go. The news would find two surprises in the Fountain of the Four Rivers.

“Tabban!” the Hassassin cursed, climbing out of the fountain and looking at his bleeding toe. The tip of his boot was torn, and the front of his big toe was cut off. Angry at his own mistake, he tore the cuff from his pant leg and stuffed the fabric into the toe of his boot. Pain shot up his leg. “Ibn al-kalb!” He clenched his fists and pushed the cloth deeper. The bleeding slowed to a trickle.

Turning his thoughts from pain to pleasure, the Hassassin got into his van. His work in Rome was done. He knew exactly what would ease his pain. Vittoria Vetra was tied up and waiting. The Hassassin, even cold and wet, felt himself grow hard.

I have earned my reward.

Across town Vittoria woke up in pain. She was on her back. All her muscles felt like stone. Stiff. Fragile. Her arms hurt. When she tried to move, she felt spasms in her shoulders. It took her a moment to realize her hands were tied behind her back. Her first reaction was confusion. Am I dreaming? But when she tried to lift her head, the pain at the base of her skull told her she was awake.

Confusion turning to fear, she looked around. She was in a simple, stone room-large and well-furnished, lit by torches. Some kind of old meeting hall. Old benches sat in a circle nearby.

Vittoria felt a breeze, now cold on her skin. Nearby, a set of double doors stood open, leading to a balcony. Through the gaps in the railing, Vittoria could have sworn she saw the Vatican.
