Angels and Demons

Chapter 113

Something was wrong.

Lieutenant Chartrand stood outside the Pope’s office and felt in the soldier's uneasy posture beside him that they both felt it. The private meeting they were guarding, Rocher had said, could save the Vatican. So Chartrand wondered why he felt so uneasy. And why was Rocher acting so strangely?

Something was definitely wrong.

Captain Rocher stood to Chartrand’s right, staring straight ahead, his sharp gaze unusually distant. Chartrand barely recognized the captain. Rocher hadn’t been himself for the past hour. His decisions didn't make sense.

Someone should be in that meeting! Chartrand thought. He had heard Maximilian Kohler lock the door after he went in. Why did Rocher allow this?

But there was more bothering Chartrand. The cardinals. The cardinals were still locked in the Sistine Chapel. This was insane. The camerlegno had wanted them out fifteen minutes ago! Rocher had stopped it and not told the camerlegno. Chartrand had voiced his worry, and Rocher had almost snapped at him. The Swiss Guard never questioned the chain of command, and Rocher was now in charge.

Half an hour, Rocher thought, glancing at his Swiss watch in the dim candlelight. Please hurry.

Chartrand wished he could hear what was happening behind the doors. Still, he knew the camerlegno was the best person to handle this. The man had been tested beyond belief tonight, and he hadn’t hesitated. He had faced the problem head-on… honest, open, a shining example to all. Chartrand felt proud to be Catholic. The Illuminati had made a mistake challenging Camerlegno Ventresca.

Just then, Chartrand’s thoughts were broken by a sound. A banging. It was coming from down the hall. The pounding was faint and muffled, but constant. Rocher looked up. The captain turned to Chartrand and pointed down the hall. Chartrand understood. He turned on his flashlight and went to check it out.

The banging was more frantic now. Chartrand ran thirty yards down the corridor to where it intersected with another. The noise seemed to be coming from around the corner, past the Sala Clementina. Chartrand was puzzled. There was only one room back there—the Pope’s private library. His Holiness’s private library had been locked since the Pope’s death. Nobody could possibly be in there!

Chartrand hurried down the second corridor, turned another corner, and rushed to the library door. The wooden entrance was small, but it stood in the dark like a stern guard. The banging was coming from somewhere inside. Chartrand hesitated. He had never been inside the private library. Few had. No one was allowed in without the Pope himself as an escort.

Cautiously, Chartrand reached for the doorknob and turned. As he had expected, the door was locked. He put his ear to the door. The banging was louder. Then he heard something else. Voices! Someone calling out!

He could not make out the words, but he could hear the panic in their shouts. Was someone trapped in the library? Had the Swiss Guard not properly evacuated the building? Chartrand hesitated, wondering if he should go back and talk to Rocher. The hell with that. Chartrand had been trained to make decisions, and he would make one now. He pulled out his side arm and fired a single shot into the door latch. The wood splintered, and the door swung open.

Beyond the doorway Chartrand saw only darkness. He shone his flashlight. The room was rectangular—oriental carpets, high oak shelves filled with books, a leather couch, and a marble fireplace. Chartrand had heard stories of this place—three thousand old books next to hundreds of current magazines, anything His Holiness wanted. The coffee table was covered with journals of science and politics.

The banging was clearer now. Chartrand shone his light across the room toward the sound. On the far wall, past the sitting area, was a huge door made of iron. It looked as strong as a vault. It had four large locks. The small etched letters in the center of the door shocked Chartrand.

IL PASSETTO

Chartrand stared. The Pope’s secret escape route! Chartrand had heard of Il Passetto , and he had even heard rumors that it had once had an entrance here in the library, but the tunnel had not been used in years! Who could be banging on the other side?

Chartrand took his flashlight and knocked on the door. There was a muffled cheer from the other side. The banging stopped, and the voices yelled louder. Chartrand could barely make out their words through the barrier.

“… Kohler… lie… camerlegno…”

“Who is that?” Chartrand yelled.

“… rt Langdon… Vittoria Ve…”

Chartrand understood enough to be confused. I thought you were dead!

“… the door,” the voices yelled. “Open… !”

Chartrand looked at the iron barrier and knew he would need dynamite to get through there. “Impossible!” he yelled. “Too thick!”

“… meeting… stop… erlegno… danger…”

Despite his training to avoid panic, Chartrand felt a sudden rush of fear at the last words. Had he understood right? Heart pounding, he turned to run back to the office. As he turned, though, he stopped. He saw something on the door… something even more shocking than the message from beyond it. Coming out of the keyholes of each of the door’s huge locks were keys. Chartrand stared. The keys were here? He blinked, not believing it. The keys to this door were supposed to be in a vault somewhere! This passage was never used—not for centuries!

Chartrand dropped his flashlight. He grabbed the first key and turned it. The lock was rusted and stiff, but it still worked. Someone had opened it recently. Chartrand worked the next lock. And the next. When the last bolt slid open, Chartrand pulled. The iron door creaked open. He grabbed his light and shone it into the passage.

Robert Langdon and Vittoria Vetra looked like ghosts as they stumbled into the library. Both were ragged and tired, but they were very much alive.

“What is this!” Chartrand demanded. “What’s going on! Where did you come from?”

“Where’s Max Kohler?” Langdon demanded.

Chartrand pointed. “In a private meeting with the camer-”

Langdon and Vittoria pushed past him and ran down the darkened hall. Chartrand turned, instinctively raising his gun at their backs. He quickly lowered it and ran after them. Rocher apparently heard them coming, because as they arrived outside the Pope’s office, Rocher had spread his legs in a protective stance and was leveling his gun at them. “Halt!”

“The camerlengo is in danger!” Langdon yelled, raising his arms in surrender as he slid to a stop. “Open the door! Max Kohler is going to kill the camerlengo!”

Rocher looked angry.

“Open the door!” Vittoria said. “Hurry!”

But it was too late.

From inside the Pope’s office came a bloodcurdling scream. It was the camerlengo.
