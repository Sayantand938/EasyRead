Angels and Demons

Chapter 97

The Hassassin smiled as he drove his van into the huge stone building overlooking the Tiber River. He carried his prize up and up… spiraling higher in the stone tunnel, glad his load was thin.

He arrived at the door.

The Church of Illumination, he thought proudly. The old Illuminati meeting room. Who would have thought it was here?

Inside, he lay her on a soft couch. Then he skillfully tied her arms behind her back and bound her feet. He knew that what he wanted would have to wait until his final task was done. Water.

Still, he thought, he had a moment to enjoy himself. Kneeling beside her, he ran his hand along her thigh. It was smooth. Higher. His dark fingers slid under the edge of her shorts. Higher.

He stopped. Patience, he told himself, feeling excited. There is work to be done.

He walked for a moment out onto the chamber’s high stone balcony. The evening breeze slowly cooled his passion. Far below, the Tiber flowed powerfully. He raised his eyes to the dome of St. Peter’s, a little less than a mile away, exposed under the bright lights of hundreds of cameras.

“Your final hour,” he said aloud, picturing the thousands of Muslims killed during the Crusades. “At midnight you will meet your God.”

Behind him, the woman moved. The Hassassin turned. He thought about letting her wake up. Seeing fear in a woman’s eyes was the most exciting thing to him.

He chose to be careful. It was best she stayed asleep while he was away. Even though she was tied up and couldn't escape, the Hassassin didn't want to come back and find her tired from fighting. I want you strong… for me.

Gently raising her head, he put his hand under her neck and found the spot under her skull. He had used this pressure point many times. With strong force, he pushed his thumb into the soft spot and felt it give way. The woman immediately went limp. Twenty minutes, he thought. She would be a wonderful end to a perfect day. After she had pleased him and died doing so, he would stand on the balcony and watch the fireworks over the Vatican at midnight.

Leaving his unconscious prize on the couch, the Hassassin went downstairs to a dungeon lit by torches. The last task. He walked to the table and honored the sacred metal shapes that had been left for him.

Water. It was his last.

Taking a torch from the wall as he had done three times before, he began to heat the end. When the end of the object was glowing white, he took it to the cell.

Inside, a lone man stood silently. Old and alone.

“Cardinal Baggia,” the killer whispered. “Have you prayed yet?”

The Italian’s eyes showed no fear. “Only for your soul.”
