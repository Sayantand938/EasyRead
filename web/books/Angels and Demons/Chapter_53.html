Angels and Demons

Chapter 53

Somewhere beneath Rome, the dark figure crept down a stone ramp into the underground tunnel. The old passageway was lit only by torches, making the air hot and heavy. Up ahead, the scared voices of grown men cried out, echoing in the small space.

As he turned the corner, he saw them, just as he had left them—four old men, terrified, trapped behind rusty iron bars in a stone cell.

“Qui êtes-vous?” one of the men asked in French. “What do you want with us?”

“Hilfe!” another said in German. “Let us go!”

“Do you know who we are?” one asked in English, his accent Spanish.

“Silence,” the rough voice ordered. The word was final.

The fourth prisoner, an Italian, quiet and thoughtful, looked into the dark emptiness of his captor’s eyes and felt he saw hell itself. God help us, he thought.

The killer checked his watch and then looked back at the prisoners. “Now then,” he said. “Who will be first?”
