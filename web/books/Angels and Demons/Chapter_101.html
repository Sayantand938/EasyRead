Angels and Demons

Chapter 101

Gunther Glick sat on a bench in a holding room inside the Swiss Guard's office. He prayed to every god he could think of. Please let this NOT be a dream. It was the biggest story of his life. The biggest story of anyone’s life. Every reporter on earth wished they were Glick right now. You are awake, he told himself. And you are a star. Dan Rather is crying right now.

Macri was next to him, looking a little stunned. Glick didn’t blame her. Besides exclusively broadcasting the camerlegno’s speech, she and Glick had given the world gruesome photos of the cardinals and the Pope-that tongue! -as well as a live video feed of the antimatter canister counting down. Incredible!

Of course, all of that had been at the camerlegno’s request, so that wasn’t why Glick and Macri were now locked in a Swiss Guard holding room. It was Glick’s daring addition to their coverage that the guards hadn’t liked. Glick knew the conversation he had just reported on wasn’t meant for his ears, but this was his moment to shine. Another Glick scoop!

“The 11th Hour Samaritan?” Macri groaned on the bench next to him, clearly unimpressed.

Glick smiled. “Brilliant, wasn’t it?”

“Brilliantly dumb.”

She’s just jealous, Glick knew. Shortly after the camerlegno’s speech, Glick had again, by chance, been in the right place at the right time. He’d overheard Rocher giving new orders to his men. Apparently, Rocher had received a phone call from a mysterious person who Rocher said had important information about the current crisis. Rocher was talking as if this man could help them and was telling his guards to prepare for the guest’s arrival.

Although the information was clearly private, Glick had done what any dedicated reporter would—without honor. He’d found a dark corner, told Macri to set up her remote camera, and he’d reported the news.

“Shocking new developments in God’s city,” he had announced, squinting his eyes for extra drama. Then he’d said that a mystery guest was coming to Vatican City to save the day. The 11th Hour Samaritan, Glick had called him—a perfect name for the faceless man appearing at the last moment to do a good deed. The other networks had picked up the catchy phrase, and Glick was again famous.

I’m brilliant, he thought. Peter Jennings just jumped off a bridge.

Of course, Glick didn't stop there. Since he had everyone's attention, he tossed in his own conspiracy theory for good measure.

Brilliant. Completely brilliant.

“You messed us up,” Macri said. “You totally blew it.”

“What do you mean? I was great!”

Macri stared in disbelief. “Former President George Bush? An Illuminatus?”

Glick smiled. How much clearer could it be? George Bush was a well-known, 33rd-degree Mason, and he was the head of the CIA when they closed their Illuminati investigation due to lack of evidence. And all those speeches about “a thousand points of light” and a “New World Order”… Bush was obviously Illuminati.

“And that part about CERN?” Macri scolded. “You're going to have a long line of lawyers waiting for you tomorrow.”

“CERN? Oh, come on! It’s so obvious! Think about it! The Illuminati disappear in the 1950s around the same time CERN is founded. CERN is a safe place for the smartest people on earth. Lots of private money. They build a weapon that can destroy the church, and oops!… they lose it!”

“So you tell the world that CERN is the new base for the Illuminati?”

“Of course! Groups like that don’t just vanish. The Illuminati had to go somewhere. CERN is a great place to hide. I’m not saying everyone at CERN is Illuminati. It’s probably like a big club, where most people are innocent, but the leaders-”

“Ever heard of saying something untrue, Glick? Getting in trouble?”

“Ever heard of real news!”

“News? You were making stuff up! I should have stopped recording! And what was that nonsense about CERN’s logo? Satanic symbols? Are you crazy?”

Glick smiled. Macri was clearly jealous. The CERN logo had been the best part. Since the priest’s speech, all the news was about CERN and antimatter. Some channels were showing the CERN logo in the background. The logo seemed normal-two circles crossing, for two particle accelerators, and five lines touching, for particle injection tubes. The whole world was looking at this logo, but Glick, who knew a bit about symbols himself, had been the first to see the Illuminati symbols hidden in it.

“You’re not a symbol expert,” Macri said, “you just got lucky. You should have let the Harvard guy handle the symbols.”

“The Harvard guy didn’t see it,” Glick said.

The Illuminati meaning in this logo is so clear!

He was thrilled inside. CERN had many accelerators, but their logo showed only two. Two is the Illuminati number of opposites. Most accelerators had only one injection tube, but the logo showed five. Five is the number of the Illuminati star. Then came the best part. Glick pointed out that the logo had a big number “6”-clearly made by one of the lines and circles-and when the logo was turned, another six showed up… and then another. The logo had three sixes! 666! The devil’s number! The mark of the beast!

Glick was a genius.

Macri looked ready to punch him.

The jealousy would pass, Glick knew, his mind now wandering to another thought. If CERN was Illuminati headquarters, was CERN where the Illuminati kept their infamous Illuminati Diamond? Glick had read about it online – “a perfect diamond, made of ancient elements with such perfection that everyone who saw it could only stand in awe.”

Glick wondered if he could uncover the secret location of the Illuminati Diamond tonight, adding it to the list of mysteries he was solving.
