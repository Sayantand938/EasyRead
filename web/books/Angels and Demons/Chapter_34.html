Angels and Demons

Chapter 34

Cardinal Mortati looked up at the beautiful ceiling of the Sistine Chapel, trying to find a quiet moment. The frescoed walls echoed with the voices of cardinals from all over the world. The men bustled about in the candlelit space, whispering excitedly and talking to each other in many languages, mainly English, Italian, and Spanish.

The light in the chapel was usually amazing—long rays of colorful sunlight cutting through the darkness like beams from heaven—but not today. As usual, all the chapel's windows were covered in black velvet to keep things secret. This made sure no one inside could send signals or talk to anyone outside. The result was a deep darkness lit only by candles… a shimmering glow that seemed to purify everyone it touched, making them look ghostly… like saints.

What an honor, Mortati thought, to oversee this sacred event. Cardinals over eighty years old were too old to be elected and didn't come to the conclave, but at seventy-nine, Mortati was the oldest cardinal here and had been chosen to oversee everything.

Following tradition, the cardinals gathered here two hours before the conclave to catch up with friends and have last-minute discussions. At 7 P.M., the late Pope’s chamberlain would arrive, say the opening prayer, and then leave. Then the Swiss Guard would close and lock all the cardinals inside. That's when the oldest and most secretive political ritual in the world would begin. The cardinals wouldn't be released until they decided who among them would be the next Pope.

Conclave. Even the name was secretive. “Con clave” literally meant "locked with a key." The cardinals weren't allowed any contact with the outside world. No phone calls. No messages. No whispers through doorways. The conclave was isolated, not to be influenced by anything outside. This would make sure the cardinals kept Solum Dum prae oculis … only God before their eyes.

Outside the chapel walls, of course, the media watched and waited, guessing which of the cardinals would become the leader of one billion Catholics worldwide. Conclaves created a tense, politically charged atmosphere, and over the centuries they had turned deadly; poisonings, fist fights, and even murder had happened within the sacred walls. Ancient history, Mortati thought. Tonight’s conclave will be united, joyful, and above all … short.

Or at least that’s what he had thought.

But now, something unexpected had happened. Strangely, four cardinals were missing from the chapel. Mortati knew that all the exits to Vatican City were guarded, and the missing cardinals couldn't have gone far, but still, with less than an hour before the opening prayer, he was feeling uneasy. After all, the four missing men weren't ordinary cardinals. They were the cardinals.

The chosen four.

As head of the meeting, Mortati had already told the Swiss Guard through the right people that the cardinals were missing. He hadn't heard back yet. Other cardinals were now seeing that they were gone. Worried whispers had started. Of all cardinals, these four should be on time! Cardinal Mortati was starting to worry it might be a long night after all.

He had no idea.
