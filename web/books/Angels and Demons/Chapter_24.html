Angels and Demons

Chapter 24

The security technician held his breath as his commander leaned over his shoulder, studying the bank of security monitors before them. A minute passed.

The commander’s silence was normal, the technician thought. The commander followed strict rules. He hadn't become the head of one of the world’s best security forces by acting without thinking.

But what is he thinking?

The thing they were looking at on the monitor was a container of some kind—a container with clear sides. That much was simple. It was the rest that was hard.

Inside the container, as if by magic, a small drop of metallic liquid seemed to be floating in the air. The drop appeared and disappeared with the robotic red blinking of a digital LED going down steadily, making the technician’s skin crawl.

“Can you make it brighter?” the commander asked, surprising the technician.

The technician did as he was told, and the picture became a little brighter. The commander leaned forward, squinting at something that had just appeared at the bottom of the container.

The technician looked where his commander was looking. Very faintly, next to the LED, was a set of initials. Four capital letters shining in the on-and-off light.

“Stay here,” the commander said. “Say nothing. I’ll take care of this.”
