Angels and Demons

Chapter 81

The television in the Pope's Office was a large Hitachi hidden in a wall cabinet across from his desk. The cabinet doors were now open, and everyone crowded around. Vittoria moved closer. As the screen lit up, a young female reporter appeared. She was a brunette with wide eyes.

"For MSNBC news," she said, "this is Kelly Horan-Jones, live from Vatican City." The image behind her was a night view of St. Peter's Basilica with all its lights on.

"You're not live," Rocher said sharply. "That's old footage! The lights in the basilica are out."

Olivetti quieted him with a hiss.

The reporter continued, sounding strained. "Shocking news from the Vatican elections tonight. We have reports that two members of the College of Cardinals have been brutally murdered in Rome."

Olivetti cursed quietly.

As the reporter spoke, a guard appeared at the door, out of breath. "Commander, the switchboard says all lines are busy. They want our official statement on-"

"Disconnect it," Olivetti said, not looking away from the TV.

The guard looked unsure. "But, commander-"

“Go!”

The guard ran off.

Vittoria felt the camerlegno had wanted to speak but held back. Instead, he stared at Olivetti for a long moment before turning back to the television.

MSNBC was now playing a recording. Swiss Guards carried Cardinal Ebner's body down the steps outside Santa Maria del Popolo and placed him in an Alpha Romeo. The recording paused and zoomed in as the cardinal’s naked body became visible just before they put him in the car trunk.

“Who shot this?” Olivetti asked sharply.

The MSNBC reporter continued speaking. “This is thought to be the body of Cardinal Ebner of Frankfurt, Germany. The men removing his body from the church are believed to be Vatican Swiss Guard.” The reporter looked like she was trying hard to seem appropriately saddened. They focused on her face, and she looked even more serious. “At this time, MSNBC wants to give our viewers a warning. The images we are about to show are very graphic and may not be suitable for everyone.”

Vittoria scoffed at the station’s fake concern for what viewers could handle, knowing the warning was just that—a way to tempt viewers. No one ever changed channels after a promise like that.

The reporter emphasized, “Again, this footage may be disturbing to some viewers.”

“What footage?” Olivetti demanded. “You just showed-”

The screen now showed a couple in St. Peter’s Square, walking through the crowd. Vittoria immediately recognized Robert and herself. In the corner of the screen was a text: COURTESY OF THE BBC. A bell was ringing.

“Oh, no,” Vittoria said aloud. “Oh… no.”

The camerlegno looked confused. He turned to Olivetti. “I thought you said you took this tape!”

Suddenly, on television, a child was screaming. The image moved to show a little girl pointing at what looked like a bloody homeless man. Robert Langdon walked quickly into the picture, trying to help the little girl. The shot zoomed in.

Everyone in the Pope’s office stared in horrified silence as the scene played out before them. The cardinal’s body fell face first onto the pavement. Vittoria appeared and shouted orders. There was blood. A brand. A terrible, unsuccessful attempt to give CPR.

“This shocking footage,” the reporter was saying, “was shot just minutes ago outside the Vatican. Our sources say this is the body of Cardinal Lamassй from France. How he came to be dressed like this and why he was not in conclave are still unknown. So far, the Vatican has not commented.” The tape began to play again.

“Not commented?” Rocher said. “Give us a minute!”

The reporter was still talking, her eyebrows showing her concern. “Although MSNBC has not yet confirmed why the attack happened, our sources say that a group calling themselves the Illuminati have claimed they are responsible for the murders.”

Olivetti shouted. “ What!”

“… find out more about the Illuminati by visiting our website at-”

“ Non й posibile!” Olivetti declared. He changed channels.

This station had a Hispanic male reporter. “-a satanic cult called the Illuminati, who some historians think-”

Olivetti started pressing the remote quickly. Every channel was showing live updates. Most were in English.

“-Swiss Guards taking a body from a church earlier tonight. The body is thought to be Cardinal-”

“-lights in the basilica and museums are off, leading to guesses-”

“-will be talking with conspiracy theorist Tyler Tingley, about this shocking return-”

“-rumors of two more murders planned for tonight-”

“-wondering now if papal hopeful Cardinal Baggia is among the missing-”

Vittoria looked away. Everything was happening so quickly. Outside the window, in the growing dark, the strong pull of human tragedy seemed to be drawing people to Vatican City. The crowd in the square grew almost instantly. People walked toward them while a new group of media people unloaded vans and set up in St. Peter’s Square.

Olivetti put down the remote control and turned to the camerlegno. “Signore, I can’t imagine how this happened. We took the tape from that camera!”

The camerlegno seemed too shocked to speak for a moment.

Nobody spoke. The Swiss Guards stood perfectly still.

“It seems,” the camerlegno said at last, sounding too heartbroken to be angry, “that we haven’t controlled this problem as well as I thought.” He looked out the window at the growing crowd. “I need to speak to them.”

Olivetti shook his head. “No, signore. That’s exactly what the Illuminati want you to do—prove they’re real, give them power. We must stay quiet.”

“And these people?” The camerlegno pointed out the window. “There will be tens of thousands soon. Then hundreds of thousands. Keeping up this act only puts them in danger. I need to warn them. Then we need to move our College of Cardinals to safety.”

“There’s still time. Let Captain Rocher find the antimatter.”

The camerlegno turned. “Are you trying to tell me what to do?”

“No, I’m giving you advice. If you’re worried about the people outside, we can say there’s a gas leak and clear the area, but admitting we’re being held hostage is dangerous.”

“Commander, I’ll say this only once. I won’t use this position to lie to the world. If I say anything at all, it will be the truth.”

“The truth? That Vatican City is about to be destroyed by evil terrorists? That only makes us weaker.”

The camerlegno glared. “How much weaker could we be?”

Rocher shouted suddenly, grabbing the remote and turning up the TV. Everyone turned to look.

On TV, the MSNBC woman now looked truly scared. Next to her was a picture of the dead Pope. “…breaking news. This just in from the BBC…” She looked to the side, as if checking she should really say this. Seeming to get the okay, she turned and seriously faced the camera. “The Illuminati have just said they were responsible for…” She paused. “They have said they killed the Pope fifteen days ago.”

The camerlegno’s mouth dropped open.

Rocher dropped the remote.

Vittoria could barely understand what she was hearing.

“Vatican law,” the woman went on, “says a Pope is never officially autopsied, so we can’t prove the Illuminati killed him. But, the Illuminati say the Pope didn’t die of a stroke like the Vatican said, but from poisoning.”

The room went completely quiet again.

Olivetti exploded. “Nonsense! A complete lie!”

Rocher started changing channels fast. The news seemed to be spreading like a disease from one channel to the next. Everyone was reporting the same thing. Headlines tried to be as shocking as possible.

MURDER AT THE VATICAN

POPE POISONED

SATAN TOUCHES HOUSE OF GOD

The camerlegno looked away. “God help us.”

As Rocher changed channels, he passed a BBC station. “-told me about the killing at Santa Maria de Popolo-”

“Wait!” the camerlegno said. “Go back.”

Rocher went back. On screen, a neat-looking man sat at a BBC news desk. Behind him was a photo of an odd-looking man with a red beard. Under the photo, it said: GUNTHER GLICK-LIVE IN VATICAN CITY. Reporter Glick was speaking by phone, the connection poor. “… my videographer filmed the cardinal being taken from the Chigi Chapel.”

“Let me repeat for our viewers,” the anchorman in London said, “BBC reporter Gunther Glick first reported this story. He has spoken by phone twice now with the supposed Illuminati killer. Gunther, did you say the killer just called to give you a message from the Illuminati?”

“He did.”

“And their message was that the Illuminati were somehow responsible for the Pope’s death?” The anchorman sounded doubtful.

“Correct. The caller told me that the Pope’s death was not a stroke, as the Vatican thought, but that the Pope had been poisoned by the Illuminati.”

Everyone in the Pope’s office froze.

“Poisoned?” the anchorman asked. “But… but how!”

“They didn’t give details,” Glick replied, “except they killed him with a drug called…”-there was a rustling of papers on the line-“something called Heparin.”

The camerlegno, Olivetti, and Rocher all looked confused.

“Heparin?” Rocher asked, looking worried. “But isn’t that… ?”

The camerlegno turned pale. “The Pope’s medicine.”

Vittoria was shocked. “The Pope was on Heparin?”

“He had thrombophlebitis,” the camerlegno said. “He had an injection every day.”

Rocher looked stunned. “But Heparin isn’t a poison. Why would the Illuminati claim-”

“Heparin can be deadly in the wrong amounts,” Vittoria said. “It’s a strong blood thinner. Too much would cause severe internal bleeding and brain hemorrhages.”

Olivetti looked at her suspiciously. “How would you know that?”

“Marine biologists use it on sea mammals in captivity to stop their blood from clotting due to less activity. Animals have died when the drug wasn't given correctly.” She paused. “A Heparin overdose in a human would cause symptoms that could easily be mistaken for a stroke… especially if there wasn't a proper autopsy.”

The camerlegno now looked very worried.

“Signore,” Olivetti said, “this is clearly an Illuminati trick to get attention. It would be impossible for someone to overdose the Pope. Nobody had access. And even if we take the bait and try to prove them wrong, how could we? Papal law forbids autopsies. Even with an autopsy, we wouldn't learn anything. We would find traces of Heparin in his body from his daily injections.”

“True.” The camerlegno’s voice became sharper. “And yet something else worries me. No one outside knew His Holiness was taking this medication.”

There was silence.

“If he overdosed with Heparin,” Vittoria said, “his body would show signs.”

Olivetti turned to her quickly. “Ms. Vetra, in case you didn’t hear me, Vatican Law prohibits papal autopsies. We are not going to disrespect His Holiness’s body by cutting him open just because an enemy makes a mocking claim!”

Vittoria felt ashamed. “I didn't mean…” She hadn't meant to seem disrespectful. “I certainly wasn't suggesting you dig up the Pope…” She hesitated, though. Something Robert told her in the Chigi flashed through her mind. He had mentioned that papal coffins were above ground and never sealed shut, a tradition from the days of the pharaohs when sealing and burying a casket was thought to trap the deceased’s soul inside. Gravity had become the way to keep them closed, with coffin lids often weighing hundreds of pounds. Technically , she realized, it would be possible to -

“What kind of signs?” the camerlegno said suddenly.

Vittoria felt her heart flutter with fear. “Overdoses can cause bleeding in the mouth.”

“Oral what?”

“The victim’s gums would bleed. After death, the blood thickens and turns the inside of the mouth black.” Vittoria had once seen a photo taken at an aquarium in London where a pair of killer whales had been mistakenly overdosed by their trainer. The whales floated lifeless in the tank, their mouths hanging open and their tongues black as soot.

The camerlegno made no reply. He turned and stared out the window.

Rocher’s voice had lost its optimism. “Signore, if this claim about poisoning is true…”

“It’s not true,” Olivetti declared. “It’s completely impossible for an outsider to access the Pope.”

“ If this claim is true,” Rocher repeated, “and our Holy Father was poisoned, then that has profound implications for our antimatter search. The suspected assassination implies a much deeper infiltration of Vatican City than we had imagined. Searching the white zones may not be enough. If we are compromised so deeply, we may not find the canister in time.”

Olivetti leveled his captain with a cold stare. “Captain, I will tell you what is going to happen.”

“No,” the camerlegno said, turning suddenly. “I will tell you what is going to happen.” He looked directly at Olivetti. “This has gone far enough. In twenty minutes I will decide whether or not to cancel conclave and evacuate Vatican City. My decision will be final. Is that clear?”

Olivetti did not blink. Nor did he respond.

The camerlegno spoke strongly now, as if using a hidden source of strength. “Captain Rocher, finish searching the white zones and report directly to me when you’re done.”

Rocher nodded, giving Olivetti a worried look.

The camerlegno then pointed to two guards. “Bring the BBC reporter, Mr. Glick, to this office right away. If the Illuminati have been talking to him, he might be able to help us. Go.”

The two soldiers left quickly.

Now the camerlegno turned to the remaining guards. “Gentlemen, I won’t allow any more deaths tonight. By ten o’clock, find the other two cardinals and catch the monster who committed these murders. Do you understand?”

“But, signore,” Olivetti replied, “we have no idea where-”

“Mr. Langdon is working on it. He seems able. I trust him.”

With that, the camerlegno walked quickly toward the door, showing a new resolve. As he left, he pointed to three guards. “You three, come with me. Now.”

The guards followed.

In the doorway, the camerlegno stopped. He turned to Vittoria. “Ms. Vetra. You too. Please come with me.”

Vittoria paused. “Where are we going?”

He walked out the door. “To see an old friend.”
