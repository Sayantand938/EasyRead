Angels and Demons

Chapter 59

The Swiss Guard "staging room" is next to the Corpo di Vigilanza barracks and is mainly used to plan security for papal events and public Vatican gatherings. But today, it was being used for something different.

The man speaking to the assembled group was Captain Elias Rocher, the second-in-command of the Swiss Guard. Rocher was a burly man with soft, doughy features. He wore the standard blue captain's uniform with his own touch—a red beret tilted on his head. His voice was surprisingly clear for such a big man, and when he spoke, his tone was like a musical instrument. Despite his precise way of speaking, Rocher’s eyes were cloudy like those of an animal that hunts at night. His men called him "orso"—grizzly bear. They sometimes joked that Rocher was "the bear who walked in the viper's shadow." Commander Olivetti was the viper. Rocher was just as dangerous as the viper, but at least you could see him coming.

Rocher's men stood perfectly still, not moving a muscle, even though the information they had just heard had greatly increased their blood pressure.

Rookie Lieutenant Chartrand stood in the back of the room, wishing he had been among the 99 percent of applicants who had not been chosen to be there. At twenty years old, Chartrand was the youngest guard on the force. He had only been in Vatican City for three months. Like every man there, Chartrand was trained by the Swiss Army and had completed two years of additional ausbilding in Bern before qualifying for the difficult Vatican prтva held in a secret barracks outside of Rome. But nothing in his training had prepared him for a crisis like this.

At first, Chartrand thought the briefing was some kind of strange training exercise. Futuristic weapons? Ancient cults? Kidnapped cardinals? Then Rocher had shown them the live video of the weapon. It was clear that this was no exercise.

"We will be cutting power in certain areas," Rocher was saying, "to remove unwanted magnetic interference. We will move in teams of four. We will wear infrared goggles to see. We will search using traditional bug detectors, adjusted for sub-three-ohm flux fields. Any questions?"

None.

Chartrand's mind was racing. "What if we don't find it in time?" he asked, immediately regretting it.

The grizzly bear looked at him from under his red beret. Then he dismissed the group with a serious salute. "Godspeed, men."
