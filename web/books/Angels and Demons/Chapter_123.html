Angels and Demons

Chapter 123

In St. Peter’s Square, Vittoria Vetra stared upward. The helicopter was a tiny dot now, the media lights unable to reach it. Even the loud rotor sound had become a distant hum. It seemed, at that moment, that everyone in the world was looking upward, quiet and waiting, heads tilted to the sky… all people, all beliefs… all hearts beating together.

Vittoria’s feelings were a storm of mixed pain. As the helicopter vanished, she pictured Robert’s face, looking down at her. What was he thinking? Didn’t he get it?

Around the square, television cameras scanned the darkness, waiting. Many faces stared upward, together in a silent countdown. The media screens all showed the same peaceful scene… a Roman sky full of bright stars. Vittoria felt tears start to fill her eyes.

Behind her on the marble ledge, 161 cardinals stared up in silent wonder. Some clasped their hands in prayer. Most stood still, frozen. Some cried. The seconds passed.

In homes, bars, businesses, airports, hospitals across the globe, people were joined in a shared experience. Men and women held hands. Others held their children. Time seemed to stop, everyone waiting together.

Then, terribly, the bells of St. Peter’s began to ring.

Vittoria let the tears fall.

Then… as the whole world watched… time was up.

The complete silence after was the most frightening thing of all.

High above Vatican City, a tiny light appeared in the sky. For a brief moment, a new star was born… a speck of light as pure and white as could be imagined.

Then it happened.

A flash. The point expanded, as if feeding on itself, spreading across the sky in a widening circle of blinding white. It shot out in all directions, speeding up with unbelievable speed, swallowing the dark. As the sphere of light grew, it became more intense, like a growing monster ready to eat the entire sky. It rushed downward, toward them, gaining speed.

Blinded, the masses of brightly lit faces gasped together, shielding their eyes, crying out in choked fear.

As the light exploded in all directions, the impossible happened. As if controlled by God’s will, the expanding circle seemed to hit a wall. It was as if the explosion were trapped in a giant glass ball. The light bounced back inward, becoming sharper, rippling across itself. The wave seemed to have reached a set size and stopped there. For that moment, a perfect and silent sphere of light glowed over Rome. Night had turned into day.

Then it hit.

The impact was deep and hollow-a booming shock wave from above. It fell on them like the fury of hell, shaking the stone foundation of Vatican City, knocking the air out of people’s chests, sending others staggering back. The echo circled the colonnade, followed by a sudden rush of warm air. The wind tore through the square, letting out a mournful sound as it whistled through the columns and struck the walls. Dust spun overhead as people huddled… witnesses to the end of the world.

Then, as quickly as it appeared, the sphere collapsed, sucking back in on itself, crushing inward to the tiny point of light from which it had come.
