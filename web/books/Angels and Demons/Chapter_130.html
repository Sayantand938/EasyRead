Angels and Demons

Chapter 130

The camerlegno began to feel the wonder and excitement fade away. As the Swiss Guard helped him down the Royal Staircase toward the Sistine Chapel, the camerlegno heard singing in St. Peter’s Square and he knew that great things had been done.

Thank God.

He had prayed for strength, and God had given it to him. When he had doubted, God had spoken. Your mission is sacred, God had said. I will give you strength. Even with God’s strength, the camerlegno had felt fear, questioning if he was doing the right thing.

If not you, God had asked, then WHO?

If not now, then WHEN?

If not this way, then HOW?

Jesus, God reminded him, had saved them all… saved them from their own lack of concern. With two acts, Jesus had opened their eyes. Horror and Hope. The crucifixion and the resurrection. He had changed the world.

But that was thousands of years ago. Time had worn away the miracle. People had forgotten. They had turned to false idols-tech gods and mental wonders. What about miracles of the heart!

The camerlegno had often prayed to God to show him how to make the people believe again. But God had been silent. It was not until the camerlegno’s darkest moment that God had come to him. Oh, the horror of that night!

The camerlegno could still remember lying on the floor in torn nightclothes, scratching at his skin, trying to rid his soul of the pain from a terrible truth he had just learned. It cannot be! he had screamed. Yet he knew it was. The lie tore at him like hellfire. The bishop who had taken him in, the man who had been like a father, the clergyman whom the camerlegno had stood beside as he became Pope… was a fake. A common sinner. Lying to the world about a deed so deeply treacherous that the camerlegno doubted even God could forgive it. “Your vow!” the camerlegno had screamed at the Pope. “You broke your vow to God! You, of all men!”

The Pope had tried to explain, but the camerlegno could not listen. He had run out, stumbling blindly through the halls, vomiting, tearing at his skin, until he found himself bloody and alone, lying on the cold dirt floor before St. Peter’s tomb. Mother Mary, what do I do? It was in that moment of pain and betrayal, as the camerlegno lay broken in the Necropolis, praying for God to take him from this faithless world, that God had come.

The voice in his head echoed like thunder. “Did you vow to serve your God?”

“Yes!” the camerlegno cried out.

“Would you die for your God?”

“Yes! Take me now!”

“Would you die for your church?”

“Yes! Please save me!”

“But would you die for… mankind?”

It was in the silence that followed that the camerlegno felt himself falling into the abyss. He tumbled farther, faster, out of control. Yet he knew the answer. He had always known.

“Yes!” he shouted into the chaos. “I would die for people! Like your son, I would die for them!”

Hours later, the camerlegno still lay shivering on his floor. He saw his mother’s face. God has plans for you, she was saying. The camerlegno sank deeper into madness. It was then God had spoken again. This time with silence. But the camerlegno understood. Bring back their faith.

If not me… then who?

If not now… then when?

As the guards unlocked the door of the Sistine Chapel, Camerlegno Carlo Ventresca felt the power moving in his veins… exactly as it had when he was a boy. God had chosen him. Long ago.

His will be done.

The camerlegno felt reborn. The Swiss Guard had bandaged his chest, bathed him, and dressed him in a fresh white linen robe. They had also given him a shot of morphine for the burn. The camerlegno wished they had not given him painkillers. Jesus endured his pain for three days on the cross! He could already feel the drug clouding his senses… a dizzying pull.

As he walked into the chapel, he was not at all surprised to see the cardinals staring at him in wonder. They are in awe of God, he reminded himself. Not of me, but how God works THROUGH me. As he moved up the center aisle, he saw confusion in every face. And yet, with each new face he passed, he sensed something else in their eyes. What was it? The camerlegno had tried to imagine how they would react to him tonight. Joyfully? Respectfully? He tried to read their eyes and saw neither emotion.

It was then the camerlegno looked at the altar and saw Robert Langdon.
