Angels and Demons

Chapter 92

Three votes. No Pope.

Inside the Sistine Chapel, Cardinal Mortati started praying for a miracle. Send us the candidates! The delay had gone on too long. Mortati could understand a single missing candidate. But all four? It left no choices. Getting a two-thirds majority under these conditions would take an act of God Himself.

When the bolts on the outer door started to grind open, Mortati and all the Cardinals turned together toward the entrance. Mortati knew this opening could mean only one thing. By law, the chapel door could only be opened for two reasons—to remove the very sick, or to let in late cardinals.

The favorites are coming!

Mortati’s heart leaped. The conclave had been saved.

But when the door opened, the gasp that spread through the chapel was not one of joy. Mortati stared in disbelief as the man walked in. For the first time in Vatican history, a camerlegno had just crossed the sacred entrance of the conclave after the doors were sealed.

What is he thinking!

The camerlegno walked to the altar and turned to speak to the stunned crowd. “Gentlemen,” he said, “I have waited as long as I can. There is something you need to know.”
