Dark Matter

Chapter 9

He wakes up earlier.

He drinks less.

Drives faster.

Reads more.

He's started exercising.

Holds his fork differently.

Laughs more easily.

Texts less.

He takes longer showers, and instead of just rubbing soap on himself, he uses a washcloth now.

He shaves every other day instead of every four days, and at the bathroom sink instead of in the shower.

Puts his shoes on right after getting dressed, not at the front door before going out.

He flosses regularly, and she even saw him plucking his eyebrows three days ago.

He hasn’t worn his favorite sleeping shirt—a faded U2 T-shirt from a concert they saw ten years ago at the United Center—in almost two weeks.

He does the dishes differently—instead of making a wobbly tower in the drying rack, he sets the wet plates and glasses on towels he’s spread on the counter.

He drinks one cup of coffee with breakfast instead of two, and he makes it weaker than before, so weak that she’s been trying to get to the kitchen first each morning to make the coffee herself.

Recently, their family dinner talks have been about ideas, books, and articles Jason is reading, and Charlie’s schoolwork, instead of just talking about what happened that day.

Speaking of Charlie, Jason is also different with their son.

More relaxed, less like a dad.

As if he’s forgotten how to be a father to a teenager.

He’s stopped staying up until two every morning watching Netflix on his iPad.

He never calls her Dani anymore.

He wants her constantly, and it feels like their first time, every time.

He looks at her with a burning intensity that reminds her of how new lovers stare, full of mystery and undiscovered things.

These thoughts, all these little realizations, pile up in her mind as Daniela stands in front of the mirror next to Jason.

It’s morning, and they’re getting ready for their days.

She’s brushing her teeth, he’s brushing his, and when he sees her staring, he grins with toothpaste foam and winks.

She wonders—

Does he have cancer and isn’t telling me?

Is he taking a new antidepressant and isn’t telling me?

Lost his job and isn’t telling me?

A sick, hot feeling erupts in her stomach: is he having an affair with one of his students and she's the one making him feel and act like this new man?

No. None of that feels right.

The thing is, nothing is clearly wrong.

On the surface, they’re actually better. He’s paying her more attention than ever. They haven’t talked and laughed this much since they first got together.

It’s just that he’s…different.

Different in many small ways that might mean nothing and might mean everything.

Jason leans over and spits into the sink.

He turns off the water and steps behind her, puts his hands on her hips, and presses gently against her.

She watches him in the mirror.

Thinking, What secrets are you hiding?

Wanting to say those words.

Those exact words.

But she keeps brushing, because what if the answer costs her this amazing situation?

He says, “I could just watch you do this all day.”

“Brush my teeth?” The words are unclear, the toothbrush still in her mouth.

“Uh-huh.” He kisses the back of her neck, and she shivers, and for a split second it all disappears—the fear, the questions, the doubt.

He says, “Ryan Holder is giving a lecture tonight at six. You want to come with me?”

Daniela leans over, spits, rinses.

“I’d love to, but I have a lesson at five thirty.”

“Then, can I take you to dinner when I get home?”

“I’d love that.”

She turns and kisses him.

Even his kisses feel different now.

Like it's a special thing, every time.

As he starts to pull away, she says, “Hey.”

“Yeah?”

She should ask.

She should talk about all these things she’s noticed.

Just say it all and get it over with.

She desperately wants to know.

She never wants to know.

So she tells herself it's not the right time, plays with his collar, fixes his hair, and sends him off with one last kiss.
