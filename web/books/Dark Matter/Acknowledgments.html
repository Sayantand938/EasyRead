Dark Matter

Acknowledgments

Dark Matter was the toughest job of my career, and I couldn’t have finished it without the help and support of the many generous, talented, and amazing people who supported me while I was writing it.

My agent and friend David Hale Smith worked some real magic this time, and the entire team at Inkwell Management has supported me every step of the way. Thanks to Richard Pine for his wise advice when we needed it most, to Alexis Hurley for her skill and determination in selling my work internationally, and to Nathaniel Jacks, deal-maker extraordinaire.

My film and TV manager, Angela Cheng Caplan, and entertainment attorney, Joel VanderKloot, are exceptional in every way. I’ve been so lucky to have them on my side.

The team at Crown are some of the smartest people I’ve ever worked with. Their passion and dedication to this book have been truly amazing. Thank you Molly Stern, Julian Pavia, Maya Mavjee, David Drake, Dyana Messina, Danielle Crabtree, Sarah Bedingfield, Chris Brand, Cindy Berman, and everyone at Penguin Random House for supporting this book.

And a second thank you to my brilliant editor, Julian Pavia, who pushed me harder than ever before and made this book better on every page.

I couldn’t ask for a better group trying to make Dark Matter the movie, a reality. Huge thanks to Matt Tolmach, Brad Zimmerman, David Manpearl, Ryan Doherty, and Ange Giannetti at Sony. And also to Michael De Luca and Rachel O’Connor, who were wonderful supporters of the book from the start.

Jacque Ben-Zekry edited all my Wayward Pines novels, and even though this wasn’t her book, she gave it the same care and attention as if it were. Dark Matter would be much less without her insight.

The physics and astronomy professor Clifford Johnson, Ph.D., helped me to not seem completely clueless when discussing the basic ideas of quantum mechanics. If I’ve made any mistakes, it’s my fault.

I could not have written Dark Matter without the work of many physicists, astronomers, and cosmologists who have dedicated their lives to finding fundamental truths about our existence. Stephen Hawking, Carl Sagan, Neil deGrasse Tyson, Michio Kaku, Rob Bryanton, and Amanda Gefter were essential in helping me begin to understand all things quantum. In particular, Michio Kaku’s clear comparison of a pond, carp, and hyperspace shaped my understanding of dimensionality and became the basis of Jason2’s explanation of the multiverse to Daniela.

My first readers stuck with me through many versions and gave me helpful feedback. Special thanks to my writing partner and great friend, Chad Hodge; my close friend, Jordan Crouch; my friends, Joe Konrath and Barry Eisler; the wonderful Ann Voss Peterson; and my insightful soul mate Marcus Sakey, who, when I visited Chicago two years ago, helped me see the potential of this book among many failing ideas, and pushed me to write it even though it scared me. Because it scared me. And a shout-out to the bar at the great Longman & Eagle in Logan Square (Chicago), where the shape and idea of Dark Matter came together.

And last but not least, thanks to my family: Rebecca, Aidan, Annslee, and Adeline. For everything. I love you.
