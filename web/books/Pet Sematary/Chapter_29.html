Pet Sematary

Chapter 29

In the morning, Gage’s temperature was almost normal. His cheeks were a little rough, but he was bright and energetic. It seemed that in just a week, his baby talk had turned into many words; he would copy almost anything you said. Ellie wanted him to say “shit.”

“Say shit, Gage,” Ellie said as she ate her oatmeal.

“Shit-Gage,” Gage replied happily while eating his cereal. Louis let him have cereal as long as he used only a little sugar. And, as usual, Gage seemed to be playing with it instead of eating it.

Ellie started to giggle.

“Say farts, Gage,” she said.

“Farz-Gage,” Gage said, smiling through the oatmeal on his face. “Farz-n-shit.”

Ellie and Louis burst out laughing. They couldn't help it.

Rachel was not as amused. “That’s enough bad language for one morning, I think,” she said, giving Louis his eggs.

“Shit-n-farz-n-farz-n-shit,” Gage sang happily, and Ellie covered her mouth to hide her giggles. Rachel’s mouth moved a little, and Louis thought she looked completely better even though she hadn't slept well. Louis figured it was mostly relief. Gage was better and she was home.

“Don’t say that, Gage,” Rachel said.

“Pretty,” Gage said, changing the subject, and then threw up all his cereal into his bowl.

“Oh, gross-OUT!” Ellie screamed and ran from the table.

Louis lost it completely then. He couldn’t help himself. He laughed until he cried and cried until he laughed again. Rachel and Gage stared at him like he had gone mad.

No, Louis could have told them. I’ve been mad, but I think I’m going to be okay now. I really think I am.

He didn’t know if it was done or not, but it felt done; maybe that would be enough.

And for a while, at least, it was.
