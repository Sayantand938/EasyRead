Pet Sematary

Chapter 19

Missy Dandridge watched Gage while Rachel took Winston Churchill to the vet. That night Ellie stayed awake past eleven, whining that she couldn’t sleep without Church and asking for water again and again. Finally Louis refused to give her any more, saying she would wet the bed. This caused a crying fit so intense that Rachel and Louis just stared at each other, surprised.

“She’s scared for Church,” Rachel said. “Let her deal with it, Lou.”

“She can’t keep crying like that for long,” Louis said. “I hope.”

He was right. Ellie’s rough, angry cries turned into sobs and whimpers. Finally, it was quiet. When Louis went to check on her, he found her asleep on the floor, hugging the cat bed that Church hardly ever used.

He took it from her arms, put her back in bed, gently brushed her hair off her sweaty forehead, and kissed her. On a whim, he went into Rachel’s small office, wrote a quick note in big letters on a piece of paper—I WILL BE BACK TOMORROW, LOVE, CHURCH—and pinned it to the cushion on the cat bed. Then he went to his bedroom, looking for Rachel. Rachel was there. They made love and fell asleep holding each other.

Church came home on Friday of Louis’s first full week at work; Ellie fussed over him, used some of her allowance to buy him cat treats, and almost hit Gage for trying to touch him. This made Gage cry harder than any scolding from his parents ever could. Being told off by Ellie was like being told off by God.

Looking at Church made Louis feel sad. It was silly, but he still felt it. Church wasn't feisty anymore. He didn't walk like a tough guy; now he walked slowly and carefully, like someone recovering from an illness. He let Ellie feed him by hand. He didn’t seem to want to go outside, not even to the garage. He had changed. Maybe it was better that he had changed.

Rachel and Ellie didn’t seem to notice.
