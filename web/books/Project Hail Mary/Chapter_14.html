That’s when things started to make sense.

Planets get magnetic fields if the conditions are right. You need a molten-iron core, you need to be in the magnetic field of a star, and you need to be spinning. If all three are true, you get a magnetic field. Earth has one—that’s why compasses work.

Erid has all of those things in overdrive. They are bigger than Earth, with a bigger iron core. You know what strong magnetic fields and thick atmospheres are really good at? Blocking radiation.

All life on Earth evolved to handle radiation. Our DNA has error-correction built in because we’re constantly hit with radiation from the sun and from space. Our magnetic field and atmosphere protect us somewhat, but not completely.

For Erid, it’s complete. Radiation just doesn’t reach the ground. Light doesn’t even reach the ground—that’s why they never evolved eyes. The surface is totally dark. Eridians are extremely sensitive to radiation, and they never even knew it existed.

Rocky and I talk about biology for hours. We're both really interested in how the other's body works. We wouldn't be good scientists if we weren't.

Eridian bodies are, honestly, amazing.

Erid is so close to its star that the amount of energy going into its living world is unbelievable. And Eridians, being at the top of the food chain, have much more energy to use than human bodies do. How much more? They have sacs in their body that just hold ATP—the main way DNA-based life stores energy. Usually, it's kept in cells, but they have so much they had to develop better ways to store it.

We're talking about crazy amounts of energy here. They take oxygen from minerals to get metals. Eridians are basically living smelters.

Humans have hair, fingernails, tooth enamel, and other "dead" stuff on our bodies that do important things. Eridians take that idea to the extreme. Rocky's shell is made of oxidized minerals. His bones are like honeycombs of metallic alloys. His blood is mostly liquid mercury. Even his nerves are inorganic silicates that send light-based signals.

In total, Rocky only has a few kilograms of biological material. Single-celled organisms travel through his blood, building up or fixing the body as needed. They also handle digestion and take care of the brain, which is safely in the center of his shell.

If bees evolved to make hives that could walk, and the queen was as smart as a human, that life-form would be like an Eridian. Except the Eridian’s "bees" are single-celled organisms.

Eridian muscles are inorganic. They're made of porous, sponge-like material sealed in flexible sacs. Most of the body's water is in those sacs. And the air pressure is so high that the 210°C water is still liquid.

They have two separate blood systems: the "ambient" system and the "hot" system. The ambient blood is 210 degrees Celsius. But the hot blood is kept at 305 degrees, which is hot enough to boil water even at Erid's air pressure. Both blood systems have blood vessels that expand or contract around the muscles as needed to set their temperature. Want to expand? Make it hot. Want to contract? Make it cold.

In short: Eridians are steam-powered.

Because of this, the body's circulation acts like a heat sink when muscles cool down. It always needs to be cooled back to normal temperature, which is why there's a radiator. Rocky "breathes" only to pass ammonia outside through capillaries in a radiator-like organ on his carapace. Five slits on top let air in and out, but it never enters his bloodstream.

Eridians don’t "breathe," but they use oxygen. They're more self-contained than humans, with plant-like and animal-like cells inside. Oxygen turns into CO2, CO2 back to oxygen, always balanced. Rocky’s body is a small biosphere needing only energy from food and airflow to release heat.

The hot blood is too hot for any biological material to survive; it boils the water inside. This sterilizes incoming food, killing pathogens.

For worker cells to service the hot-blood system, it must cool to normal levels. When that happens, the Eridian can’t use muscles, which is why they sleep.

They don’t "sleep" like humans; they’re truly paralyzed. The brain, also being maintained, has no conscious function. A sleeping Eridian can’t wake up.

That's why they watch over each other while sleeping. Someone must keep you safe. This likely began in caveman (cave-Eridian?) times and is now a social norm.
