The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 88

Langdon felt drained as he and Sophie rushed through a turnstile at the Temple tube station and hurried into the dirty maze of tunnels and platforms. Guilt tore through him. I brought Leigh into this, and now he's in terrible danger.

Rémy's involvement was a surprise, but it also made sense. Whoever was after the Grail had recruited someone on the inside. They went to Teabing's for the same reason I did. Throughout history, those who knew about the Grail had always attracted both thieves and scholars. The fact that Teabing had been a target all along should have made Langdon feel less guilty about involving him. It didn't. We need to find Leigh and help him. Right away. Langdon followed Sophie to the westbound District and Circle Line platform, where she rushed to a pay phone to call the police, despite Rémy's warning against it. Langdon sat on a dirty bench nearby, feeling regretful.

"The best way to help Leigh," Sophie repeated as she dialed, "is to involve the London authorities immediately. Trust me."

Langdon hadn't agreed at first, but as they planned, Sophie's logic started to make sense. Teabing was safe for now. Even if Rémy and the others knew where the knight's tomb was, they might still need Teabing's help to figure out the orb reference. Langdon worried about what would happen after the Grail map was found. Leigh will become a big problem.

If Langdon had any chance of helping Leigh, or of ever seeing the keystone again, he had to find the tomb first. Unfortunately, Rémy has a big head start. Slowing Rémy down was Sophie's job.

Finding the right tomb was Langdon's.

Sophie would make Rémy and Silas wanted by the London police, forcing them to hide or, even better, catching them. Langdon's plan was less certain—to take the tube to nearby King's College, known for its electronic theology database. The ultimate research tool, Langdon had heard. Instant answers to any religious history question. He wondered what the database would say about "a knight a Pope interred."

He stood up and paced, wishing the train would come soon.

At the pay phone, Sophie finally connected to the London police.

"Snow Hill Division," the dispatcher said. "How can I help you?"

"I'm reporting a kidnapping." Sophie knew to be brief.

"Name please?"

Sophie hesitated. "Agent Sophie Neveu, French Judicial Police." The title worked. "Yes, ma'am. Let me get a detective for you." As the call connected, Sophie wondered if the police would believe her description of Teabing's captors. A man in a tux. Could a suspect be easier to spot? Even if Rémy changed, his partner was an albino monk. Hard to miss. Plus, they had a hostage and couldn't use public transport. She wondered how many Jaguar limos were in London.

Sophie waited for the detective. Come on! She heard clicks and buzzes, like she was being moved to another line.

Fifteen seconds passed.

Finally, a man answered. "Agent Neveu?"

Shocked, Sophie recognized the rough voice.

"Agent Neveu," Bezu Fache demanded. "Where are you?" Sophie was silent. Captain Fache had asked the London police to tell him if Sophie called.

"Listen," Fache said, speaking sharply in French. "I made a mistake tonight. Robert Langdon is innocent. All charges are dropped. But you're both in danger. You need to come in."

Sophie's mouth dropped open. She didn't know what to say. Fache wasn't one to apologize.

"You didn't tell me," Fache went on, "that Jacques Saunière was your grandfather. I'm willing to ignore your disobedience last night because of the emotional stress you're under. But right now, you and Langdon need to get to the closest London police station for safety."

He knows I'm in London? What else does Fache know? Sophie heard what sounded like drilling or machinery in the background. She also heard a strange clicking on the line. "Are you tracking this call, Captain?"

Fache's voice was strong now. "We need to work together, Agent Neveu. We both have a lot at stake. This is about limiting the damage. I made mistakes last night, and if those mistakes lead to the deaths of an American professor and a DCPJ cryptologist, my career will be over. I've been trying to get you to safety for hours."

A warm wind blew through the station as a train came closer with a low rumble. Sophie planned to be on it. Langdon seemed to have the same idea; he was getting ready and walking toward her.

"The man you want is Rémy Legaludec," Sophie said. "He's Teabing's servant. He just kidnapped Teabing inside the Temple Church and—"

"Agent Neveu!" Fache shouted as the train roared into the station. "This isn't something to talk about on a public line. You and Langdon will come in now. For your own safety! That's a direct order!"

Sophie hung up and rushed onto the train with Langdon.
