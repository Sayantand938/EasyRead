The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 75

The chartered turboprop was flying over the sparkling lights of Monaco when Aringarosa hung up on Fache for the second time. He reached for the airsickness bag again but felt too tired to be sick.

Just let it end!

Fache's latest news seemed impossible, but almost nothing made sense tonight. What is happening? Everything had gone completely wrong. What have I gotten Silas into?

What have I gotten myself into!

On unsteady legs, Aringarosa walked to the cockpit. "I need to change where we're going." The pilot looked over his shoulder and laughed. "You're kidding, right?"

"No. I have to get to London right away."

"Father, this is a charter flight, not a taxi."

"I will pay you more, of course. How much? London is only one hour north and we barely have to change direction, so—"

"It's not about money, Father, there are other problems."

"Ten thousand euro. Right now."

The pilot turned, shocked. "How much? What priest has that kind of money?"

Aringarosa went to his black briefcase, opened it, and took out a bearer bond. He gave it to the pilot.

"What's this?" the pilot asked.

"A ten-thousand-euro bearer bond from the Vatican Bank."

The pilot looked unsure.

"It's like cash."

"Only cash is cash," the pilot said, giving the bond back.

Aringarosa felt weak and leaned on the cockpit door. "This is life or death. You have to help me. I need to get to London."

The pilot looked at the bishop's gold ring. "Real diamonds?"

Aringarosa looked at the ring. "I can't give this up." The pilot shrugged, turning to look out the windshield again.

Aringarosa felt sadder. He looked at the ring. The bishop would get everything it stood for anyway. After a long pause, he took the ring off his finger and set it carefully on the dashboard.

Aringarosa slipped out of the cockpit and sat down again. Fifteen seconds later, he felt the pilot turn a bit more to the north.

Even so, Aringarosa's great moment was falling apart.

It had started as a sacred mission. A clever plan. Now, like a house of cards, it was falling apart... and there was no end in sight.
