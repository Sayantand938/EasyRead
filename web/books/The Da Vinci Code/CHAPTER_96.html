The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 96

Silas woke up suddenly.

He didn't know what had woken him or how long he had slept. Was I dreaming? Sitting up on his straw mat, he listened to the quiet breathing of the Opus Dei residence, the stillness broken only by the soft murmurs of someone praying in a room below. These were familiar sounds and should have calmed him.

But he felt a sudden wariness.

Standing in his underwear, Silas walked to the window. Was I followed? The courtyard below was empty, just as he had seen it when he came in. He listened. Silence. So why am I uneasy? Silas had learned long ago to trust his gut. It had kept him alive as a child on the streets of Marseilles, before prison, before Bishop Aringarosa gave him a new life. Looking out the window, he saw the faint outline of a car through the hedge. On the car's roof was a police siren. A floorboard creaked in the hallway. A door latch moved.

Silas reacted without thinking, rushing across the room and stopping just behind the door as it burst open. The first police officer stormed in, swinging his gun at the empty room. Before he knew where Silas was, Silas slammed the door into him, crushing a second officer coming through. As the first officer turned to shoot, Silas dove for his legs. The gun fired, the bullet going over Silas's head, as he hit the officer's shins, knocking his legs out from under him, and sending the man down, his head hitting the floor. The second officer struggled to his feet in the doorway, and Silas kneed him in the groin, then climbed over the body into the hall.

Almost naked, Silas ran down the staircase. He knew he had been betrayed, but by whom? When he reached the foyer, more officers were rushing through the front door. Silas turned and ran deeper into the residence. The women's entrance. Every Opus Dei building has one. Running down narrow hallways, Silas went through a kitchen, past terrified workers, who scattered to avoid the naked albino as he knocked over bowls and silverware, and burst into a dark hallway near the boiler room. He saw the door he was looking for, an exit light shining at the end.

Running full speed through the door into the rain, Silas jumped off the low landing, not seeing the officer coming until it was too late. The two men collided, Silas's broad, naked shoulder hitting the man's chest with crushing force. He drove the officer backward onto the pavement, landing hard on top of him. The officer's gun fell away. Silas could hear men running down the hall shouting. Rolling, he grabbed the gun as the officers appeared. A shot rang out on the stairs, and Silas felt a burning pain below his ribs. Filled with rage, he opened fire at all three officers, their blood spraying.

A dark shadow loomed behind, coming out of nowhere. The angry hands that grabbed his bare shoulders felt like they had the power of the devil himself. The man roared in his ear. SILAS, NO!

Silas spun and fired. Their eyes met. Silas was already screaming in horror as Bishop Aringarosa fell.
