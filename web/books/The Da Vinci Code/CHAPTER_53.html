The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 53

"Monsieur Vernet!" The night manager of the Zurich Depository Bank was relieved to hear the bank president's voice. "Where are you? The police are here, everyone's waiting!"

"I have a small problem," the bank president said, sounding worried. "I need your help now."

You have a big problem, the manager thought. The police had surrounded the bank and were threatening to have the DCPJ captain come with the warrant the bank wanted. "How can I help?"

"Armored truck number three. I need to find it."

Confused, the manager checked his schedule. "It's here, downstairs at the loading dock."

"Actually, no. The truck was stolen by the two people the police are looking for."

"What? How did they get out?"

"I can't explain on the phone, but this could be very bad for the bank."

"What do you need me to do?"

"I'd like you to turn on the truck's emergency tracker." The night manager looked at the LoJack control box across the room. Like many armored cars, each of the bank's trucks had a radio-controlled tracking device that could be turned on from the bank. The manager had only used the emergency system once, after a hijacking, and it had worked perfectly, finding the truck and sending its location to the police automatically. But tonight, the manager felt the president wanted him to be more careful. "Sir, if I turn on the LoJack system, it will immediately tell the police we have a problem." Vernet didn't speak for a few seconds. "Yes, I know. Do it anyway. Truck number three. I'll wait. I need the truck's exact location as soon as you have it."

"Right away, sir."

Thirty seconds later, twenty-five miles away, hidden under the armored truck, a small tracker turned on.
