The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 73

The air traffic controller at Bourget Airfield's night shift was dozing in front of an empty radar screen when the captain of the Judicial Police almost broke down his door.

"Teabing's jet," Bezu Fache shouted, walking into the small tower, "where did it go?" The controller's first reaction was a rambling, weak attempt to protect the privacy of their British client—one of the airfield's most valued customers. It didn't work at all.

"Okay," Fache said, "I am arresting you for allowing a private plane to take off without filing a flight plan." Fache signaled to another officer, who came forward with handcuffs, and the traffic controller felt a wave of fear. He thought about the newspaper stories arguing whether the nation's police captain was a hero or a threat. That question had just been answered.

"Wait!" the controller heard himself beg at the sight of the handcuffs. "I can tell you this much. Sir Leigh Teabing often travels to London for medical treatments. He has a hangar at Biggin Hill Executive Airport in Kent. It's outside of London." Fache waved away the officer with the cuffs. "Is Biggin Hill where he's going tonight?"

"I don't know," the controller said truthfully. "The plane took off on its usual route, and his last radar contact pointed towards the United Kingdom. Biggin Hill is a very good guess."

"Were there others on the plane?"

"I swear, sir, I have no way of knowing that. Our clients can drive right to their hangars and load whatever they want. Who is on board is up to the customs officials at the airport where they land."

Fache checked his watch and looked out at the few jets parked in front of the terminal. "If they're going to Biggin Hill, how long until they land?"

The controller quickly looked through his records. "It's a short flight. His plane could be on the ground by... about six-thirty. In fifteen minutes."

Fache frowned and turned to one of his men. "Get a vehicle up here. I'm going to London. And get me the Kent police. Not British MI5. I want this low-key. Kent police. Tell them I want Teabing's plane to be allowed to land. Then I want it surrounded on the ground. Nobody gets off until I get there."
