The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 57

Standing at the roadblock outside the Depository Bank of Zurich, Lieutenant Collet wondered what was taking Fache so long to get the search warrant. The bankers were clearly hiding something. They said Langdon and Neveu had come earlier and were turned away because they didn't have the right account information.

So why won't they let us inside to look around?

Finally, Collet's cell phone rang. It was the command post at the Louvre. "Do we have a search warrant yet?" Collet asked.

"Forget about the bank, Lieutenant," the agent told him. "We just got a tip. We know exactly where Langdon and Neveu are hiding."

Collet sat down heavily on the hood of his car. "You're kidding me."

"I have an address in the suburbs. Somewhere near Versailles."

"Does Captain Fache know?"

"Not yet. He's busy with an important call."

"I'm on my way. Have him call as soon as he's free." Collet wrote down the address and got in his car. As he drove away from the bank, Collet realized he had forgotten to ask who had told DCPJ where Langdon was. But it didn't matter. Collet had been given a chance to make up for his doubts and earlier mistakes. He was about to make the biggest arrest of his career.

Collet radioed the five cars with him. "No sirens, men. Langdon can't know we're coming."

Forty kilometers away, a black Audi stopped on a country road and parked in the shadows at the edge of a field. Silas got out and looked through the bars of the iron fence around the large area in front of him. He looked up the long, moonlit hill to the château in the distance. The downstairs lights were all on. Strange for this hour, Silas thought, smiling. The Teacher's information was correct. I will not leave without the keystone, he promised himself. I will not fail the bishop and the Teacher.

Checking the thirteen bullets in his Heckler Koch, Silas pushed it through the bars and let it drop onto the mossy ground inside. Then, grabbing the top of the fence, he pulled himself up and over, landing on the other side. Ignoring the sharp pain from his cilice, Silas picked up his gun and started the long walk up the grassy hill.
