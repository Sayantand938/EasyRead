The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 78

Sophie felt a rush of excitement as she held the cryptex and began dialing the letters. An ancient word of wisdom frees this scroll. Langdon and Teabing seemed to hold their breath as they watched.

S... O... F...

"Carefully," Teabing said. "Very carefully."

...I... A.

Sophie lined up the last dial. "Okay," she whispered, looking up at the others. "I'm going to pull it open."

"Remember the vinegar," Langdon whispered, both scared and excited. "Be careful." Sophie knew that if this cryptex was like the ones she had opened as a child, she just needed to hold the cylinder at both ends, right past the dials, and pull, using slow, steady pressure in opposite directions. If the dials were set to the correct password, then one of the ends would slide off, like a lens cap, and she could reach in and take out the rolled papyrus document, which would be wrapped around the vial of vinegar. However, if the password they had entered was wrong, Sophie's pulling force would move a hinged lever inside, which would press down into the space and push on the glass vial, eventually breaking it if she pulled too hard.

Pull gently, she thought.

Teabing and Langdon both moved closer as Sophie put her hands around the ends of the cylinder. In the excitement of figuring out the code word, Sophie had almost forgotten what they expected to find inside. This is the Priory keystone. According to Teabing, it held a map to the Holy Grail, revealing the tomb of Mary Magdalene and the Sangreal treasure... the greatest collection of secret truth.

Now holding the stone tube, Sophie double-checked that all of the letters were lined up correctly with the marker. Then, slowly, she pulled. Nothing happened. She pulled a little harder. Suddenly, the stone slid apart like a smooth telescope. The heavy end piece came off in her hand. Langdon and Teabing almost jumped up. Sophie's heart beat faster as she put the end cap on the table and tilted the cylinder to look inside.

A scroll!

Looking into the hollow of the rolled paper, Sophie saw it was wrapped around something round—the vinegar vial, she thought. But the paper wasn't the usual thin papyrus. It was vellum. That's strange, she thought, vinegar can't dissolve lambskin vellum. She looked again and realized the object wasn't a vial of vinegar after all. It was something else.

"What's wrong?" Teabing asked. "Pull out the scroll." Sophie frowned, grabbed the rolled vellum and the object inside, pulling them out of the container.

"That's not papyrus," Teabing said. "It's too thick."

"I know. It's padding."

"For what? The vinegar vial?"

"No." Sophie unrolled the scroll, showing what was inside. "For this." When Langdon saw the object, his heart sank.

"God help us," Teabing said, slumping. "Your grandfather was cruel." Langdon was amazed. Saunière won't make this easy. On the table was a second cryptex. Smaller. Made of black onyx. It was inside the first one. Saunière loved opposites. Two cryptexes. Everything in pairs. Double meanings. Male female. Black inside white. Langdon felt the symbolism growing. White gives birth to black.

Every man came from woman.

White — female.

Black — male.

Reaching over, Langdon picked up the smaller cryptex. It looked just like the first, but half the size and black. He heard the familiar gurgle. It seemed the vial of vinegar they heard earlier was inside this smaller cryptex.

"Well, Robert," Teabing said, sliding the vellum page to him.

"You'll be glad to hear we're at least flying the right way." Langdon looked at the thick vellum sheet. Another four-line verse was written in fancy handwriting. Again, in iambic pentameter. The verse was puzzling, but Langdon only needed to read the first line to know that Teabing's plan to come to Britain would work. IN LONDON LIES A KNIGHT A POPE INTERRED.

The rest of the poem clearly meant that the password to open the second cryptex could be found by visiting this knight's tomb, somewhere in the city.

Langdon turned to Teabing, excited. "Do you have any idea which knight this poem means?"

Teabing grinned. "Not a clue. But I know exactly which crypt we should search."

At that moment, fifteen miles ahead, six Kent police cars sped down rainy streets toward Biggin Hill Executive Airport.
