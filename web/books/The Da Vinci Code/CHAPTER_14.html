The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 14

"Where's Langdon?" Fache demanded, breathing out the last of a cigarette as he walked back into the command post.

"Still in the men's room, sir." Lieutenant Collet had been expecting the question. Fache grumbled, "Taking his time, I see."

The captain looked at the GPS dot over Collet's shoulder, and Collet could almost see what he was thinking. Fache was trying not to go check on Langdon. Ideally, someone being watched should have as much time and freedom as possible, so they feel safe. Langdon needed to come back on his own. Still, it had been almost ten minutes. Too long.

"Any chance Langdon suspects us?" Fache asked.

Collet shook his head. "We're still seeing small movements inside the men's room, so the GPS dot is clearly still on him. Maybe he feels sick? If he had found the dot, he would have removed it and tried to run."

Fache checked his watch. "Fine."

Still, Fache seemed worried. All evening, Collet had noticed a strange intensity in his captain. Usually calm and cool under pressure, Fache tonight seemed emotionally involved, as if this were somehow a personal matter for him.

Not surprising, Collet thought. Fache really needs this arrest. Lately, the Board of Ministers and the media had become more openly critical of Fache's aggressive methods, his issues with powerful foreign embassies, and his huge overspending on new technologies. Tonight, a high-tech, high-profile arrest of an American would do a lot to silence Fache's critics, helping him keep his job a few more years until he could retire with a good pension. God knows he needs the pension, Collet thought. Fache's love for technology had hurt him both professionally and personally. It was rumored that Fache had invested all his savings in the technology boom a few years back and lost everything. And Fache is a man who wears only the finest shirts.

Tonight, there was still plenty of time. Sophie Neveu's strange interruption, though unfortunate, had been only a small problem. She was gone now, and Fache still had moves to make. He had yet to tell Langdon that his name had been written on the floor by the victim. P.S. Find Robert Langdon. The American's reaction to that little bit of evidence would be very telling.

"Captain?" one of the DCPJ agents called from across the office. "You need to take this." He held out a phone, looking worried.

"Who is it?" Fache asked.

The agent frowned. "It's the head of our Cryptology Department."

"So?"

"It's about Sophie Neveu, sir. Something's wrong."
