The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 93

London's Opus Dei Centre is a simple brick building at 5 Orme Court, looking out over the North Walk in Kensington Gardens. Silas had never been here, but he felt a growing sense of safety as he walked toward the building. Even though it was raining, Rémy had dropped him off nearby to keep the limousine away from the main roads. Silas didn't mind the walk. The rain felt like it was cleaning him.

As Rémy suggested, Silas had wiped down his gun and thrown it into a sewer. He was happy to be rid of it. He felt better. His legs still hurt from being tied up for so long, but Silas had suffered much worse pain. He wondered, though, about Teabing, whom Rémy had left tied up in the back of the limousine. The Briton must be in pain by now.

"What will you do with him?" Silas had asked Rémy as they drove here. Rémy had shrugged. "That's for the Teacher to decide." His tone seemed very final. Now, as Silas neared the Opus Dei building, the rain started coming down harder, soaking his heavy robe and stinging the wounds from the day before. He was ready to leave behind the wrongdoings of the past day and cleanse his soul. He had finished his task.

Walking across a small courtyard to the front door, Silas wasn't surprised to find it unlocked. He opened it and entered the plain foyer. A soft electronic chime rang upstairs as Silas stepped onto the carpet. The bell was common in these buildings, where the residents spent most of the day praying in their rooms. Silas could hear movement on the creaky wood floors above.

A man in a cloak came downstairs. "May I help you?" His eyes were kind and didn't seem to notice Silas's shocking appearance.

"Thank you. My name is Silas. I am an Opus Dei member."

"American?"

Silas nodded. "I'm only in town for the day. Could I rest here?"

"You don't even need to ask. There are two empty rooms on the third floor. Shall I bring you some tea and bread?"

"Thank you." Silas was very hungry.

Silas went upstairs to a simple room with a window. He took off his wet robe and prayed in his underwear. He heard his host come up and leave a tray outside his door. Silas finished praying, ate his food, and went to sleep.

Three floors below, a phone rang. The Opus Dei member who had welcomed Silas answered it.

"This is the London police," the caller said. "We're looking for an albino monk. We heard he might be there. Have you seen him?" The member was surprised. "Yes, he's here. Is something wrong?"

"Is he there now?"

"Yes, upstairs praying. What's happening?"

"Leave him exactly where he is," the officer ordered. "Don't tell anyone. I'm sending officers over immediately."
