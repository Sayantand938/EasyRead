The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 39

Even though the plain room in the brownstone on Rue La Bruyère had seen a lot of suffering, Silas doubted anything could compare to the pain now gripping his pale body. I was tricked. Everything is lost.

Silas had been deceived. The brothers had lied, choosing death rather than revealing their secret. Silas did not have the strength to call the Teacher. Not only had Silas killed the only four people who knew where the keystone was hidden, he had killed a nun inside Saint-Sulpice. She was working against God! She scorned the work of Opus Dei!

A crime of impulse, the woman's death made things much worse. Bishop Aringarosa had made the phone call that got Silas into Saint-Sulpice; what would the abbé think when he found out the nun was dead? Even though Silas had placed her back in her bed, the wound on her head was obvious. Silas had tried to put the broken tiles back in the floor, but that damage was also obvious. They would know someone had been there.

Silas had planned to hide within Opus Dei when his task here was done. Bishop Aringarosa will protect me. Silas could imagine nothing better than a life of meditation and prayer deep inside Opus Dei's headquarters in New York City. He would never go outside again. Everything he needed was within that sanctuary. Nobody will miss me. Unfortunately, Silas knew, a prominent man like Bishop Aringarosa could not disappear so easily. I have endangered the bishop. Silas stared blankly at the floor and thought about taking his own life. After all, it was Aringarosa who gave Silas life in the first place... in that small rectory in Spain, educating him, giving him purpose.

"My friend," Aringarosa had told him, "you were born an albino. Don't let others shame you for this. Don't you understand how special this makes you? Didn't you know that Noah himself was an albino?"

"Noah of the Ark?" Silas had never heard this.

Aringarosa was smiling. "Indeed, Noah of the Ark. An albino. Like you, he had skin as white as an angel. Think about this. Noah saved all of life on the planet. You are destined for great things, Silas. The Lord has freed you for a reason. You have your calling. The Lord needs your help to do His work."

Over time, Silas learned to see himself in a new way. I am pure. White. Beautiful. Like an angel. At the moment, though, in his room at the residence hall, it was his father's disappointed voice that whispered to him from the past.

You are a disaster. A ghost.

Kneeling on the wooden floor, Silas prayed to be forgiven. Then, taking off his robe, he reached again for the whip.
