The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 102

The mist hung low in Kensington Gardens as Silas limped to a hidden spot. Kneeling on the damp grass, he felt warm blood flow from the bullet wound under his ribs. Yet, he stared straight ahead.

The fog made the place look like heaven.

Raising his bloody hands to pray, he watched raindrops touch his fingers, turning them white. As the drops fell harder on his back and shoulders, he felt his body slowly vanish into the mist.

I am a ghost.

A breeze blew past, carrying the wet, earthy smell of new life. With every bit of his broken body, Silas prayed. He prayed to be forgiven. He prayed for mercy. And, most of all, he prayed for his teacher... Bishop Aringarosa... that God would not take him too soon. He has so much work left to do.

The fog swirled around him, and Silas felt so light he thought the mist would carry him away. Closing his eyes, he said a last prayer.

From somewhere in the mist, Manuel Aringarosa's voice whispered to him. Our Lord is a good and merciful God.

Silas's pain finally started to disappear, and he knew the bishop was right.
