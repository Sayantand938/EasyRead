CHAPTER 77

Langdon was impressed. Teabing had just written out the entire twenty-two-letter Hebrew alphabet— alef-beit —from memory. He used Roman letters instead of Hebrew, but still read them perfectly. A B G D H V Z Ch T Y K L M N S O P Tz Q R Sh Th

"Alef, Beit, Gimel, Dalet, Hei, Vav, Zayin, Chet, Tet, Yud, Kaf, Lamed, Mem, Nun, Samech, Ayin, Pei, Tzadik, Kuf, Reish, Shin, and Tav." Teabing wiped his forehead and continued. "In Hebrew, vowel sounds aren't written. So, when we write Baphomet in Hebrew, it loses its three vowels, leaving us—"

"Five letters," Sophie said quickly.

Teabing nodded and wrote again. "Here's Baphomet spelled in Hebrew letters. I'll add the missing vowels to make it clearer.

B a P V o M e Th

"Remember," he added, "Hebrew is usually written the other way, but we can use Atbash this way too. Now, we just make our substitution by writing the alphabet backward under the original."

"There's an easier way," Sophie said, taking the pen. "It works for all ciphers like Atbash. I learned it at the Royal Holloway." Sophie wrote the first half of the alphabet left to right, then the second half right to left underneath. "Cryptanalysts call it the fold-over. Less complicated, and cleaner." A

Teabing looked at her work and chuckled. "You're exactly right. I'm glad those boys at the Holloway are doing their job."

Looking at Sophie's substitution matrix, Langdon felt a thrill that he thought must have been similar to the thrill felt by early scholars when they first used the Atbash Cipher to decode the famous Mystery of Sheshach. For years, religious scholars had been puzzled by biblical references to a city called Sheshach. The city was not on any map or in any other records, yet it was mentioned many times in the Book of Jeremiah—the king of Sheshach, the city of Sheshach, the people of Sheshach. Finally, a scholar used the Atbash Cipher on the word, and the results were amazing. The cipher showed that Sheshach was actually a code word for another very famous city. The decoding was simple.

Sheshach, in Hebrew, was spelled: Sh-Sh-K.

Sh-Sh-K, when put in the substitution matrix, became B-B-L.

B-B-L, in Hebrew, spelled Babel.

The mysterious city of Sheshach was revealed to be the city of Babel, and a lot of biblical study followed. In a few weeks, several more Atbash code words were found in the Old Testament, revealing many hidden meanings that scholars didn't know were there.

"We're getting close," Langdon whispered, unable to hide his excitement.

"Very close, Robert," Teabing said. He looked at Sophie and smiled. "You ready?" She nodded.

"Okay, Baphomet in Hebrew without the vowels is: B-P-V-M-Th. Now we just use your Atbash substitution matrix to change the letters into our five-letter password." Langdon's heart beat fast. B-P-V-M-Th. The sun was shining through the windows now. He looked at Sophie's substitution matrix and slowly began to make the change. B is Sh... P is V... Teabing was grinning like a child at Christmas. "And the Atbash Cipher reveals..." He stopped suddenly. "Good God!" His face turned pale.

Langdon looked up quickly.

"What's wrong?" Sophie asked.

"You won't believe this." Teabing glanced at Sophie. "Especially you."

"What do you mean?" she said.

"This is... brilliant," he whispered. "Absolutely brilliant!" Teabing wrote again on the paper.

"Drumroll, please. Here is your password." He showed them what he had written. Sh-V-P-Y-A

Sophie frowned. "What is it?"

Langdon didn't recognize it either.

Teabing's voice seemed to tremble with awe. "This, my friend, is an actual ancient word of wisdom."

Langdon read the letters again. An ancient word of wisdom frees this scroll. A moment later he understood. He never saw this coming. "An ancient word of wisdom!" Teabing was laughing. "Quite literally!"

Sophie looked at the word and then at the dial. Right away she realized Langdon and Teabing had missed a serious problem. "Hold on! This can't be the password," she argued. "The cryptex doesn't have an Sh on the dial. It uses a standard Roman alphabet."

"Read the word," Langdon urged. "Keep in mind two things. In Hebrew, the symbol for the sound Sh can also be pronounced as S, depending on the accent. Just as the letter P can be pronounced F." SVFYA? she thought, puzzled.

"Genius!" Teabing added. "The letter Vav often stands in for the vowel sound O!" Sophie looked at the letters again, trying to sound them out.

"S...o...f...y...a."

She heard her own voice and couldn't believe what she'd said. "Sophia? It spells Sophia?"

Langdon nodded eagerly. "Yes! Sophia means wisdom in Greek. Your name, Sophie, comes from a 'word of wisdom.' "

Sophie suddenly missed her grandfather a lot. He hid the Priory keystone with my name. Her throat tightened. It seemed so perfect. But as she looked at the five letter dials on the cryptex, she saw there was still a problem. "But wait... Sophia has six letters."

Teabing's smile didn't fade. "Look at the poem again. Your grandfather wrote, 'An ancient word of wisdom.' "

"Yes?"

Teabing winked. "In old Greek, wisdom is spelled S-O-F-I-A."
