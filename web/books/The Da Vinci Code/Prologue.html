The Da Vinci Code

Prologue

Louvre Museum, Paris 10:46 P.M.

Famous curator Jacques Saunière stumbled through the arched entrance of the museum's Grand Gallery. He reached for the closest painting, a Caravaggio. Holding the gold frame, the seventy-six-year-old man pulled the artwork toward him until it came off the wall, and Saunière fell backward under the canvas.

As he expected, a loud iron gate slammed shut nearby, blocking the entrance to the area. The wooden floor vibrated. In the distance, an alarm went off.

The curator paused, catching his breath, assessing the situation. I am still alive. He crawled out from under the canvas and looked around the large space for a place to hide. A voice spoke, very close. "Do not move."

On his hands and knees, the curator froze, turning his head slowly.

Only fifteen feet away, outside the sealed gate, the huge form of his attacker stared through the iron bars. He was wide and tall, with pale skin and thinning white hair. His eyes were pink with dark red pupils. The albino drew a pistol from his coat and aimed it through the bars, directly at the curator. "You shouldn't have run." His accent was hard to place. "Now tell me where it is."

"I already told you," the curator stammered, kneeling defenseless on the floor of the gallery. "I have no idea what you're talking about!"

"You're lying." The man stared at him, perfectly still except for the gleam in his pale eyes.

"You and your group have something that doesn't belong to you."

The curator felt a rush of adrenaline. How could he possibly know this?

"Tonight the true guardians will get it back. Tell me where it's hidden, and you'll live." The man pointed his gun at the curator's head. "Is it a secret you'll die for?" Saunière couldn't breathe.

The man tilted his head, looking down the barrel of his gun.

Saunière held up his hands in defense. "Wait," he said slowly. "I'll tell you what you need to know." The curator spoke his next words carefully. The lie he told was one he had practiced many times... each time hoping he would never have to use it.

When the curator had finished speaking, his attacker smiled with satisfaction. "Yes. That's exactly what the others told me."

Saunière recoiled. The others?

"I found them, too," the huge man sneered. "All three of them. They confirmed what you have just said."

It cannot be! The curator's real identity, along with the identities of his three sénéchaux, was nearly as protected as the ancient secret they guarded. Saunière now understood his sénéchaux, following strict rules, had told the same lie before they died. It was part of the plan.

The attacker aimed his gun again. "When you are gone, I will be the only one who knows the truth."

The truth. In an instant, the curator understood the real horror of what was happening. If I die, the truth will be lost forever. Without thinking, he tried to crawl away to safety.

The gun fired, and the curator felt a burning heat as the bullet hit his stomach. He fell forward... fighting the pain. Slowly, Saunière rolled over and looked back through the bars at his attacker.

The man was now aiming right at Saunière's head.

Saunière closed his eyes, his thoughts a jumble of fear and sadness. The click of an empty gun echoed in the corridor.

The curator's eyes snapped open.

The man looked down at his weapon, seeming almost entertained. He reached for another magazine, but then seemed to change his mind, smirking calmly at Saunière's stomach. "My work here is done." The curator looked down and saw the bullet hole in his white linen shirt. It was surrounded by a small circle of blood a few inches below his breastbone. My stomach. Cruelly, the bullet had missed his heart. As a veteran of la Guerre d'Algérie, the curator had seen this terrible, slow death before. For fifteen minutes, he would live as his stomach acids leaked into his chest, slowly poisoning him.

"Pain is good, sir," the man said.

Then he was gone.

Now alone, Jacques Saunière looked again at the iron gate. He was stuck, and the doors would not open for at least twenty minutes. By the time anyone reached him, he would be dead. Even so, the fear he felt now was worse than the fear of dying. I must pass on the secret.

He stood up, thinking of his three murdered friends. He thought of the generations before them... of the mission they all had. A continuous chain of knowledge.

Suddenly, despite all the safety measures... despite all the backups... Jacques Saunière was the only one left, the only protector of one of the most important secrets ever kept. Shivering, he stood up.

I must find a way....

He was trapped inside the Grand Gallery, and there was only one person he could trust with the secret. Saunière looked up at the walls of his fancy prison. The world's most famous paintings seemed to smile at him like old friends. Wincing in pain, he gathered his thoughts and strength. He knew that the desperate task ahead would take every last second of his life.
