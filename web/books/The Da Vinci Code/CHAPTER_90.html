The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 90

High in the hayloft at Château Villette, Collet stared at the computer monitor in amazement. "This system is listening in on all these places?"

"Yes," the agent said. "It seems data has been gathered for over a year now." Collet read the list again, speechless.

COLBERT SOSTAQUE—Chairman of the Conseil Constitutionnel

JEAN CHAFFÉE—Curator, Musée du Jeu de Paume

EDOUARD DESROCHERS—Senior Archivist, Mitterrand Library

JACQUES SAUNIÈRE—Curator, Musée du Louvre

MICHEL BRETON—Head of DAS (French Intelligence)

The agent pointed to the screen. "Number four is clearly a problem." Collet nodded, stunned. He had noticed it right away. Jacques Saunière was being spied on. He looked at the rest of the list again. How could anyone possibly spy on these important people? "Have you listened to any of the audio files?"

"A few. Here's one of the newest." The agent clicked a few computer keys. The speakers crackled to life. "Capitaine, un agent du Département de Cryptographie est arriv é ." Collet could not believe what he was hearing. "That's me! That's my voice!" He remembered sitting at Saunière's desk and radioing Fache in the Grand Gallery to tell him about Sophie Neveu's arrival. The agent nodded. "A lot of our Louvre investigation tonight would have been heard if someone was interested."

"Did you have someone check for the bug?"

"No need. I know exactly where it is." The agent went to a pile of old notes and blueprints on the worktable. He picked out a page and handed it to Collet. "Look familiar?" Collet was amazed. He was holding a copy of an old diagram, showing a simple machine. He couldn't read the handwritten Italian labels, but he knew what it was. A model for a fully working medieval French knight. The knight sitting on Saunière's desk!

Collet's eyes moved to the edges, where someone had written notes on the copy in red marker. The notes were in French and seemed to be ideas about how to put a listening device into the knight.
