The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 79

Lieutenant Collet took a Perrier from Teabing's fridge and walked back through the drawing room. Instead of going with Fache to London where things were happening, he was now watching over the PTS team that had spread out through Château Villette. So far, the evidence they had found was not useful: one bullet in the floor; a paper with symbols and the words blade and chalice; and a bloody spiked belt that PTS said was connected to the Catholic group Opus Dei, which had been in the news for its recruiting in Paris.

Collet sighed. Good luck figuring this out.

Walking down a fancy hallway, Collet went into the large ballroom study, where the head PTS examiner was dusting for fingerprints. He was a large man in suspenders.

"Anything?" Collet asked, walking in.

The examiner shook his head. "Nothing new. Several sets matching those in the house."

"What about the prints on the cilice belt?"

"Interpol is still working on it. I sent them everything we found."

Collet pointed to two sealed evidence bags on the desk. "And this?" The man shrugged. "Just in case. I bag anything strange."

Collet walked over. Strange?

"This Brit's a weird one," the examiner said. "Take a look at this." He went through the evidence bags and picked one, giving it to Collet.

The photo showed the main entrance of a Gothic cathedral—the usual, set-back archway, getting smaller through many layers to a small doorway.

Collet looked at the photo and turned. "This is strange?"

"Turn it over."

On the back, Collet saw notes written in English, saying a cathedral's long empty nave was a hidden pagan gift to a woman's womb. This was odd. But the note about the cathedral's doorway was what shocked him. "Wait! He thinks a cathedral's entrance is like a woman's..."

The examiner nodded. "With ridges like lips and a cute little cinquefoil clitoris above the doorway." He sighed. "Makes you want to go back to church." Collet picked up the second evidence bag. Through the plastic, he saw a large shiny photo of what looked like an old paper. The title at the top said: Les Dossiers Secrets — Number 4° lm1 249

"What's this?" Collet asked.

"No clue. He's got copies of it everywhere, so I bagged it." Collet looked at the paper.

Prieuré de Sion? Collet wondered.

"Lieutenant?" Another agent poked his head in. "The switchboard has an urgent call for Captain Fache, but they can't find him. Can you take it?"

Collet went back to the kitchen and answered the call.

It was André Vernet.

The banker's polished accent barely hid the stress in his voice. "I thought Captain Fache would call me, but I haven't heard from him."

"The captain is very busy," Collet replied. "Can I help you?"

"I was promised I'd be updated on your progress tonight." For a moment, Collet thought he knew the man's voice, but he couldn't quite remember from where. "Monsieur Vernet, I'm currently in charge of the Paris investigation. My name is Lieutenant Collet."

There was a long pause. "Lieutenant, I have another call. Excuse me. I'll call you later." He hung up.

For a few seconds, Collet held the phone. Then it hit him. I knew I knew that voice! The realization made him gasp.

The armored car driver.

With the fake Rolex.

Collet now understood why the banker had hung up so fast. Vernet had remembered the name Lieutenant Collet—the officer he plainly lied to earlier tonight.

Collet thought about what this strange twist meant. Vernet is involved. He knew right away he should call Fache. But he also knew this lucky chance was his opportunity to stand out.

He called Interpol right away and asked for every bit of information they had on the Depository Bank of Zurich and its president, André Vernet.
