The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 80

"Seat belts, please," Teabing's pilot said as the Hawker 731 went down into a gray, drizzly morning. "We'll be landing in five minutes."

Teabing felt happy to be home when he saw the misty hills of Kent spread out below the plane. England was less than an hour from Paris, but it felt like a different world. This morning, the wet, green of his homeland looked especially welcoming. My time in France is over. I'm returning to England victorious. The keystone has been found. The question, of course, was where the keystone would lead. Somewhere in the United Kingdom. Teabing had no idea where, but he could already taste success. As Langdon and Sophie watched, Teabing got up and went to the other side of the cabin. He slid open a wall panel to show a hidden wall safe. He entered the combination, opened the safe, and took out two passports. "Passports for Rémy and me." Then he took out a thick stack of fifty-pound notes. "And something for you two."

Sophie looked unsure. "A bribe?"

"Creative diplomacy. Private airfields allow certain things. A British customs official will meet us at my hangar and ask to come on the plane. Instead of letting him on, I'll tell him I'm traveling with a French celebrity who doesn't want anyone to know she's in England—you know, for press reasons—and I'll offer the official this generous tip as thanks for his discretion."

Langdon looked amazed. "And the official will accept?"

"Not from just anyone, but these people all know me. I'm not an arms dealer. I was knighted." Teabing smiled. "Being a member has its benefits." Rémy came up the aisle, holding the Heckler Koch pistol. "Sir, what about me?" Teabing looked at his servant. "I want you to stay on board with our guest until we return. We can't very well drag him all over London with us."

Sophie looked worried. "Leigh, I meant what I said about the French police finding your plane before we return."

Teabing laughed. "Yes, imagine their surprise if they come aboard and find Rémy." Sophie was surprised by his casual attitude. "Leigh, you brought a tied-up hostage across international borders. This is serious."

"So are my lawyers." He glared at the monk in the back of the plane. "That animal broke into my home and almost killed me. That's a fact, and Rémy will back me up."

"But you tied him up and flew him to London!" Langdon said.

Teabing held up his right hand like he was taking an oath in court. "Forgive an odd old knight his silly preference for the British court system. I know I should have called the French police, but I'm a snob and don't trust the relaxed French to prosecute properly. This man almost killed me. Yes, I made a quick decision forcing my servant to help me bring him to England, but I was under a lot of stress. My fault. My fault."

Langdon looked shocked. "Coming from you, Leigh, that might just work."

"Sir?" the pilot called back. "The tower just radioed. They've got some kind of maintenance issue near your hangar, and they're asking me to bring the plane straight to the terminal instead."

Teabing had been flying to Biggin Hill for over ten years, and this was a first. "Did they say what the problem is?"

"The controller wasn't specific. Something about a gas leak at the pumping station? They asked me to park in front of the terminal and keep everyone on board until they say it's okay. Safety thing. We're not supposed to get off until we get the all clear from airport people." Teabing was doubtful. That must be a big gas leak. The pumping station was far from his hangar.

Rémy also looked worried. "Sir, this seems very strange." Teabing turned to Sophie and Langdon. "My friends, I have a bad feeling we're about to be met by a welcoming party."

Langdon sighed sadly. "I guess Fache still thinks I'm the one he wants."

"Either that," Sophie said, "or he's too far into this to admit he was wrong." Teabing wasn't listening. No matter what Fache thought, they needed to act fast. Don't forget what matters most. The Grail. We're so close. Below them, the landing gear came down with a thud.

"Leigh," Langdon said, sounding very sorry, "I should turn myself in and handle this legally. Leave you all out of it."

"Good heavens, Robert!" Teabing waved it off. "Do you really think they're going to let the rest of us go? I just transported you illegally. Miss Neveu helped you escape from the Louvre, and we have a man tied up in the back of the plane. Come on! We're all in this together."

"Maybe a different airport?" Sophie said.

Teabing shook his head. "If we pull up now, by the time we get clearance anywhere else, our welcoming party will include army tanks."

Sophie slumped.

Teabing sensed that if they were to have any chance of delaying a confrontation with the British authorities long enough to find the Grail, bold action had to be taken. "Give me a minute," he said, hobbling toward the cockpit.

"What are you doing?" Langdon asked.

"Sales meeting," Teabing said, wondering how much it would cost him to persuade his pilot to perform one very unusual move.
