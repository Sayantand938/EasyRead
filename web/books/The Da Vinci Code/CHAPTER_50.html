The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 50

The plain Fiat sedan leaving Castel Gandolfo wound down through the Alban Hills into the valley below. In the back seat, Bishop Aringarosa smiled, feeling the weight of the bonds in the briefcase on his lap and wondering how long before he and the Teacher could make the exchange.

Twenty million euro.

The sum would buy Aringarosa power worth far more than that.

As his car sped back toward Rome, Aringarosa again wondered why the Teacher had not yet contacted him. Pulling his cell phone from his cassock pocket, he checked the signal. Very weak.

"Cell service is spotty up here," the driver said, glancing at him in the rearview mirror. "In about five minutes, we'll be out of the mountains, and service gets better."

"Thank you." Aringarosa felt a sudden wave of worry. No service in the mountains? Maybe the Teacher had been trying to reach him all this time. Maybe something had gone very wrong. Quickly, Aringarosa checked the phone's voice mail. Nothing. Then again, he realized, the Teacher never would have left a recorded message; he was a man who was extremely careful with his communications. Nobody understood better than the Teacher the dangers of speaking openly in this modern world. Electronic spying had played a big role in how he had gathered his amazing amount of secret knowledge.

For this reason, he takes extra precautions.

Unfortunately, the Teacher's rules for caution included a refusal to give Aringarosa any kind of contact number. I alone will start contact, the Teacher had told him. So keep your phone close. Now that Aringarosa realized his phone might not have been working right, he feared what the Teacher might think if he had been calling repeatedly with no answer. He'll think something is wrong.

Or that I failed to get the bonds.

The bishop started to sweat a little.

Or worse... that I stole the money and ran away!
