The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 27

Seated at Saunière's desk, Lieutenant Collet held the phone to his ear in disbelief. Did I hear Fache right? "A bar of soap? But how could Langdon have known about the GPS dot?"

"Sophie Neveu," Fache replied. "She told him."

"What! Why?"

"Good question, but I just heard a recording that confirms she warned him." Collet was speechless. What was Neveu thinking? Fache had proof that Sophie had messed with a DCPJ operation? Sophie Neveu was not only going to be fired, she was also going to jail.

"But, Captain... where is Langdon now?"

"Have any fire alarms gone off there?"

"No, sir."

"And no one has come out under the Grand Gallery gate?"

"No. We've got a Louvre security officer on the gate. Just as you asked."

"Okay, Langdon must still be inside the Grand Gallery."

"Inside? But what is he doing?"

"Is the Louvre security guard armed?"

"Yes, sir. He's a senior warden."

"Send him in," Fache ordered. "I can't get my men back to the outside for a few minutes, and I don't want Langdon running for an exit." Fache paused. "And you should tell the guard Agent Neveu is probably in there with him."

"Agent Neveu left, I thought."

"Did you actually see her leave?"

"No, sir, but—"

"Well, nobody outside saw her leave either. They only saw her go in." Collet was stunned by Sophie Neveu's boldness. She's still inside the building?

"Handle it," Fache ordered. "I want Langdon and Neveu held at gunpoint by the time I get back."

As the truck drove off, Captain Fache gathered his men. Robert Langdon had been hard to catch tonight, and with Agent Neveu now helping him, he might be even harder to trap than expected.

Fache decided to be careful.

Playing it safe, he ordered half of his men back to the Louvre area. The other half he sent to guard the only place in Paris where Robert Langdon could find safety.
