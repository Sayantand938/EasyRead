The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 59

The male receptionist at the Opus Dei headquarters on Lexington Avenue in New York City was surprised to hear Bishop Aringarosa's voice on the phone. "Good evening, sir."

"Any messages for me?" the bishop asked, sounding unusually worried.

"Yes, sir. I'm glad you called. I couldn't reach you at your apartment. You had an urgent phone message about thirty minutes ago."

"Yes?" He seemed relieved by the news. "Did the caller say who they were?"

"No, sir, just a number." The operator gave him the number.

"Begins with thirty-three? That's France, right?"

"Yes, sir. Paris. The caller said it was crucial that you call him right away."

"Thank you. I've been waiting for that call." Aringarosa quickly hung up. As the receptionist put down the phone, he wondered why Aringarosa's phone sounded so staticky. The bishop's schedule showed him in New York this weekend, yet he sounded far away. The receptionist didn't worry about it. Bishop Aringarosa had been acting oddly the last few months.

My cell phone must not have been working, Aringarosa thought as the Fiat neared the exit for Rome's Ciampino Charter Airport. The Teacher was trying to reach me. Although Aringarosa was concerned about missing the call, he was glad the Teacher felt comfortable calling Opus Dei headquarters directly.

Things must have gone well in Paris tonight.

As Aringarosa started dialing, he felt excited about going to Paris soon. I'll be there before dawn. Aringarosa had a private plane waiting to take him to France. Regular airlines wouldn't work at this hour, especially with what was in his briefcase.

The line started ringing.

A woman answered. "Direction Centrale Police Judidaire." Aringarosa hesitated. This was unexpected. "Yes, I was told to call this number?"

"Qui êtes-vous?" the woman said. "Your name?" Aringarosa wasn't sure if he should say it. The French police?

"Your name, sir?" the woman asked again.

"Bishop Manuel Aringarosa."

"Un moment." There was a click.

After a long wait, another man answered, sounding rough and worried. "Bishop, I'm glad I finally got you. We have a lot to talk about."
