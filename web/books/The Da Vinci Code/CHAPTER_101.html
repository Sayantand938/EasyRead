The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 101

Robert Langdon stood under the high dome of the empty Chapter House and looked into the barrel of Leigh Teabing's gun.

Robert, are you with me, or against me? The Royal Historian's words echoed in Langdon's head.

Langdon knew there was no good answer. If he said yes, he would be betraying Sophie. If he said no, Teabing would have to kill them both. Langdon's years of teaching hadn't given him any skills for dealing with guns, but they had taught him something about answering impossible questions. When a question has no right answer, there's only one honest response. The gray area between yes and no.

Silence.

Looking at the cryptex in his hands, Langdon simply chose to walk away. Without looking up, he stepped backward into the room's wide open space. Neutral ground. He hoped his focus on the cryptex showed Teabing that working together was possible, and that his silence showed Sophie he hadn't left her.

All the while buying time to think.

Langdon suspected that thinking was exactly what Teabing wanted him to do. That's why he gave me the cryptex. So I could feel the weight of my decision. The British historian hoped that holding the Grand Master's cryptex would make Langdon fully understand how important its contents were, letting his academic curiosity take over, forcing him to realize that not unlocking the keystone would mean losing history itself.

With Sophie at gunpoint across the room, Langdon feared that finding the cryptex's hidden password was his only hope of trading for her release. If I can free the map, Teabing will negotiate. Forcing himself to focus on this important task, Langdon moved slowly toward the far windows... letting his mind fill with the many astronomical images on Newton's tomb. You seek the orb that ought be on his tomb.

It speaks of Rosy flesh and seeded womb.

Turning his back to the others, he walked toward the towering windows, searching for any inspiration in their stained-glass mosaics. There was none.

Imagine you are Saunière, he urged, gazing out into College Garden. What would he think is the orb that should be on Newton's tomb? Images of stars, comets, and planets sparkled in the falling rain, but Langdon ignored them. Saunière was not a scientist. He was a man interested in people, art, and history. The sacred feminine... the chalice... the Rose... the exiled Mary Magdalene... the decline of the goddess... the Holy Grail.

Legend had always shown the Grail as something hard to get, always just out of reach, whispering in your ear, tempting you closer and then vanishing. Looking out at the rustling trees of College Garden, Langdon felt her playful presence. The signs were everywhere. Like a teasing shape in the fog, the branches of Britain's oldest apple tree were full of five-petaled blossoms, all shining like Venus. The goddess was in the garden now. She was dancing in the rain, singing old songs, peeking out from behind the bud-filled branches as if to remind Langdon that the fruit of knowledge was growing just beyond his reach.

Across the room, Sir Leigh Teabing watched confidently as Langdon gazed out the window as if hypnotized.

Exactly as I hoped, Teabing thought. He will agree eventually.

For some time, Teabing had thought Langdon might hold the key to the Grail. It was no accident that Teabing started his plan on the same night Langdon was supposed to meet Jacques Saunière. Listening to the curator, Teabing was sure the man's eagerness to meet privately with Langdon could mean only one thing. Langdon's mysterious manuscript has bothered the Priory.

Langdon has found a truth, and Saunière is afraid it will be revealed. Teabing was sure the Grand Master was calling Langdon to silence him.

The Truth has been quiet for too long!

Teabing knew he had to act fast. Silas's attack would do two things. It would stop Saunière from convincing Langdon to keep quiet, and it would make sure that once Teabing had the keystone, Langdon would be in Paris to help if Teabing needed him. Arranging the fatal meeting between Saunière and Silas had been almost too easy. I knew Saunière's biggest fears. Yesterday afternoon, Silas had called the curator, pretending to be a distressed priest. "Monsieur Saunière, please forgive me, I must speak to you right away. I should never break the rules of confession, but I feel I must in this case. I just heard a confession from a man who said he murdered members of your family."

Saunière's response was surprised but cautious. "My family died in an accident. The police report proved it."

"Yes, a car accident," Silas said, setting the trap. "The man I spoke to said he forced their car off the road into a river."

Saunière fell silent.

"Monsieur Saunière, I would never have phoned you directly, but this man made a comment which makes me now fear for your safety." He paused. "The man also mentioned your granddaughter, Sophie."

The mention of Sophie's name was the trigger. The curator reacted instantly. He ordered Silas to come see him immediately in the safest place Saunière knew—his Louvre office. Then he phoned Sophie to warn her she might be in danger. Drinks with Robert Langdon were immediately canceled.

Now, with Langdon separated from Sophie on the other side of the room, Teabing felt he had successfully divided the two companions. Sophie Neveu remained stubborn, but Langdon clearly understood the situation. He was trying to figure out the password. He understands how important it is to find the Grail and free her.

"He won't open it for you," Sophie said coldly. "Even if he can." Teabing glanced at Langdon as he held the gun on Sophie. He was almost sure now he would have to use the weapon. Although the idea bothered him, he knew he would not hesitate if necessary. I have given her every chance to do the right thing. The Grail is more important than any of us.

At that moment, Langdon turned from the window. "The tomb..." he said suddenly, facing them with a small glimmer of hope in his eyes. "I know where to look on Newton's tomb. Yes, I think I can find the password!"

Teabing's heart leaped. "Where, Robert? Tell me!"

Sophie sounded horrified. "Robert, no! You're not going to help him, are you?" Langdon approached confidently, holding the cryptex before him. "No," he said, his eyes hardening as he turned to Leigh. "Not until he lets you go."

Teabing's optimism faded. "We are so close, Robert. Don't you dare start playing games with me!"

"No games," Langdon said. "Let her go. Then I'll take you to Newton's tomb. We'll open the cryptex together."

"I'm not going anywhere," Sophie declared, her eyes narrowing with rage. "That cryptex was given to me by my grandfather. It is not yours to open."

Langdon wheeled, looking fearful. "Sophie, please! You're in danger. I'm trying to help you!"

"How? By revealing the secret my grandfather died trying to protect? He trusted you, Robert. I trusted you!"

Langdon's blue eyes showed panic now, and Teabing could not help but smile to see the two of them working against one another. Langdon's attempts to be gallant were more sad than anything. He's about to reveal one of history's greatest secrets, and he's worrying about a woman who has shown she doesn't deserve to know it.

"Sophie," Langdon pleaded. "Please... you must leave." She shook her head. "Not unless you either hand me the cryptex or smash it on the floor."

"What?" Langdon gasped.

"Robert, my grandfather would rather his secret be lost forever than see it in the hands of his murderer." Sophie's eyes looked as if they would fill with tears, but they didn't. She stared directly back at Teabing. "Shoot me if you have to. I am not leaving my grandfather's legacy in your hands."

Very well. Teabing aimed the weapon.

"No!" Langdon shouted, raising his arm and suspending the cryptex precariously over the hard stone floor. "Leigh, if you even think about it, I will drop this." Teabing laughed. "That bluff worked on Rémy. Not on me. I know you better than that."

"Do you, Leigh?"

Yes, I do. You need to work on your poker face, my friend. It took me a few seconds, but I see now that you are lying. You have no idea where on Newton's tomb the answer is. "Really, Robert? You know where to look on the tomb?"

"I do."

The hesitation in Langdon's eyes was quick, but Leigh noticed it. It was a lie. A desperate, sad trick to save Sophie. Teabing felt deeply disappointed in Robert Langdon. I am a lone knight, surrounded by unworthy people. And I will have to figure out the keystone myself.

Langdon and Neveu were now just a threat to Teabing... and to the Grail. As hard as it would be, he knew he could do what he had to do without guilt. The only problem would be getting Langdon to put down the keystone so Teabing could safely end this game.

"A show of faith," Teabing said, lowering the gun from Sophie. "Put down the keystone, and we'll talk."

Langdon knew his lie hadn't worked.

He could see the dark determination in Teabing's face and knew the moment was here. When I put this down, he will kill us both. Even without looking at Sophie, he could hear her heart begging him in silent desperation. Robert, this man is not worthy of the Grail. Please don't let him have it. No matter what it costs.

Langdon had already made his decision a few minutes ago, while standing alone at the window looking out over College Garden.

Protect Sophie.

Protect the Grail.

Langdon almost yelled in despair. But I don't know how!

The harsh moments of realization brought a clarity he'd never known. The Truth is right in front of you, Robert. He didn't know where the insight came from. The Grail isn't teasing you, it's calling to someone worthy.

Now, bowing like a servant a few feet from Leigh Teabing, Langdon held the cryptex inches above the stone floor.

"Yes, Robert," Teabing whispered, pointing the gun at him. "Put it down." Langdon looked up at the open space of the Chapter House dome. Crouching lower, Langdon looked at Teabing's gun, aimed right at him.

"I'm sorry, Leigh."

In one quick move, Langdon jumped up, swinging his arm up, and throwing the cryptex straight up toward the dome.

Leigh Teabing didn't feel his finger squeeze the trigger, but the gun fired with a loud bang. Langdon was now standing, almost jumping, and the bullet hit the floor near his feet. Part of Teabing wanted to aim and fire again in anger, but the stronger part made him look up at the dome.

The keystone!

Time seemed to stop, turning into a slow dream as Teabing's whole world became the keystone in the air. He watched it rise to the highest point... hanging for a moment... and then falling down, spinning, back toward the stone floor.

All of Teabing's hopes and dreams were crashing down. It can't hit the floor! I can reach it! Teabing's body reacted without thinking. He let go of the gun and threw himself forward, dropping his crutches as he reached out with his soft, manicured hands. Stretching his arms and fingers, he grabbed the keystone in midair.

Falling forward with the keystone held high, Teabing knew he was falling too fast. With nothing to break his fall, his outstretched arms hit first, and the cryptex smashed hard against the floor.

There was a sickening crunch of glass inside.

For a full second, Teabing didn't breathe. Lying there stretched out on the cold floor, staring down the length of his arms at the marble cylinder in his bare hands, he begged the glass vial inside to hold. Then the sharp smell of vinegar filled the air, and Teabing felt the cool liquid flowing out through the dials onto his palm.

Wild panic took hold of him. NO! The vinegar was pouring out, and Teabing imagined the papyrus dissolving inside. Robert, you fool! The secret is lost!

Teabing felt himself sobbing uncontrollably. The Grail is gone. Everything destroyed. Shaking with disbelief at Langdon's actions, Teabing tried to force the cylinder apart, wanting to catch a last glimpse of history before it disappeared forever. To his surprise, as he pulled the ends of the keystone, the cylinder came apart.

He gasped and looked inside. It was empty except for pieces of wet glass. No dissolving papyrus. Teabing rolled over and looked up at Langdon. Sophie stood beside him, pointing the gun down at Teabing.

Confused, Teabing looked back at the keystone and saw it. The dials were no longer random. They spelled a five-letter word: APPLE.

"The orb from which Eve ate," Langdon said calmly, "bringing on God's holy anger. Original sin. The symbol of the fall of the sacred feminine."

Teabing felt the truth hit him with painful clarity. The orb that should be on Newton's tomb could only be the rosy apple that fell from heaven, hit Newton on the head, and inspired his life's work. His labor's fruit! The rosy flesh with a seeded womb!

"Robert," Teabing stuttered, stunned. "You opened it. Where... is the map?" Without a blink, Langdon reached into his tweed coat pocket and carefully pulled out a thin rolled papyrus. Just a few feet from Teabing, Langdon unrolled the scroll and looked at it. After a long pause, a knowing smile appeared on Langdon's face. He knows! Teabing's heart yearned for that knowledge. His life's dream was right there. "Tell me!" Teabing demanded. "Please! Oh God, please! It's not too late!" As heavy footsteps echoed down the hall toward the Chapter House, Langdon quietly rolled the papyrus and put it back in his pocket.

"No!" Teabing shouted, trying to stand but failing.

The doors crashed open, and Bezu Fache stormed in like a bull, his wild eyes searching and finding his target—Leigh Teabing—helpless on the floor. Relieved, Fache put away his gun and turned to Sophie. "Agent Neveu, I'm glad you and Mr. Langdon are safe. You should have come in when I asked."

The British police followed Fache, grabbing the suffering prisoner and handcuffing him.

Sophie seemed shocked to see Fache. "How did you find us?"

Fache pointed to Teabing. "He made the mistake of showing his ID when he entered the abbey. The guards heard a police report about our search for him."

"It's in Langdon's pocket!" Teabing screamed like a crazy person. "The map to the Holy Grail!" As they lifted Teabing and carried him away, he threw his head back and howled. "Robert! Tell me where it's hidden!"

As Teabing passed, Langdon looked him in the eye. "Only those who deserve it find the Grail, Leigh. You taught me that."
