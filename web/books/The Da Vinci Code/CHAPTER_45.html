The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 45

"The police are blocking the street," André Vernet said, walking into the waiting room. "It will be hard to get you out." As he closed the door, Vernet saw the strong plastic case on the belt and stopped. Oh no! They got into Saunière's account?

Sophie and Langdon were at the table, gathered around what looked like a big wooden jewelry box. Sophie quickly closed the lid and looked up. "We did have the account number," she said.

Vernet was stunned. This changed everything. He politely looked away from the box and tried to plan. I have to get them out of the bank! But with the police blocking the street, Vernet could think of only one way. "Mademoiselle Neveu, if I can get you out safely, will you take the item with you or put it back in the vault before you leave?"

Sophie looked at Langdon, then back to Vernet. "We need to take it." Vernet nodded. "Okay. Then, whatever it is, hide it in your jacket as we walk. I don't want anyone else to see it."

As Langdon took off his jacket, Vernet rushed to the belt, closed the empty crate, and typed in a few simple commands. The belt started moving, taking the plastic container back to the vault. Taking the gold key from the stand, he gave it to Sophie.

"This way, please. Hurry."

At the back loading dock, Vernet saw police lights flashing in the underground garage. He frowned. They were likely blocking the ramp. Am I really going to do this? He was sweating now.

Vernet pointed to one of the bank's small armored trucks. Safe Transport was another service from the Depository Bank of Zurich.

"Get in the back," he said, pulling open the heavy rear door and pointing to the shiny steel area. "I'll be right back."

As Sophie and Langdon climbed in, Vernet hurried across the loading dock to the dock overseer's office, let himself in, got the truck keys, and found a driver's uniform jacket and cap. Taking off his suit coat and tie, he started to put on the driver's jacket. Then he reconsidered and put on a shoulder holster under the uniform. On his way out, he grabbed a driver's pistol from the rack, loaded it, and stuck it in the holster, buttoning his uniform over it. Returning to the truck, Vernet pulled the driver's cap down low and looked in at Sophie and Langdon, who were standing inside the empty steel box.

"You'll want this on," Vernet said, reaching inside and flipping a wall switch to turn on the single light on the hold's ceiling. "And you'd better sit down. Not a sound while we go out the gate."

Sophie and Langdon sat down on the metal floor. Langdon held the treasure wrapped in his tweed jacket. Swinging the heavy doors closed, Vernet locked them inside. Then he got behind the wheel and started the engine.

As the armored truck slowly moved toward the top of the ramp, Vernet could already feel sweat under his driver's cap. He saw many more police lights in front than he had expected. As the truck went up the ramp, the inner gate swung open to let him pass. Vernet moved forward and waited for the gate behind him to close before driving ahead and setting off the next sensor. The second gate opened, and the exit was ahead.

Except for the police car blocking the top of the ramp.

Vernet wiped his forehead and drove forward.

A tall officer stepped out and waved him to a stop a few meters from the roadblock. Four patrol cars were parked in front.

Vernet stopped. Pulling his driver's cap down further, he tried to look as rough as his refined upbringing would allow. Without getting out, he opened the door and looked down at the agent, whose face was stern and pale.

"Qu'est-ce qui se passe?" Vernet asked, his voice rough.

"Je suis Jérome Collet," the agent said. "Lieutenant Police Judiciaire." He pointed to the truck's cargo hold. "Qu'est-ce qu'ily a là dedans?"

"Hell if I know," Vernet replied in crude French. "I'm just a driver." Collet looked unimpressed. "We're looking for two criminals." Vernet laughed. "Then you came to the right place. Some of these bastards I drive for have so much money they must be criminals."

The agent held up a passport picture of Robert Langdon. "Was this man in your bank tonight?" Vernet shrugged. "No idea. I'm a dock rat. They don't let us anywhere near the clients. You need to go in and ask the front desk."

"Your bank is demanding a search warrant before we can enter." Vernet put on a disgusted look. "Administrators. Don't even get me started."

"Open your truck, please." Collet motioned toward the cargo hold. Vernet stared at the agent and forced an obnoxious laugh. "Open the truck? You think I have keys?

You think they trust us? You should see the crap wages I get." The agent's head tilted to one side, his skepticism obvious. "You're telling me you don't have keys to your own truck?"

Vernet shook his head. "Not the cargo area. Only the ignition. These trucks get sealed by supervisors on the loading dock. Then the truck sits there while someone drives the cargo keys to the drop-off. Once we get the call that the cargo keys are with the receiver, then I get the okay to drive. Not a second before. I never know what I'm hauling."

"When was this truck sealed?"

"Must have been hours ago. I'm driving all the way up to St. Thurial tonight. Cargo keys are already up there."

The agent made no response, his eyes searching as if trying to read Vernet's mind. A drop of sweat was about to slide down Vernet's nose. "You mind?" he said, wiping his nose with his sleeve and motioning to the police car blocking his way. "I'm on a tight schedule."

"Do all the drivers wear Rolexes?" the agent asked, pointing to Vernet's wrist. Vernet glanced down and saw the shiny band of his ridiculously expensive watch peeking out from beneath his jacket sleeve. Merde. "This piece of junk? Bought it for twenty euro from a Taiwanese street vendor in St. Germain des Prés. I'll sell it to you for forty." The agent paused and finally stepped aside. "No thanks. Have a safe trip." Vernet didn't breathe again until the truck was a good fifty meters down the street. And now he had another problem. His cargo. Where do I take them?
