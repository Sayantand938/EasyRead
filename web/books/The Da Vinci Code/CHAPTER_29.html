The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 29

Inside Saint-Sulpice, Silas carried the heavy iron candle holder from the altar back to the obelisk. The shaft would work well as a battering ram. He looked at the gray marble panel covering the hollow in the floor and knew he couldn't break it without making a lot of noise.

Iron on marble would echo loudly in the high ceilings.

Would the nun hear him? She should be asleep. Still, Silas didn't want to risk it. He looked for something to wrap around the tip of the iron pole, but saw only the altar's linen cloth, which he didn't want to ruin. My cloak, he thought. Knowing he was alone in the church, Silas untied his cloak and took it off. As he did, he felt a sting as the wool stuck to the fresh wounds on his back.

Now naked except for his loincloth, Silas wrapped his cloak around the end of the iron rod. Then, aiming at the center of the floor tile, he hit it hard. A muffled thud. The stone didn't break. He hit it again. Again, a dull thud, but this time with a crack. On the third try, the covering finally shattered, and stone pieces fell into a hollow space below.

A compartment!

Silas quickly pulled the remaining pieces from the opening and looked into the empty space. His heart raced as he knelt down. He raised his pale, bare arm and reached inside. At first, he felt nothing. The compartment floor was bare, smooth stone. Then, reaching deeper under the Rose Line, he touched something! A thick stone tablet. He got his fingers around the edge, gripped it, and gently lifted the tablet out. As he stood and looked at his find, he saw it was a rough stone slab with words carved into it. For a moment, he felt like Moses.

As Silas read the words on the tablet, he was surprised. He had expected the keystone to be a map, or a set of complex instructions, maybe even in code. But the keystone had a very simple inscription.

Job 38:11

A Bible verse? Silas was shocked by the devilish simplicity. Was the secret location of what they sought hidden in a Bible verse? The brotherhood would stop at nothing to make fun of the righteous!

Job. Chapter thirty-eight. Verse eleven.

Silas didn't remember the exact words of verse eleven, but he knew the Book of Job was about a man whose faith in God lasted through many trials. Fitting, he thought, barely able to hide his eagerness.

Looking back, he stared down the shining Rose Line and smiled. There on the main altar, open on a fancy book stand, was a huge leather Bible.

Up in the balcony, Sister Sandrine was shaking. She had almost run away to do what she was told, but then the man below took off his cloak. When she saw his pale skin, she was shocked and confused. His wide, pale back was covered in fresh, bloody cuts. Even from far away, she could see the wounds were new.

This man has been whipped terribly!

She also saw the bloody cilice around his leg, with a dripping wound under it. What kind of God would want someone hurt like this? Sister Sandrine knew she would never understand the ways of Opus Dei. But that didn't matter right now. Opus Dei is looking for the keystone. She didn't know how they knew about it, but she didn't have time to wonder.

The bloody monk was now putting his cloak back on, holding his treasure as he walked toward the altar, toward the Bible.

Holding her breath, Sister Sandrine left the balcony and hurried down the hall to her room. She got on her hands and knees, reached under her wooden bed, and pulled out a sealed envelope she had hidden there years ago.

She ripped it open and found four Paris phone numbers.

Shaking, she began to dial.

Downstairs, Silas put the stone tablet on the altar and eagerly reached for the leather Bible. His long white fingers were sweating as he turned the pages. He flipped through the Old Testament and found the Book of Job. He found chapter thirty-eight. As he ran his finger down the text, he looked forward to the words he was about to read.

They will lead the way!

Finding verse eleven, Silas read the text. It was only seven words. Confused, he read it again, feeling that something had gone terribly wrong. The verse simply read: HITHERTO SHALT THOU COME, BUT NO FURTHER.
