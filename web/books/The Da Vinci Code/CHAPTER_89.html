The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 89

The spotless inside of Teabing's plane was now covered in metal bits and smelled like air and gas. Bezu Fache had sent everyone away and sat alone with his drink and the heavy wooden box found in Teabing's safe.

Running his finger across the rose design, he lifted the fancy lid. Inside, he found a stone tube with dials that had letters on them. The five dials spelled SOFIA. Fache stared at the word for a moment, then lifted the tube and looked at every part of it. Then, pulling on the ends, Fache slid off one of the caps. The tube was empty. Fache put it back in the box and looked out the plane's window at the hangar, thinking about his short talk with Sophie and the information from PTS in Château Villette. The sound of his phone pulled him out of his thoughts.

It was the DCPJ switchboard. The person on the phone apologized. The president of the Zurich bank had called many times, and even though he was told that the captain was in London for work, he kept calling. Reluctantly, Fache told the operator to put the call through.

"Monsieur Vernet," Fache said before the man could speak, "I am sorry I didn't call you sooner. I have been busy. As I promised, your bank's name hasn't been in the news. So, what exactly is your problem?"

Vernet sounded worried as he told Fache how Langdon and Sophie had taken a small wooden box from the bank and then convinced Vernet to help them escape. "Then, when I heard on the radio that they were criminals," Vernet said, "I stopped the truck and demanded the box back, but they attacked me and stole the truck."

"You're worried about a wooden box," Fache said, looking at the rose design on the cover and gently opening the lid to see the white tube. "Can you tell me what was in the box?"

"What's inside doesn't matter," Vernet snapped back. "I am worried about my bank's reputation. We have never had a robbery. Ever. It will ruin us if I can't get this item back for my client."

"You said Agent Neveu and Robert Langdon had a password and a key. Why do you say they stole the box?"

"They murdered people tonight, including Sophie Neveu's grandfather. They obviously got the key and password illegally."

"Mr. Vernet, my men have looked into your background and interests. It's clear you're a man of culture and taste. I imagine you're a man of honor, too. As am I. With that said, I give you my word as head of the Police Judiciaire that your box, and your bank's good name, are completely safe."
