The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 13

For a few seconds, Langdon stared in amazement at the photo of Saunière's postscript. P.S. Find Robert Langdon. He felt like he was losing his balance. Saunière left a postscript with my name on it? He couldn't imagine why, even in his most far-fetched thoughts.

"Now do you understand," Sophie said, her eyes pleading, "why Fache asked you to come here tonight, and why he suspects you most of all?"

The only thing Langdon understood right now was why Fache had looked so pleased when Langdon said Saunière would have named his killer.

Find Robert Langdon.

"Why would Saunière write this?" Langdon asked, his confusion turning to anger.

"Why would I want to kill Jacques Saunière?"

"Fache hasn't found a reason yet, but he's been recording everything you've said tonight, hoping you'll give him one."

Langdon opened his mouth, but still couldn't speak.

"He's wearing a tiny microphone," Sophie explained. "It's connected to a transmitter in his pocket that sends the signal back to the command post."

"This is impossible," Langdon stammered. "I have an alibi. I went straight back to my hotel after my lecture. You can ask the hotel desk."

"Fache already did. His report shows you getting your room key from the front desk at about ten-thirty. Unfortunately, the murder happened closer to eleven. You could have easily left your hotel room without being seen."

"This is crazy! Fache has no proof!"

Sophie's eyes widened, as if to say: No proof? "Mr. Langdon, your name is written on the floor next to the body, and Saunière's date book says you were with him around the time of the murder." She paused. "Fache has more than enough proof to arrest you for questioning."

Langdon suddenly felt he needed a lawyer. "I didn't do this." Sophie sighed. "This isn't American television, Mr. Langdon. In France, the laws protect the police, not criminals. Unfortunately, there's also the media to consider. Jacques Saunière was a very important and well-loved person in Paris, and his murder will be news in the morning. Fache will be under immediate pressure to make a statement, and it looks much better for him to already have a suspect in custody. Whether you're guilty or not, you will definitely be held by DCPJ until they can figure out what really happened."

Langdon felt trapped. "Why are you telling me all this?"

"Because, Mr. Langdon, I believe you are innocent." Sophie looked away for a moment and then back into his eyes. "And also because it's partly my fault that you're in trouble."

"I'm sorry? Is it your fault Saunière is trying to frame me?"

"Saunière wasn't trying to frame you. It was a mistake. That message on the floor was meant for me."

Langdon needed a minute to understand that. "I'm sorry, what?"

That message wasn't for the police. He wrote it for me. I think he was forced to do everything so quickly that he didn't realize how it would look to the police." She paused. "The numbered code is pointless. Saunière wrote it to make sure the investigation involved code experts, making sure that I would know as soon as possible what had happened to him." Langdon felt himself losing touch quickly. Whether Sophie Neveu was crazy was now uncertain, but at least Langdon understood why she was trying to help him. P.S. Find Robert Langdon. She apparently believed the curator had left her a secret note telling her to find Langdon. "But why do you think his message was for you?"

"The Vitruvian Man," she said plainly. "That sketch has always been my favorite Da Vinci piece. Tonight he used it to get my attention."

"Wait. You're saying the curator knew your favorite piece of art?" She nodded. "I'm sorry. This is all out of order. Jacques Saunière and I..."

Sophie's voice broke, and Langdon heard a sudden sadness there, a painful past, hidden just below the surface. Sophie and Jacques Saunière apparently had some kind of special relationship. Langdon studied the beautiful young woman, knowing that older men in France often had young mistresses. Even so, Sophie Neveu as a "kept woman" didn't seem right.

"We had a fight ten years ago," Sophie said, her voice a whisper now. "We've barely spoken since. Tonight, when Crypto got the call that he had been murdered, and I saw the pictures of his body and text on the floor, I realized he was trying to send me a message."

"Because of The Vitruvian Man?"

"Yes. And the letters P.S."

"Post Script?"

She shook her head. "P.S. are my initials."

"But your name is Sophie Neveu."

She looked away. "P.S. is the nickname he used for me when I lived with him." She blushed. "It meant Princesse Sophie"

Langdon didn't reply.

"Silly, I know," she said. "But that was years ago. When I was a little girl."

"You knew him when you were a little girl?"

"Very well," she said, her eyes filling with emotion. "Jacques Saunière was my grandfather."
