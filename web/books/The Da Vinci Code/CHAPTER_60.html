The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 60

Sangreal... Sang Real... San Greal... Royal Blood... Holy Grail.

It was all connected.

The Holy Grail is Mary Magdalene... the mother of Jesus Christ's royal bloodline. Sophie felt another wave of confusion as she stood in the quiet ballroom, staring at Robert Langdon. The more Langdon and Teabing revealed tonight, the more confusing this puzzle became.

"As you can see," Teabing said, walking toward a bookshelf, "Leonardo isn't the only one who has tried to tell the world the truth about the Holy Grail. The royal bloodline of Jesus Christ has been written about in great detail by many historians." He ran his finger down a row of books.

Sophie tilted her head and looked at the titles:

THE TEMPLAR REVELATION:

Secret Guardians of the True Identity of Christ

THE WOMAN WITH THE ALABASTER JAR:

Mary Magdalene and the Holy Grail

THE GODDESS IN THE GOSPELS

Reclaiming the Sacred Feminine

"Here's a very famous book," Teabing said, pulling a worn hardcover from the stack and handing it to her. The cover read:

HOLY BLOOD, HOLY GRAIL

The Acclaimed International Bestseller

Sophie looked up. "An international bestseller? I've never heard of it."

"You were young. It caused quite a stir in the eighties. I think the authors made some questionable assumptions, but their main idea is good, and they finally made the idea of Christ's family line popular."

"How did the Church react to the book?"

"They were angry, of course. But that was expected. After all, the Vatican had tried to hide this secret in the fourth century. That's partly what the Crusades were about: collecting and destroying information. Mary Magdalene was a huge threat to the early Church leaders. Jesus had chosen her to start the Church, and she had proof that the Church's new god had a human family line. To protect itself from Magdalene's power, the Church spread the idea that she was a prostitute and hid proof of Christ's marriage to her. This stopped any claims that Christ had a surviving family and was just a human prophet." Sophie looked at Langdon, who nodded. "Sophie, there's a lot of historical evidence to support this."

"I agree," Teabing said, "the claims are extreme, but you have to understand why the Church would want to cover it up. They couldn't have survived if people knew about a bloodline. A child of Jesus would weaken the important idea that Christ was divine and therefore the Christian Church, which claimed it was the only way for people to reach God and get into heaven."

"The five-petal rose," Sophie said, pointing quickly to the side of one of Teabing's books. The same design as on the rosewood box.

Teabing looked at Langdon and smiled. "She sees well." He turned back to Sophie. "That's the Priory's symbol for the Grail. Mary Magdalene. Because the Church banned her name, she was secretly known by other names—the Chalice, the Holy Grail, and the Rose." He paused. "The Rose links to the five-pointed star of Venus and the Compass Rose. Also, the word rose is the same in English, French, German, and many other languages."

"Rose," Langdon added, "is also an anagram of Eros, the Greek god of love." Sophie looked surprised as Teabing continued.

"The Rose has always been the main symbol of female sexuality. In old goddess cults, the five petals stood for the five stages of female life—birth, periods, motherhood, menopause, and death. Now, the rose's link to women is more visual." He looked at Robert. "Maybe the symbologist can explain?" Robert paused. For too long.

"Good grief!" Teabing sighed. "You Americans are so shy." He looked back at Sophie.

"Robert's struggling to say that the flower looks like female parts, the wonderful opening from which everyone is born. If you've seen paintings by Georgia O'Keeffe, you'll know what I mean."

"The point," Langdon said, gesturing to the bookshelf, "is that all these books support the same story."

"That Jesus was a father." Sophie still seemed unsure.

"Yes," Teabing said. "And Mary Magdalene carried His royal family. The Priory of Sion still worships her as the Goddess, the Holy Grail, the Rose, and the Divine Mother."

Sophie again pictured the ritual in the basement.

"According to the Priory," Teabing continued, "Mary Magdalene was pregnant when Jesus was crucified. To protect Christ's unborn child, she had to escape the Holy Land. With help from Jesus' trusted uncle, Joseph of Arimathea, Mary Magdalene secretly went to France, then called Gaul. She found safety there in the Jewish community. It was in France that she had a daughter. Her name was Sarah."

Sophie looked up. "They know the child's name?"

"Much more than that. Magdalene's and Sarah's lives were carefully recorded by their Jewish protectors. Remember that Magdalene's child was from the line of Jewish kings—David and Solomon. Because of this, the Jews in France saw Magdalene as sacred royalty and honored her as the beginning of the royal line of kings. Many scholars of that time wrote about Mary Magdalene's life in France, including the birth of Sarah and the family tree that followed." Sophie was surprised. "There's a family tree of Jesus Christ?"

"Yes. And it's said to be a key part of the Sangreal documents. A full family history of Christ's early descendants."

"But what good is a written family history of Christ's bloodline?" Sophie asked. "It's not proof. Historians couldn't confirm if it's real."

Teabing chuckled. "No more than they can confirm if the Bible is real."

"Meaning?"

"Meaning that history is always written by the winners. When two cultures fight, the loser is destroyed, and the winner writes the history books—books that praise their own side and put down the defeated enemy. As Napoleon said, 'What is history, but a story we all agree on?'" He smiled. "History is always a one-sided story." Sophie had never thought of it that way.

"The Sangreal documents simply tell the other side of the Christ story. In the end, what you believe is a matter of faith and personal searching, but at least the information has survived. The Sangreal documents have tens of thousands of pages of information. People who saw the Sangreal treasure say it was carried in four huge trunks. Inside those trunks are said to be the Purist Documents —thousands of pages of original documents from before Constantine, written by Jesus' early followers, who respected Him as a human teacher and prophet. Also said to be part of the treasure is the famous "Q" Document —a manuscript that even the Vatican says they think exists. It's said to be a book of Jesus' teachings, maybe written in His own hand."

"Writings by Christ Himself?"

"Of course," Teabing said. "Why wouldn't Jesus have written about His ministry? Most people did back then. Another shocking document thought to be in the treasure is a manuscript called The Magdalene Diaries —Mary Magdalene's own story of her relationship with Christ, His crucifixion, and her time in France."

Sophie paused. "And these four chests of documents were the treasure that the Knights Templar found under Solomon's Temple?"

"Exactly. The documents that made the Knights so strong. The documents that people have been searching for in countless Grail quests throughout history."

"But you said the Holy Grail was Mary Magdalene. If people are searching for documents, why call it a search for the Holy Grail?"

Teabing looked at her, his face gentler. "Because the hiding place of the Holy Grail includes a sarcophagus."

Outside, the wind howled in the trees.

Teabing spoke more softly now. "The quest for the Holy Grail is really the quest to kneel before the bones of Mary Magdalene. A journey to pray at the feet of the rejected one, the lost sacred feminine."

Sophie felt a sudden sense of awe. "The hiding place of the Holy Grail is actually... a tomb?" Teabing's hazel eyes grew watery. "It is. A tomb holding the body of Mary Magdalene and the documents that tell the true story of her life. Really, the quest for the Holy Grail has always been a quest for the Magdalene—the Queen who was wronged, buried with proof that her family should rightfully be in power."

Sophie waited while Teabing composed himself. So much about her grandfather still didn't make sense. "Members of the Priory," she finally said, "all these years have been responsible for protecting the Sangreal documents and the tomb of Mary Magdalene?"

Yes, but the brotherhood had another, more important job—to protect the bloodline itself. Christ's family was always in danger. The early Church feared that if the family grew, the secret of Jesus and Magdalene would come out and challenge the Catholic belief—that of a divine Messiah who did not have relationships with women or have sex." He paused. "However, Christ's line grew quietly in France until the fifth century, when it married into French royalty and created the Merovingian bloodline."

This surprised Sophie. Every student in France learns about the Merovingians. "The Merovingians founded Paris."

"Yes. That's why the Grail legend is so strong in France. Many of the Vatican's Grail searches here were secret missions to kill members of the royal bloodline. Have you heard of King Dagobert?"

Sophie vaguely remembered the name from a horrible story in history class. "Dagobert was a Merovingian king, right? Stabbed in the eye while sleeping?"

"Exactly. The Vatican and Pepin d'Heristal killed him. Late seventh century. After Dagobert's murder, the Merovingian bloodline was almost wiped out. Luckily, Dagobert's son, Sigisbert, secretly escaped and continued the line, which later included Godefroi de Bouillon—founder of the Priory of Sion."

"The same man," Langdon said, "who told the Knights Templar to get the Sangreal documents from under Solomon's Temple to give the Merovingians proof that they were related to Jesus Christ."

Teabing nodded, sighing deeply. "The Priory of Sion has a very important job. They have three duties. They must protect the Sangreal documents. They must protect Mary Magdalene's tomb. And, of course, they must take care of and protect the bloodline of Christ—the few members of the royal Merovingian bloodline who have survived."

The words filled the room, and Sophie felt a strange feeling, as if her bones were shaking with a new truth. Descendants of Jesus who survived. Her grandfather's voice whispered in her ear again. Princess, I must tell you the truth about your family.

She felt a chill.

Royal blood.

She couldn't believe it.

Princess Sophie.

"Sir Leigh?" The servant's voice buzzed from the intercom on the wall, making Sophie jump. "Could you come to the kitchen for a moment?"

Teabing frowned at the bad timing. He walked to the intercom and pressed the button.

"Rémy, as you know, I'm busy with my guests. If we need anything else from the kitchen tonight, we'll get it ourselves. Thank you, and good night."

"A word before I go to bed, sir. If you would."

Teabing grunted and pressed the button. "Make it quick, Rémy."

"It's a house matter, sir, not for guests to hear." Teabing looked surprised. "And it can't wait until morning?"

"No, sir. My question will only take a minute."

Teabing rolled his eyes and looked at Langdon and Sophie. "Sometimes I wonder who's in charge here?" He pressed the button again. "I'll be right there, Rémy. Can I bring you anything?"

"Just freedom from being controlled, sir."

"Rémy, you know your pepper steak is the only reason I haven't fired you."

"You always say that, sir. You always say that."
