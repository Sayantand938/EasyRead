The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 2

One mile away, the huge albino named Silas limped through the front gate of the fancy brownstone house on Rue La Bruyère. The spiked belt he wore around his leg cut into his skin, but his soul felt happy, knowing he was serving the Lord. Pain is good.

His red eyes looked around the lobby as he entered the house. Empty. He climbed the stairs quietly, not wanting to wake any of the others who lived there. His bedroom door was open; locks were not allowed here. He went in, closing the door behind him.

The room was simple—hardwood floors, a plain dresser, a canvas mat in the corner that was his bed. He was a visitor here this week, but for many years he had been lucky enough to have a similar safe place in New York City.

The Lord has given me shelter and a reason to live.

Tonight, Silas felt he had finally started to pay back what he owed. He hurried to the dresser, found the cell phone hidden in the bottom drawer, and made a call.

"Yes?" a man's voice answered.

"Teacher, I have returned."

"Speak," the voice ordered, sounding glad to hear from him.

"All four are gone. The three leaders... and the Head Master himself." There was a short pause, as if for prayer. "Then I assume you have the information?"

"All four agreed. Separately."

"And you believed them?"

"They agreed too much for it to be a coincidence."

An excited breath. "Excellent. I was afraid the brotherhood's reputation for secrecy would win."

"The fear of death is a strong motivator."

"So, my student, tell me what I need to know."

Silas knew the information he had gotten from his victims would be shocking. "Teacher, all four confirmed the existence of the clef de voûte... the legendary keystone." He heard a quick breath over the phone and could feel the Teacher's excitement. "The keystone. Just as we thought."

According to legend, the brotherhood had created a map of stone—a clef de voûte... or keystone —an engraved tablet that showed the final resting place of the brotherhood's greatest secret... information so powerful that protecting it was the reason the brotherhood existed.

"When we have the keystone," the Teacher said, "we will be almost there."

"We are closer than you think. The keystone is here in Paris."

"Paris? Incredible. It is almost too easy."

Silas told the story of what happened earlier that night... how each of his four victims, right before dying, had desperately tried to save their sinful lives by telling their secret. They all told Silas the same thing—that the keystone was cleverly hidden in a specific spot inside one of Paris's old churches—the Eglise de Saint-Sulpice.

"Inside a house of the Lord," the Teacher cried out. "How they make fun of us!"

"As they have for centuries."

The Teacher paused, as if letting the victory of this moment sink in. Finally, he spoke.

"You have done a great service to God. We have waited centuries for this. You must get the stone for me. Right away. Tonight. You know what's at risk."

Silas knew the stakes were huge, but what the Teacher was asking now seemed impossible. "But the church is like a fortress. Especially at night. How will I get in?" With the assured tone of a man with great power, the Teacher explained what to do.

When Silas hung up the phone, his skin felt alive with excitement.

One hour, he told himself, thankful that the Teacher had given him time to do what was needed to cleanse himself before entering a house of God. I must rid my soul of today's sins. The sins he committed today had a sacred purpose. Acts of war against God's enemies had been going on for centuries. Forgiveness was certain.

Even so, Silas knew, forgiveness needed sacrifice.

Pulling his shades, he stripped naked and knelt in the center of his room. Looking down, he examined the spiked cilice belt clamped around his thigh. All true followers of The Way wore this device—a leather strap, studded with sharp metal barbs that cut into the flesh as a constant reminder of Christ's suffering. The pain caused by the device also helped fight against the desires of the flesh.

Although Silas already had worn his cilice today longer than the required two hours, he knew today was no ordinary day. Grasping the buckle, he cinched it one notch tighter, wincing as the barbs dug deeper into his flesh. Exhaling slowly, he savored the cleansing ritual of his pain. Pain is good, Silas whispered, repeating the sacred mantra of Father Josemaría Escrivá—the Teacher of all Teachers. Although Escrivá had died in 1975, his wisdom lived on, his words still whispered by thousands of faithful servants around the globe as they knelt on the floor and performed the sacred practice known as "corporal mortification." Silas turned his attention now to a heavy knotted rope coiled neatly on the floor beside him. The Discipline. The knots were caked with dried blood. Eager for the purifying effects of his own agony, Silas said a quick prayer. Then, gripping one end of the rope, he closed his eyes and swung it hard over his shoulder, feeling the knots slap against his back. He whipped it over his shoulder again, slashing at his flesh. Again and again, he lashed.

Castigo corpus meum.

Finally, he felt the blood begin to flow.
