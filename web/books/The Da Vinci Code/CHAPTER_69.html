The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 69

The Hawker 731's twin Garrett TFE-731 engines roared, lifting the plane with powerful force. Outside the window, Le Bourget Airfield shrank away quickly. I'm running away from the country, Sophie thought, her body pressed back into the leather seat. Until now, she had thought her game with Fache could be justified to the Ministry of Defense. I was trying to protect an innocent man. I was trying to fulfill my grandfather's last wishes. That chance, Sophie knew, was gone. She was leaving the country, without papers, with a wanted man, and transporting a tied-up hostage. If there was ever a "reasonable" course, she had crossed it. Almost at the speed of sound.

Sophie sat with Langdon and Teabing near the front of the cabin—the Fan Jet Executive Elite Design, according to the gold label on the door. Their comfortable chairs were bolted to tracks on the floor and could be moved and locked around a rectangular wooden table. A mini-boardroom. But the fancy surroundings didn't hide the undignified situation in the back of the plane where, in a separate area near the bathroom, Teabing's servant Rémy sat with the gun, reluctantly guarding the bloody monk who lay tied up at his feet like luggage.

"Before we focus on the keystone," Teabing said, "I was wondering if I could say a few words." He sounded uneasy, like a father about to have a serious talk with his children. "My friends, I know I am just a guest on this journey, and I appreciate that. But, as someone who has spent his life searching for the Grail, I feel I must warn you that you are about to start a journey with no turning back, no matter the dangers." He turned to Sophie. "Miss Neveu, your grandfather gave you this cryptex hoping you would keep the secret of the Holy Grail alive."

"Yes."

"You feel you must follow the trail wherever it leads." Sophie nodded, but she felt another reason burning within her. The truth about my family. Even though Langdon said the keystone had nothing to do with her past, Sophie still felt something personal connected to this mystery, as if this cryptex, made by her grandfather, was trying to speak to her and resolve the emptiness that had haunted her.

"Your grandfather and three others died tonight," Teabing continued, "to keep this keystone away from the Church. Opus Dei almost got it tonight. You understand, I hope, that this puts you in a very important position. You have been given a torch. A two-thousand-year-old flame that cannot be allowed to go out. This torch cannot fall into the wrong hands." He paused, looking at the rosewood box. "I know you didn't have a choice, Miss Neveu, but considering what is at stake, you must either fully accept this responsibility... or you must give it to someone else."

"My grandfather gave the cryptex to me. I'm sure he thought I could handle it." Teabing looked hopeful but unsure. "Good. A strong will is needed. But, I wonder if you understand that unlocking the keystone will bring a much bigger challenge."

"How?"

"My dear, imagine you suddenly have a map that shows the location of the Holy Grail. You will have a truth that can change history forever. You will be the keeper of a truth that people have searched for centuries. You will have to decide whether to reveal that truth to the world. The person who does will be admired by many and hated by many. The question is whether you will be strong enough to do it." Sophie paused. "I'm not sure that is my decision." Teabing raised his eyebrows. "No? If not the person with the keystone, then who?"

"The brotherhood that has kept the secret safe for so long."

"The Priory?" Teabing looked doubtful. "But how? The brotherhood was destroyed tonight. Killed off, as you said. Whether they were bugged or had a spy, we'll never know, but someone got to them and found out who their four leaders were. I wouldn't trust anyone who claims to be from the brotherhood now."

"So what do you suggest?" Langdon asked.

"Robert, you know the Priory hasn't protected the truth all these years just to let it sit around forever. They've been waiting for the right time in history to share their secret. A time when the world can handle it."

"And you think that time is now?" Langdon asked.

"Definitely. It's obvious. All the signs are there, and if the Priory wasn't planning to reveal their secret soon, why has the Church attacked now?" Sophie argued, "The monk hasn't told us why he's doing this yet."

"The monk's goal is the Church's goal," Teabing replied, "to destroy the documents that reveal the big lie. The Church almost succeeded tonight, and the Priory is trusting you, Miss Neveu. Saving the Holy Grail means carrying out the Priory's wish to share the truth with the world." Langdon interrupted. "Leigh, asking Sophie to make that decision is a lot to ask of someone who just learned about the Sangreal documents an hour ago." Teabing sighed. "I'm sorry if I'm pressuring you, Miss Neveu. I've always believed these documents should be public, but it's your decision. I just think you should start thinking about what happens if we open the keystone."

"Gentlemen," Sophie said, firmly. "To use your words, 'You don't find the Grail, the Grail finds you.' I'm going to trust that the Grail found me for a reason, and I'll know what to do when the time comes."

They both looked surprised.

"So then," she said, pointing to the rosewood box. "Let's continue."
