CHAPTER HOUSE

Langdon and Sophie jogged under the sign, moving too fast to see the small notice saying some areas were closed for repairs. They came out into a high-walled courtyard with no roof, where morning rain was falling. Above, the wind howled across the opening with a low drone, like someone blowing over a bottle. As they entered the narrow, low walkways around the courtyard, Langdon felt his usual discomfort in tight spaces. These walkways were called cloisters, and Langdon felt uneasy noting that these cloisters lived up to their Latin link to the word claustrophobic.

Focusing on the end of the tunnel, Langdon followed the signs for the Chapter House. The rain was spitting now, and the walkway was cold and damp, with gusts of rain blowing through the single row of pillars that let light into the cloister. Another couple hurried past, trying to escape the worsening weather. The cloisters looked empty now, likely the abbey's least appealing spot in the wind and rain. Forty yards down the east cloister, an archway appeared on their left, leading to another hallway. Although this was the entrance they sought, it was blocked off by a drape and an official-looking sign.

CLOSED FOR RENOVATION

PYX CHAMBER

ST. FAITH'S CHAPEL

The long, empty corridor beyond the swag was scattered with scaffolding and drop cloths. Just past the swag, Langdon could see the entrances to the Pyx Chamber on the right and St. Faith's Chapel on the left. The entrance to the Chapter House, however, was much farther down, at the far end of the hallway. Even from here, he could see its heavy wooden door wide open, with grayish natural light streaming in from the room’s enormous windows overlooking College Garden.

Go through Chapter House, out south exit, to public garden.

"We just left the east cloister," Langdon said, "so the south exit to the garden must be through there and to the right."

Sophie was already stepping over the swag and moving ahead.

As they hurried down the dim corridor, the sounds of wind and rain from the open cloister faded behind them. The Chapter House was a separate structure—a freestanding annex at the end of the hallway to ensure privacy for the Parliament proceedings held there.

"It looks huge," Sophie whispered as they approached.

Langdon had forgotten just how large the room was. Even from outside the entrance, he could see across the vast floor to the breathtaking windows on the far side of the octagon, stretching five stories to a vaulted ceiling. From here, they would have a perfect view of the garden.

Stepping inside, both Langdon and Sophie squinted. After the dark cloisters, the Chapter House felt like a sunroom. They were about ten feet in, scanning the south wall, when they realized the promised door wasn’t there. They were standing in a massive dead end.

The creak of a heavy door behind them made them turn just as it slammed shut with a loud thud, the latch locking into place.

A lone man stood behind it, calm as he aimed a small revolver at them. He was heavyset and leaned on a pair of aluminum crutches.

For a moment, Langdon thought he must be dreaming.

It was Leigh Teabing.
