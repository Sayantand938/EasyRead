The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 66

Collet and his agents kicked open the front door of Sir Leigh Teabing's estate, guns ready. Spreading out, they searched every room on the first floor. They found a bullet hole in the living room floor, signs of a fight, a little blood, a strange, spiky leather belt, and a roll of duct tape that had been partly used. The whole floor seemed empty. Just as Collet was about to split up his men to check the basement and the land behind the house, he heard voices from upstairs.

"They're upstairs!"

Charging up the wide stairs, Collet and his men went from room to room in the huge house, making sure dark bedrooms and hallways were safe as they got closer to the voices. The sound seemed to be coming from the last bedroom at the end of a very long hallway. The agents crept down the hall, blocking off other ways out.

As they got close to the last bedroom, Collet saw the door was wide open. The voices had stopped without warning, replaced by a strange rumbling, like an engine. Gun raised, Collet gave the signal. Silently reaching around the door frame, he flipped on the light switch. He spun into the room with men right behind him, yelling and pointing his gun at... nothing.

An empty guest bedroom. Spotless.

The rumbling sound of a car engine came from a black electronic panel on the wall next to the bed. Collet had seen these in other parts of the house. It was some kind of intercom. He hurried over to it. The panel had about a dozen buttons with labels:

STUDY... KITCHEN... LAUNDRY... CELLAR...

So where the hell do I hear a car?

MASTER BEDROOM... SUN ROOM... BARN... LIBRARY...

Barn! Collet was downstairs in seconds, running to the back door, grabbing an agent on the way. They crossed the back lawn and arrived, out of breath, at the front of a weathered gray barn. Even before entering, Collet heard the fading sounds of a car engine. He drew his weapon, rushed in, and flicked on the lights.

The right side of the barn was a simple workshop—lawnmowers, car tools, gardening supplies. A familiar intercom panel hung on the nearby wall. One of its buttons was pressed down, transmitting.

GUEST BEDROOM II.

Collet turned, anger rising. They lured us upstairs with the intercom! Searching the other side of the barn, he found a long row of horse stalls. No horses. Apparently the owner preferred a different kind of horsepower; the stalls had been turned into an impressive car parking area. The collection was amazing—a black Ferrari, a perfect Rolls-Royce, an old Astin Martin sports car, a vintage Porsche 356.

The last stall was empty.

Collet ran over and saw oil stains on the stall floor. They can't leave the area. The driveway and gate were blocked by two patrol cars to prevent this.

"Sir?" The agent pointed down the stalls.

The barn's back slider was wide open, leading to a dark, muddy slope of rough fields stretching into the night behind the barn. Collet ran to the door, trying to see into the darkness. He could only make out the faint shadow of a forest in the distance. No headlights. The wooded valley was probably full of unmapped fire roads and hunting trails, but Collet was sure they wouldn't make it to the woods. "Get some men spread out down there. They're probably stuck nearby. These fancy sports cars can't handle that kind of ground."

"Um, sir?" The agent pointed to a pegboard with several sets of keys. The labels above the keys had familiar names.

DAIMLER... ROLLS-ROYCE... ASTIN MARTIN... PORSCHE...

The last hook was empty.

When Collet read the label above the empty hook, he knew he was in trouble.
