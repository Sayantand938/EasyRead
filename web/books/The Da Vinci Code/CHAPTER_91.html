The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 91

Silas sat in the passenger seat of the parked Jaguar limousine near the Temple Church. His hands felt a little sweaty on the keystone as he waited for Rémy to finish tying up and gagging Teabing in the back with the rope they had found in the trunk.

Finally, Rémy got out of the back of the limo, walked around, and slid into the driver's seat next to Silas.

"Safe?" Silas asked.

Rémy chuckled, shaking off the rain and looking over his shoulder through the open window at the slumped shape of Leigh Teabing, who was hard to see in the shadows in the back. "He's not going anywhere."

Silas could hear Teabing's quiet cries and realized Rémy had used some of the old duct tape to gag him.

"Shut your mouth!" Rémy shouted over his shoulder at Teabing. Reaching to a control panel on the fancy dashboard, Rémy pressed a button. A dark window rose behind them, closing off the back. Teabing disappeared, and his voice was silenced. Rémy glanced at Silas. "I've been listening to his annoying whining long enough."

Minutes later, as the Jaguar stretch limo drove through the streets, Silas's cell phone rang. The Teacher. He answered eagerly. "Hello?"

"Silas," the Teacher's familiar French accent said, "I'm glad to hear your voice. This means you are safe."

Silas was also comforted to hear the Teacher. It had been hours, and the plan had gone very wrong. Now, at last, it seemed to be going right again. "I have the keystone."

"This is wonderful news," the Teacher told him. "Is Rémy with you?" Silas was surprised to hear the Teacher say Rémy's name. "Yes. Rémy set me free."

"As I told him to do. I'm just sorry you were held captive for so long."

"Physical discomfort doesn't matter. The important thing is that we have the keystone."

"Yes. I need it brought to me right away. Time is crucial." Silas was excited to finally meet the Teacher in person. "Yes, sir, I would be honored."

"Silas, I want Rémy to bring it to me."

Rémy? Silas was crushed. After all he had done for the Teacher, he thought he would be the one to give him the prize. Does the Teacher like Rémy better?

"I can tell you're disappointed," the Teacher said, "which means you don't understand what I'm planning." He lowered his voice. "Believe me, I'd much rather get the keystone from you—a man of God instead of a criminal—but Rémy needs to be taken care of. He didn't follow my orders and made a big mistake that has put our whole mission in danger." Silas felt a chill and looked at Rémy. Kidnapping Teabing wasn't part of the plan, and figuring out what to do with him was a new issue.

"You and I are men of God," the Teacher whispered. "We can't let anything stop us from our goal." He paused ominously. "That's why I'm asking Rémy to bring me the keystone. Do you understand?"

Silas heard anger in the Teacher's voice and was surprised he wasn't more understanding. He had to show his face, Silas thought. Rémy did what he had to do. He saved the keystone. "I understand," Silas said.

"Good. For your own safety, get off the street immediately. The police will be looking for the limousine soon, and I don't want you caught. Opus Dei has a place to stay in London, right?"

"Of course."

"And they want you there?"

"Like a brother."

"Then go there and stay hidden. I will call you as soon as I have the keystone and have taken care of my problem."

"You are in London?"

"Do as I say, and everything will be fine."

"Yes, sir."

The Teacher sighed, as if what he had to do now was deeply sad. "It's time I speak to Rémy."

Silas handed Rémy the phone, sensing it might be the last call Rémy Legaludec ever took.

As Rémy took the phone, he knew this poor, twisted monk had no idea what was going to happen to him now that he had done what he was needed for.

The Teacher used you, Silas.

And your bishop is a pawn.

Rémy still wondered how the Teacher could be so convincing. Bishop Aringarosa had believed everything, blinded by his own need to believe. Aringarosa wanted to believe too much. Rémy didn't really like the Teacher, but he was proud to have earned his trust and helped him so much. I've earned my reward.

"Listen closely," the Teacher said. "Take Silas to the Opus Dei dorm and drop him off a few streets away. Then drive to St. James's Park, next to Parliament and Big Ben. Park the limousine on Horse Guards Parade. We'll talk there."

Then, the line went dead.
