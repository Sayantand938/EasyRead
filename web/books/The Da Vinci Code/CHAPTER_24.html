The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 24

Silas looked up at the Saint-Sulpice obelisk, taking in the tall marble pillar. He felt excited and tense. He looked around the church again to be sure he was alone. Then he knelt at the base of the pillar, not to worship, but because he had to. The keystone is hidden under the Rose Line.

At the base of the Sulpice obelisk.

All the brothers had agreed.

On his knees, Silas felt the stone floor. He saw no cracks or marks to show a tile that could be moved, so he started tapping lightly with his knuckles on the floor. Following the brass line closer to the obelisk, he knocked on each tile next to the brass line. Finally, one of them sounded odd.

There's an empty space under the floor!

Silas smiled. His victims had told the truth.

He stood up and looked around for something to break the floor tile.

High above Silas, in the balcony, Sister Sandrine held back a gasp. Her worst fears had come true. This visitor was not who he appeared to be. The mysterious Opus Dei monk had come to Saint-Sulpice for another reason. A secret reason.

You are not the only one with secrets, she thought.

Sister Sandrine Bieil was more than just the keeper of this church. She was a guard. And tonight, the old wheels had started turning. The arrival of this stranger at the foot of the obelisk was a signal from the brotherhood.

It was a quiet cry for help.
