The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 86

Like a ghost, Silas moved silently behind his target. Sophie Neveu felt him too late. Before she could turn, Silas pressed the gun against her back and wrapped a strong arm around her chest, pulling her against his large body. She yelled in surprise. Teabing and Langdon both turned, looking shocked and scared.

"What...?" Teabing gasped. "What did you do to Rémy!"

"Your only worry," Silas said calmly, "is that I leave here with the keystone." This recovery mission, as Rémy had called it, was to be clean and simple: Go into the church, take the keystone, and leave; no killing, no fighting.

Holding Sophie tightly, Silas moved his hand from her chest, down to her waist, slipping it inside her sweater pockets, searching. He could smell the soft scent of her hair through his own breath that smelled of alcohol. "Where is it?" he whispered. The keystone was in her sweater pocket before. So where is it now?

"It's over here," Langdon's deep voice echoed from across the room. Silas turned to see Langdon holding the black cryptex in front of him, waving it like a matador teasing a bull.

"Put it down," Silas demanded.

"Let Sophie and Leigh leave the church," Langdon replied. "You and I can deal with this." Silas pushed Sophie away from him and aimed the gun at Langdon, moving toward him.

"Not a step closer," Langdon said. "Not until they leave the building."

"You can't tell me what to do."

"I disagree." Langdon raised the cryptex high over his head. "I will smash this on the floor and break the vial inside if I have to."

Although Silas sneered at the threat, he felt a flash of fear. This was unexpected. He aimed the gun at Langdon's head, his voice as steady as his hand. "You would never break the keystone. You want to find the Grail as much as I do."

"You're wrong. You want it more. You've proven you're willing to kill for it."

Forty feet away, watching from the annex pews near the archway, Rémy Legaludec felt alarm rising. The plan had not gone as expected, and even from there, he could see Silas was unsure what to do. The Teacher had ordered Rémy to forbid Silas from firing his gun.

"Let them go," Langdon demanded again, holding the cryptex high and staring into Silas's gun.

The monk's red eyes filled with anger and frustration, and Rémy feared Silas might shoot Langdon while he was holding the cryptex. The cryptex cannot fall!

The cryptex was Rémy's ticket to freedom and wealth. A little over a year ago, he was just a fifty-five-year-old servant living in Château Villette, catering to the whims of the awful cripple Sir Leigh Teabing. Then he was approached with an amazing offer. Rémy's connection to Sir Leigh Teabing—the world's leading Grail historian—was going to bring Rémy everything he had ever wanted. Since then, every moment in Château Villette had led him to this moment. I am so close, Rémy thought, gazing into the Temple Church and the keystone in Robert Langdon's hand. If Langdon dropped it, all would be lost.

Am I willing to show my face? The Teacher had strictly forbidden it. Rémy was the only one who knew the Teacher's identity.

"Are you sure you want Silas to do this?" Rémy had asked the Teacher less than half an hour ago, when he got orders to steal the keystone. "I am capable myself." The Teacher was firm. "Silas served us well with the four Priory members. He will get the keystone. You must remain hidden. If others see you, they will need to be killed, and there has been enough killing already. Do not show your face."

My face will change, Rémy thought. With what you've promised to pay me, I will become a completely new man. Surgery could even change his fingerprints, the Teacher had said. Soon he would be free—another unrecognizable, beautiful face soaking up the sun on the beach.

"Understood," Rémy said. "I will help Silas from the shadows."

"Just so you know, Rémy," the Teacher had told him, "the tomb isn't in the Temple Church. Don't worry. They're looking in the wrong place." Rémy was shocked. "And you know where the tomb is?"

"Of course. I'll tell you later. For now, you must act fast. If the others figure out where the tomb really is and leave the church before you get the cryptex, we could lose the Grail forever."

Rémy didn't care about the Grail, except that the Teacher wouldn't pay him until it was found. Rémy felt excited every time he thought about the money he'd soon have. One third of twenty million euro. Enough to disappear forever. Rémy had imagined the beach towns on the Côte d'Azur, where he planned to spend his life in the sun and be waited on for a change.

But now, here in the Temple Church, with Langdon threatening to break the keystone, Rémy's future was in danger. He couldn't stand the thought of almost getting there and then losing everything, so Rémy decided to act. The gun in his hand was a small, hidden Jframe Medusa, but it would be deadly enough up close. Stepping out of the shadows, Rémy walked into the circular room and pointed the gun right at Teabing's head. "Old man, I've been waiting a long time for this."

Sir Leigh Teabing's heart almost stopped when he saw Rémy pointing a gun at him. What is he doing!

Teabing recognized the small Medusa revolver as his own, the one he kept locked in the limousine's glove box for safety.

"Rémy?" Teabing stammered in shock. "What's going on?" Langdon and Sophie looked just as stunned.

Rémy moved behind Teabing and pressed the gun into his back, high on the left, right behind his heart.

Teabing felt his muscles tighten with fear. "Rémy, I don't—"

"I'll make it easy," Rémy snapped, watching Langdon over Teabing's shoulder. "Put down the keystone, or I shoot."

Langdon seemed frozen for a moment. "The keystone is useless to you," he stuttered. "You can't open it."

"Arrogant fools," Rémy scoffed. "Haven't you noticed I've been listening tonight as you talked about these poems? Everything I heard, I've told others. Others who know more than you. You're not even looking in the right place. The tomb you're looking for is somewhere else!"

Teabing felt panicked. What is he saying!

"Why do you want the Grail?" Langdon asked. "To destroy it? Before the End of Days?" Rémy called to the monk. "Silas, take the keystone from Mr. Langdon." As the monk moved forward, Langdon stepped back, holding the keystone high, ready to throw it to the floor.

"I'd rather break it," Langdon said, "than see it in the wrong hands." Teabing now felt a wave of horror. He could see his life's work disappearing before his eyes. All his dreams about to be ruined.

"Robert, no!" Teabing cried out. "Don't! That's the Grail you're holding! Rémy would never shoot me. We've known each other for ten—"

Rémy aimed at the ceiling and fired the Medusa. The blast was huge for such a small weapon, the gunshot sounding like thunder inside the stone room.

Everyone froze.

"I am not joking," Rémy said. "The next one is in his back. Give the keystone to Silas." Langdon slowly held out the cryptex. Silas stepped forward and took it, his red eyes shining with the joy of revenge. Putting the keystone in the pocket of his robe, Silas stepped back, still holding Langdon and Sophie at gunpoint.

Teabing felt Rémy's arm tighten around his neck as the servant started to leave, pulling Teabing with him, the gun still in his back.

"Let him go," Langdon demanded.

"We're taking Mr. Teabing for a drive," Rémy said, still moving backward. "If you call the police, he will die. If you try to stop us, he will die. Understand?"

"Take me," Langdon demanded, his voice breaking. "Let Leigh go." Rémy laughed. "I don't think so. He and I have such a good history. Besides, he might still be useful."

Silas was also backing up now, holding Langdon and Sophie at gunpoint while Rémy pulled Leigh toward the exit, his crutches scraping behind him.

Sophie's voice was steady. "Who are you working for?"

The question made Rémy smirk as he left. "You'd be surprised, Mademoiselle Neveu."
