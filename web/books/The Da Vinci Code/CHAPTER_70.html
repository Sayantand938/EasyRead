The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 70

Standing in the drawing room of Château Villette, Lieutenant Collet watched the dying fire and felt sad. Captain Fache had arrived moments earlier and was now in the next room, yelling into the phone, trying to organize the failed search for the missing Range Rover. It could be anywhere by now, Collet thought.

Having disobeyed Fache's direct orders and lost Langdon again, Collet was thankful that the forensics team had found a bullet hole in the floor, which at least proved Collet's story that a shot had been fired. Still, Fache was in a bad mood, and Collet knew there would be serious consequences later.

Sadly, the clues they were finding here didn't seem to explain anything about what was happening or who was involved. The black Audi outside had been rented with a fake name and fake credit card numbers, and the fingerprints in the car didn't match anything in Interpol's records. Another agent rushed into the living room, looking urgent. "Where's Captain Fache?" Collet barely looked up from the burning embers. "He's on the phone."

"I'm off the phone," Fache snapped, walking into the room. "What is it?" The second agent said, "Sir, Central just heard from André Vernet at the Depository Bank of Zurich. He wants to talk to you alone. He's changing his story."

"Oh?" Fache said.

Now Collet looked up.

"Vernet is admitting that Langdon and Neveu were inside his bank tonight."

"We already knew that," Fache said. "Why did Vernet lie about it?"

"He said he'll only talk to you, but he's agreed to fully cooperate."

"In exchange for what?"

"For keeping his bank's name out of the news and helping him get back some stolen items. It seems Langdon and Neveu stole something from Saunière's account."

"What?" Collet exclaimed. "How?"

Fache didn't react, his eyes fixed on the second agent. "What did they steal?"

"Vernet didn't say, but he seems ready to do anything to get it back." Collet tried to picture how this could have happened. Maybe Langdon and Neveu had threatened a bank worker? Maybe they forced Vernet to open Saunière's account and help them escape in the armored truck. As possible as it seemed, Collet struggled to believe Sophie Neveu could be part of something like that.

From the kitchen, another agent called out to Fache. "Captain? I'm checking Mr. Teabing's speed dial numbers and I'm on the phone with Le Bourget Airfield. I have some bad news."

Thirty seconds later, Fache was gathering his things and getting ready to leave Château Villette. He had just found out that Teabing kept a private jet at Le Bourget Airfield and that the plane had taken off about thirty minutes ago.

The Bourget representative on the phone claimed not to know who was on the plane or where it was going. The takeoff wasn't planned, and no flight information was recorded. Very illegal, even for a small airfield. Fache was sure he could get the answers he needed by putting enough pressure on them.

"Lieutenant Collet," Fache ordered, heading for the door. "I have to leave you in charge of the PTS investigation here. Try to get something right for once."
