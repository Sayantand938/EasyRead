The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 31

"They're dead!" Sister Sandrine stammered into the phone at her home. She was leaving a message on a machine. "Please answer! They're all dead!" The first three numbers on the list had brought awful news—a distraught widow, a detective at a murder scene, and a sad priest comforting a grieving family. All three contacts were dead. And now, as she called the fourth and last number—the number she was only to call if the first three didn't answer—she got a machine. The message just asked the caller to leave a message.

"The floor panel is broken!" she begged as she left the message. "The other three are dead!" Sister Sandrine didn't know who the four men were, but the private numbers hidden under her bed were only for one reason.

If that floor panel is ever broken, the messenger had told her, it means the highest level has been broken into. One of us is in danger and has had to lie. Call the numbers. Warn the others. Don't fail us.

It was a silent alarm. Simple and reliable. The plan had amazed her when she first heard it. If one brother's identity was discovered, he could lie to warn the others. But tonight, it seemed more than one had been discovered.

"Please answer," she whispered in fear. "Where are you?"

"Hang up the phone," a deep voice said from the doorway.

Turning in fear, she saw the large monk. He was holding the heavy iron candle holder. Shaking, she put the phone back.

"They are dead," the monk said. "All four of them. And they have tricked me. Tell me where the keystone is."

"I don't know!" Sister Sandrine said truthfully. "That secret is kept by others." Others who are dead!

The man stepped forward, his white fists tight on the iron stand. "You are a sister of the Church, yet you serve them?"

"Jesus had only one real message," Sister Sandrine said, standing firm. "I don't see that message in Opus Dei."

A sudden burst of anger flashed in the monk's eyes. He attacked, swinging the candle stand like a club. As Sister Sandrine fell, her last feeling was a deep sense of dread.

All four are dead.

The valuable truth is lost forever.
