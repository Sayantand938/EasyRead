The Da Vinci Code

CHAPTER 98

Langdon and Sophie walked slowly down the north side, staying in the shadows behind the large pillars that separated it from the main area. Although they were more than halfway down, they still couldn't see Newton's tomb clearly. The coffin was set back in a space, hidden from this side view.

"At least there's no one over there," Sophie whispered.

Langdon nodded, relieved. The whole area of the main hall near Newton's tomb was empty. "I'll go over," he whispered. "You should stay hidden in case someone—" Sophie had already left the shadows and was walking across the open floor.

"—is watching," Langdon sighed, hurrying to catch up.

Walking across the large main hall at an angle, Langdon and Sophie were silent as the detailed tomb slowly came into view... a black marble coffin... a statue of Newton lying down... two winged boys... a huge pyramid... and... a very large orb.

"Did you know about that?" Sophie asked, sounding surprised.

Langdon shook his head, also surprised.

"Those look like constellations carved on it," Sophie said.

As they got closer to the space, Langdon felt uneasy. Newton's tomb was covered with orbs—stars, comets, planets. You seek the orb that ought be on his tomb? It might be like trying to find a single blade of grass on a golf course.

"Stars and planets," Sophie said, looking concerned. "And a lot of them." Langdon frowned. The only connection he could think of between planets and the Grail was the pentacle of Venus, and he had already tried the password "Venus" on the way to the Temple Church.

Sophie went straight to the sarcophagus, but Langdon stayed back a few feet, watching the abbey around them.

"Divinity," Sophie said, tilting her head and reading the titles of the books Newton was leaning on. "Chronology. Opticks. Philosophiae Naturalis Principia Mathematica?" She turned to him. "Does any of this help?"

Langdon stepped closer, thinking about it. "Principia Mathematica, if I remember correctly, is about the gravity of planets... which are round, but it seems like a stretch."

"What about the zodiac signs?" Sophie asked, pointing to the constellations on the orb. "You mentioned Pisces and Aquarius earlier, right?"

The End of Days, Langdon thought. "The end of Pisces and the start of Aquarius was supposedly when the Priory planned to release the Sangreal documents to the world." But the year 2000 came and went with nothing happening, leaving historians unsure when the truth would come out.

"It's possible," Sophie said, "that the Priory's plans to reveal the truth are related to the last line of the poem."

It speaks of Rosy flesh and seeded womb. Langdon felt a shiver of possibility. He hadn't thought of the line that way before.

"You told me earlier," she said, "that when the Priory planned to reveal the truth about 'the Rose' and her fertile womb was directly linked to the position of planets—round objects." Langdon nodded, feeling the first hints of a solution appearing. Even so, he felt that astronomy wasn't the answer. The Grand Master's previous solutions had all had a clear, symbolic meaning—the Mona Lisa, Madonna of the Rocks, SOFIA. This clear meaning was missing in the idea of planets and the zodiac. So far, Jacques Saunière had been a careful code writer, and Langdon believed that his final password—those five letters that unlocked the Priory's biggest secret—would be both symbolically right and very clear. If this solution was like the others, it would be obvious once he saw it.

"Look!" Sophie gasped, startling him as she grabbed his arm. From the fear in her touch Langdon thought someone was coming, but when he turned to her, she was staring in shock at the top of the black marble sarcophagus. "Someone was here," she whispered, pointing to a spot on the sarcophagus near Newton's outstretched right foot.

Langdon didn't get why she was worried. A thoughtless tourist had left a charcoal rubbing pencil on the sarcophagus lid, near Newton's foot. It's nothing. Langdon reached to grab it, but as he leaned toward the sarcophagus, the light moved on the shiny black marble, and Langdon stopped. Now he saw why Sophie was scared.

Written on the sarcophagus lid, at Newton's feet, was a faint message in charcoal:

I have Teabing.

Go through Chapter House,

out south exit, to public garden.

Langdon read the words again, his heart racing.

Sophie turned and looked around the nave.

Even though he felt a sense of dread seeing the words, Langdon told himself this was good. Leigh is still alive. There was something else here too. "They don't know the password either," he whispered.

Sophie nodded. Why else would they let them know they were here?

"They might want to trade Leigh for the password."

"Or it's a trap."

Langdon shook his head. "I don't think so. The garden is outside the abbey walls. It's open to everyone." Langdon had once visited the abbey's famous College Garden—a small fruit orchard and herb garden—from when monks grew natural medicines. With the oldest fruit trees in Great Britain, College Garden was a popular place for tourists who didn't need to enter the abbey. "I think sending us outside shows they trust us. So we feel safe."

Sophie looked unsure. "You mean outside, where there are no metal detectors?" Langdon frowned. She had a point.

Looking back at the tomb with the orbs, Langdon wished he knew the cryptex password... something to bargain with. I got Leigh into this, and I'll do anything I can to help him.

"The note says to go through the Chapter House to the south exit," Sophie said. "Maybe from the exit we can see the garden? That way we can check things out before we go out there and risk getting hurt?"

That was a good idea. Langdon remembered the Chapter House as a huge eight-sided hall where the first British Parliament met before the modern Parliament building existed. It had been years since he'd been there, but he remembered it being through the cloister. Stepping back from the tomb, Langdon looked around the choir screen to his right, across the nave to the side opposite where they had come down. A wide arched passageway was nearby, with a large sign.

THIS WAY TO:

CLOISTERS

DEANERY

COLLEGE HALL

PYX CHAMBER

ST. FAITH'S CHAPEL
